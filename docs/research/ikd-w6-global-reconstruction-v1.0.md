# IKD-W6 Expanded Alternative-Basis Reconstruction

## Status

Complete for the registered expanded reconstruction universe. This is a bounded internal result, not a proof over every possible formal vocabulary.

## Question

Can the preservation achieved by RCCD-001 be reconstructed from a materially different and strictly cheaper basis without reintroducing recoverable commitments, constrained evolution, dependency-preserving revision, queryable historical identity, and a uniform bounded-query interface?

## Method

The search widened beyond the original candidate architectures. It tested relational, transition-only, provenance-only, causal, proof-theoretic, dependent-type, process-algebraic, coalgebraic, information-flow, algebraic-rewriting, categorical, dynamical, event-sourced, and cost-guided synthesized bases.

A candidate counted as a replacement only when it preserved semantic and operational commitments, dependencies, revision consequences, historical identity, and the registered bounded queries while strictly improving total charged cost. Encoders, decoders, semantic bridges, search procedures, schedulers, canonicalizers, history stores, proof objects, and certificates were charged. Moving RCCD commitments into a decoder or helper did not count as elimination.

## Results

Pure relational incidence, bare transition structure, provenance alone, and information-flow primitives failed preservation. The causal, proof, type, process, coalgebraic, algebraic, categorical, dynamical, and event-sourced reconstructions succeeded only by reintroducing equivalents of the RCCD commitments. Cost-guided synthesis compressed surface syntax but transferred the missing structure into decoder tables, canonicalizers, query contracts, and preservation certificates, so total charged cost did not strictly improve.

Five countermodel families survived the search: dependency-sensitive revisions with equal behavior, history-sensitive systems with equal final states, semantic-version distinctions with equal current transition extensions, observationally equivalent hidden processes with different commitments, and hidden schedulers that alter accepted revision order.

## Result

`no_strictly_cheaper_non_equivalent_reconstruction_found_in_expanded_registered_search`

This strengthens the case that RCCD is not merely an artifact of the original candidate vocabulary or representation family. It does not prove that no unknown basis can replace it. The next required step is to turn the surviving countermodels and failed ablations into explicit lower-bound arguments.

## Claim boundary

This work does not establish global necessity, global minimality, uniqueness, universal instantiation, actual-process correspondence, or external validation.
