# IKD-W2 Expanded Candidate Competition v1.0

## Status

Complete bounded comparison.

## Inputs and controls

The competition consumes the candidate universe frozen by `IKD-W1-CANDIDATE-FREEZE-001`. Admission occurred before scoring. All candidates face the same bounded source scope, six preservation dimensions, failure rules, and componentwise machinery ledger. Candidate-specific repair, unrestricted interpreters, hidden provenance, future access, finite truncation represented as exact coverage, and scalar winner selection are prohibited.

The scope is `S_core` plus the six separately represented effective USD-W1 subclasses. Actual-process correspondence and unexecuted cross-feature conjunctions remain outside this comparison.

## Results

Three architectures satisfy the complete bounded preservation contract:

- `FARA-001`;
- `LTS-PROV-001`;
- `COALG-DYN-001`.

They remain pairwise incomparable under the frozen Pareto rule. FARA retains lower derived machinery and semantic-description cost. LTS-PROV retains a lower supplied-primitive and operation profile. COALG-DYN matches the lower operation count while preserving behavior coinductively, but requires more derived machinery than FARA and a different primitive basis than LTS-PROV.

The other five new candidates are scope-limited rather than silently repaired:

- `PROC-CAT-001` requires explicit provenance and observation machinery for revision identity, rejected alternatives, and finite-query access.
- `CAUSAL-SCM-001` preserves intervention-sensitive dependency but requires external semantic-version and revision-history ledgers.
- `PROOF-TYPE-001` preserves typed derivational witnesses but imports numeric, stochastic, and epistemic interfaces for the full scope.
- `INFO-FLOW-001` natively preserves access and update constraints but lacks native semantic supersession and commitment history.
- `ALG-REWRITE-001` preserves equational and rewrite behavior but requires added observation, stochastic-query, and revision-provenance machinery.

All added machinery is charged. None of these partial candidates is promoted to success.

## Decision

The terminal classification is `three_incomparable_successful_architectures_in_frozen_bounded_scope`.

This supersedes the prior two-candidate frontier but does not establish an exhaustive candidate universe, global minimality, a universal structure, or a no-go theorem. The result strengthens the need for `IKD-W3-COMMON-FACTOR`: the next task is to test whether FARA, LTS-PROV, and COALG-DYN share a nontrivial reasoning-relevant factor rather than merely generic state, relation, or transition structure.

## Nonclaims

No claim is made about cross-feature compositional closure, actual-process correspondence, external validation, or global candidate exhaustiveness.
