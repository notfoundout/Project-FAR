# TUE-W1 Evidential Unknown Boundary v1.0

## Question

Can the Unknown cases preserved by SC-W6 be made assessable without redefining success after seeing the evidence?

## Frozen measurement rule

The workstream distinguishes process access from representational fit. A case is assessable only when the relevant candidate commitments, permitted changes, grounds or defeaters, history, and recovery queries can be tested through a predeclared measurement procedure. Missing evidence remains Unknown. Retrospective explanation is not automatically treated as the process ground that produced a decision.

All prompting, logging, elicitation, replay, similarity, stochastic proposal, decoding, and interpretation machinery is charged.

## Creative generation

For instrumented creative hypothesis generation, the required evidence consists of a time-ordered candidate log, rejection reasons, constraint perturbations, selective ground removal, and branch-preserving replay. These measurements expose candidate commitments, generation and acceptance constraints, support and conflict relations, and search lineage.

The instrumented case is assessable and admits an RCCD realization without a sixth primitive. Proposal operators, similarity functions, randomness, and evaluators remain domain machinery rather than new universal commitment types.

This resolves the registered creative-generation Unknown only for instrumented cases. It does not claim that every creative process is instrumentable.

## Tacit moral salience

Structured contrast cases, salience elicitation, selective reason defeat, order reversal, and delayed retesting make articulated parts of moral judgment assessable. Those parts map to commitments, normative constraints, reasons and defeaters, judgment history, and uniform recovery.

A residual boundary remains where a subject reports salience that cannot be reliably articulated, manipulated, or recovered. That remainder is not counted as an RCCD success or failure. No sixth primitive is justified by unavailable evidence.

## Inaccessible processes

For systems whose relevant grounds, admissibility conditions, dependencies, or path identities remain inaccessible, input-output agreement cannot establish full reasoning-faithfulness. Such systems remain outside the adjudicable preservation scope until access improves.

Inaccessibility is therefore an epistemic boundary, not a counterexample and not positive support.

## Result

`instrumentation_resolves_creative_generation_within_rccd_while_tacit_and_inaccessible_cases_define_a_principled_evidential_boundary_without_kernel_extension`

The workstream maps to the registered terminal outcome `principled_inaccessibility_boundary`. It found no genuine sixth primitive and no faithful RCCD counterexample.
