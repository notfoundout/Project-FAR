# EVC-W3 R4 adversarial team guide v1.0

## Mandate

The team is not being asked to confirm the Project FAR result. It is being asked to defeat, narrow, or expose the dependence of the bounded incomparable-kernel result on its frozen scope, preservation contract, equivalence rules, cost accounting, candidate construction, invariance transformations, ablation logic, minimality search, counterexample handling, independence labels, and claim boundaries.

The registered result is bounded. A valid attack may refute it, narrow it, or leave it intact with qualifications. Failure to find an attack does not establish universality.

## Independence

The team must be a separate human or organization with no substantive role in constructing the challenged artifacts. The same author, the same agent-controlled workflow, or a nominally separate implementation under common control is not R4 independence.

Disclose prior exposure, collaborations, financial or institutional conflicts, and any author communication. Authors may answer factual package questions, but may not steer the team toward a preferred verdict.

## Release discipline

Record the immutable release commit or signed archive digest and first-access time. Freeze the initial report before receiving an author rebuttal. Any substantive package repair after access creates a new version; the original report and package remain preserved.

## Required work

Complete every challenge in `EVC-W3-R4-CHALLENGE-CORPUS-001`. Each challenge requires:

1. an attempted countermodel, counterexample, alternative derivation, or impossibility argument;
2. exact evidence references;
3. a disposition of `sustained`, `rejected`, or `unresolved`;
4. a reason that does not rely only on author assertion.

A novel candidate sketch under R4-C11 is a stress test, not a full EVC-W4 candidate evaluation. Record what it could change and what additional evaluation would be required.

## Finding classification

Use `blocking` when a load-bearing bounded conclusion is defeated. Use `scope_limiting` when a valid result narrows the admitted class, preservation guarantee, invariance class, necessity result, or minimal frontier. Use nonblocking categories for defects or qualifications that do not defeat the registered terminal classification.

Do not convert a scope limitation into a total refutation without showing that the terminal bounded result cannot survive the narrowed scope.

## Verdict

- `survived`: all mandatory attacks completed; no blocking or scope-limiting attack succeeds.
- `scope_limited`: at least one valid attack narrows the registered bounded result.
- `refuted`: a load-bearing conclusion is defeated.
- `unresolved`: independence, package completeness, attack coverage, or evidence is insufficient.

The weakest completed attack domain controls the maximum verdict strength.

## Required output

Submit the completed machine-readable result template and a prose report. Preserve unsuccessful attacks, ambiguities, deviations, author communications, the initial verdict, any author response, and any later amendment.

Do not claim universal structure, candidate exhaustiveness, actual-process identity, or external validation beyond the evidence actually produced.
