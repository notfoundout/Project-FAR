# POST-USD-IKD-001 v1.0 — Internal Universal-Kernel Discovery Continuation

## Status

Registered prospectively and unexecuted.

## Reason for continuation

The completed post-W5 USD program produced a bounded result: FARA and LTS-PROV are successful, non-equivalent, locally load-bearing, representation-invariant minima inside the frozen four-candidate universe. That result does not answer whether they instantiate a deeper common structure, whether materially different architectures alter the frontier, or whether separate featurewise representation results compose.

The next active program therefore remains internal discovery. The frozen external proof-review, R3 technical-replication, and R4 adversarial packages are preserved unchanged but are not released or executed before terminal internal adjudication.

This deferral does not count as external validation, rejection of external validation, or evidence that external validation is unnecessary.

## Controlling question

Do FARA, LTS-PROV, and materially different successful architectures instantiate a nontrivial reasoning-relevant commitment that survives expanded candidate search, cross-feature composition, representation change, alternative reconstruction, and complete cost accounting?

## Discovery sequence

1. Freeze materially new candidate architectures.
2. Execute expanded candidate competition.
3. Search for a candidate-neutral common factor beneath all successful minima.
4. Execute cross-feature composition tests.
5. Expand representation-invariance transformations.
6. Expand alternative-basis reconstruction.
7. Establish declared-class lower bounds or preserve countermodels.
8. Recompute the minimal frontier.
9. Perform terminal internal adjudication.

The sequence is dependency-driven. Candidate architecture is not imposed in advance. Each later workstream must consume the accepted outputs and unresolved boundaries of the preceding work.

## Materially new candidate requirement

The next execution unit must admit candidates from independently motivated architecture families, including process or category theory, causal models, proof or type theory, information flow, dynamical or coalgebraic systems, and algebraic approaches.

A candidate is not materially new merely because it uses different names, serializations, object layouts, helper classes, or implementation languages. It fails admission when its operative commitments are a direct compilation or hidden reconstruction of FARA or LTS-PROV without independent primitives and constraints.

## Common-factor standard

A common factor must do more than intersect labels or retain generic graph structure. It must:

- preserve the registered reasoning commitments;
- survive the expanded admissible representation class;
- discriminate against the registered contrast class or discharge another frozen reasoning-relevant role;
- survive alternative reconstruction without uncounted equivalent reintroduction;
- retain a complete machinery and cost ledger.

An unrestricted interpreter, source-specific decoder, arbitrary metadata layer, or post-hoc scope restriction is a failed common-factor construction.

## Cross-feature composition

The six positive USD-W1 results concern separately frozen subclasses. The continuation does not infer combined coverage from separate success. It must execute conjunctions directly, including combinations of partial observability, countably infinite carriers, continuous dynamics, open-ended history, semantic change, and infinite-support stochasticity.

Actual-process correspondence remains outside purely formal composition and continues to require an empirical bridge.

## Terminal outcomes

The internal program may terminate as:

- one nontrivial common kernel;
- multiple translation-equivalent kernels;
- multiple incomparable kernels with no deeper factor found within the declared search;
- generic structured-system properties only;
- a bounded no-single-kernel result;
- unresolved.

Negative, equivalence, incomparability, and unresolved outcomes remain valid. Failure to discover a deeper kernel is not a global nonexistence proof unless a valid closure or lower-bound argument covers the declared search class.

Release requires the terminal IKD adjudication artifact to freeze the exact claim, proof scope, assumptions, counterexamples, and unresolved boundaries that an external party would evaluate.
