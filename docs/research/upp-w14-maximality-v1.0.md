# UPP-W14 Relative Maximality

## Theorem boundary

This workstream proves the strongest supportable maximality result under the frozen extension rules of `POST-TUE-UPP-001`. It does not infer unrestricted universality from an absence of counterexample.

The target statement is: every registered extension challenge that satisfies the independently frozen target-class contract either embeds into the RCCD construction while preserving all eight registered dimensions, or supplies a concrete counterexample that refutes relative maximality. Challenges outside the target class are recorded as boundary cases rather than forced into RCCD.

## Search closure

The frozen challenge ledger covers distributed realization, probabilistic transition policies, learned semantic decoding, branching revision histories, and an opaque output-only device. Each case is evaluated for class membership, machinery closure, effective embedding, stable identity, total registered queries, and preservation of structure, semantics, operation, dependency, information, history, query totality, and failure/Unknown separation.

Supporting reduction evidence enumerates all 31 proper subsets of the five RCCD components. This evidence supports nontriviality and inherited irreducibility; it does not rename PR #295 or convert finite search into global minimality.

## Adjudication

A registered in-class nonembedding is a refutation. An unresolved membership, equivalence, machinery, or preservation fact yields `Unknown`. A challenge excluded by the frozen class contract is not a counterexample. Hidden decoders, external semantic repair, unregistered oracles, circular reconstruction, and costs shifted into omitted machinery are rejected.

The result is therefore relative to frozen extension rules and the registered challenge space. The open-world boundary remains explicit: new admissible challenges can reopen the result, and absence of counterexample outside the registry is not proof.
