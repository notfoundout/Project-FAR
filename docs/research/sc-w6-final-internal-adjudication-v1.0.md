# SC-W6 Final Internal Adjudication v1.0

## Final answer

Project FAR now has its strongest defensible internal answer.

RCCD is the necessary nontrivial common kernel for the naturalized class of normatively assessable reasoning systems, relative to the independently justified full reasoning-faithfulness contract and the broadened family of uniform effective representations.

This is the terminal internal outcome:

`rccd_necessary_kernel_for_natural_class`

## Final bounded theorem

For every system in the naturalized reasoning class C*, every representation satisfying P* within E* must preserve distinctions equivalent to:

1. recoverable commitments;
2. constrained evolution;
3. dependency-sensitive revision;
4. historical identity;
5. uniform bounded recovery.

No resolved registered case requires a sixth universal primitive. No faithful effective representation avoided these commitments after hidden machinery was charged. Different successful formalisms remain representationally and computationally plural.

## Why this is stronger than the W9 result

The W9 theorem was bounded by a registered C/P/E scope. The post-W9 challenge attacked whether that scope encoded RCCD into necessity.

The challenge found:

- class membership can be stated without RCCD vocabulary;
- weaker contracts yield weaker kernels, showing RCCD was not mechanically preserved under every contract;
- the full contract is independently motivated by ordinary reasoning facts;
- broad changes of formalism do not provide a faithful effective escape;
- held-out contexts produced no primitive extension or faithful counterexample;
- opaque creative-generation and tacit-salience cases remain Unknown rather than being counted as support.

## Exact scope

C* includes systems that perform normatively assessable selection, inference, revision, planning, comparison, or justification over distinguishable alternatives, provided that the relevant reasoning facts are accessible enough for preservation to be tested.

P* requires preservation of current content, permitted and forbidden change, grounds and defeaters, supersession and path-sensitive identity, and uniform effective recovery.

E* includes the materially different effective representation families tested by the program, with semantic maps, codebooks, decoders, schedulers, histories, closure procedures, proof objects, normalization, aggregation, and adapters charged rather than treated as free.

## What is universal and what is not

The universal claim concerns commitment structure. It does not identify one universal notation, ontology, calculus, program, neural architecture, physical substrate, or implementation.

RCCD-equivalent realizations may remain Pareto-incomparable. Kernel unity is compatible with realization plurality.

## Defeating conditions

The conclusion must be reopened if any of the following is established:

- an assessable in-scope case needs a genuine sixth irreducible primitive;
- a uniform effective representation satisfies P* without RCCD-equivalent commitments;
- C* or P* is shown to be circularly defined by R1-R5;
- two faithful kernels cannot reconstruct one another without hidden primitive machinery;
- an RCCD component can be removed under P* without loss.

## Unknown boundary

Uninstrumented creative hypothesis generation and tacit moral salience remain unresolved. They do not defeat the theorem because no assessable faithful counterexample has been established, but they prevent unrestricted claims over inaccessible processes.

## Closure

The post-W9 internal scope-challenge program is complete. Additional supportive mappings do not strengthen this internal conclusion unless they target a defeating condition or resolve a registered Unknown.

External review is not part of this adjudication and is not required to state the internal answer. It remains a later question of independent confidence, not unfinished internal discovery.
