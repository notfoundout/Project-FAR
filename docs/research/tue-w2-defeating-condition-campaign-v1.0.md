# TUE-W2 Defeating-Condition Campaign v1.0

## Question

Can any of the five defeating conditions registered by SC-W6 be established under a fixed adversarial campaign?

## Method

The campaign attacked all five conditions. A proposed escape was not credited merely for changing notation. Every decoder, scheduler, codebook, history, proof object, closure rule, oracle, adapter, and interpretation map needed for faithful recovery was charged as machinery. Unknown was neither Pass nor Fail. One genuine defeat would override all supportive evidence.

## Results

### DC-1: sixth primitive

Attention, creative novelty, social authority, continuous confidence, and salience were tested as possible additions. Instrumented attention and creativity decomposed into commitment content, admissibility constraints, dependencies, history, and recovery. Social authority was domain-specific rather than universal. Residual tacit salience remained Unknown because the required internal distinction was not accessible enough to establish either RCCD fit or irreducibility.

**Disposition:** not established.

### DC-2: faithful RCCD escape

Pure policy oracles, predictive-state quotients, behavior-only equivalence, extensional transition tables, and stateless proof transformers were tested. The behavior-only forms failed P* by losing grounds, defeaters, semantic identity, or history. The richer forms became faithful only after adding charged annotations and recovery interfaces equivalent to R1-R5.

**Disposition:** not established.

### DC-3: circularity of C* or P*

Class membership was restated using normatively assessable selection, inference, revision, planning, comparison, and justification over distinguishable alternatives. P* was independently paraphrased as preservation of current claims, allowed change, reasons and counterreasons, temporal provenance, and effective inspection. The contract ladder continued to show that weaker contracts produce weaker kernels rather than RCCD under every definition.

**Disposition:** not established.

### DC-4: non-reconstructible plural kernels

Proof-theoretic, causal-intervention, predictive-control, provenance, social-authority, and salience-centered candidates were compared. Independently faithful proof and causal realizations reconstructed the same commitment kernel. Predictive-control candidates remained unfaithful until dependency and history machinery was added. Domain-specific authority structure did not create a second universal kernel.

**Disposition:** not established.

### DC-5: removal of R1-R5

Each component was ablated. Removing R1 lost content; R2 lost permitted-change distinctions; R3 lost selective ground-defeat behavior; R4 collapsed path-sensitive identity; R5 required a case-specific oracle, which was charged as reintroduction of the recovery interface.

**Disposition:** not established.

## Terminal outcome

`no_defeating_condition_established`

This means the SC-W6 result survives the registered campaign. It does not mean every conceivable attack has been exhausted. Tacit salience remains a principled Unknown, external adversarial independence has not occurred, and actual-process correspondence remains outside scope.
