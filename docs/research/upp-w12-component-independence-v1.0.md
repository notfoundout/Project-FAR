# UPP-W12: Relative independence of the five RCCD components

## Theorem boundary

This workstream asks whether the five components already shown necessary are merely five names for fewer underlying obligations. The result is relative to the frozen UPP framework. It is not a claim that the components are metaphysically separate substances or that no future vocabulary could reorganize them.

Let the registered component package be:

1. recoverable commitment;
2. constrained evolution;
3. dependency structure;
4. semantic interpretation;
5. historical trace.

Relative independence holds when each component has at least one preservation obligation that can be removed while the obligations of the other four remain available in an admissible, machinery-closed representation.

## Separating witnesses

### Recoverable commitment

A system may preserve typed dependency edges, semantic roles, admissibility constraints, and a complete revision history while exposing no stable answer to the question, “what is currently committed?” This loses commitment identity and query totality without erasing the other four structures.

### Constrained evolution

A system may expose commitments, their meanings, their dependencies, and their complete histories while treating every proposed commitment change as permitted. The absence of a nontrivial permitted/forbidden distinction removes constrained evolution while leaving the other obligations intact.

### Dependency structure

A system may contain meaningful commitments, admissibility rules, and complete revision histories but retain only an unstructured set of reasons. Without typed, directed, operationally relevant support or defeat edges, dependency structure is lost.

### Semantic interpretation

A system may preserve opaque commitment identities, their admissible changes, dependency edges, and event histories while lacking recoverable content, role, or truth/satisfaction conditions. Syntax and identity survive; interpretation does not.

### Historical trace

A system may expose the current commitments, meanings, dependency graph, and transition policy while retaining only a final snapshot. Without stable event identities, predecessor links, or supersession lineage, historical trace is lost.

## Anti-reduction rules

A reduction is not established by shared storage, correlated outputs, shared code, or vocabulary replacement. A valid reduction must be effective, total on the frozen registered queries, invariant under commitment-equivalent representations, and fully machinery-accounted. It must recover every distinctive obligation of the target component without invoking that component under another name.

One-way simulation is insufficient. Post hoc human explanation is insufficient. A hidden decoder or oracle invalidates the reduction. Unresolved evidence yields `unknown`, not proof of either independence or reducibility.

## Interaction without collapse

The components are not isolated. Interpretation applies to commitments; dependency edges connect interpreted identities; admissibility constrains transitions; histories record those transitions. Independence means that these cross-links do not erase the separately testable obligations of each component.

## Result

Five separating witnesses, pairwise anti-aliasing controls, a joint anti-reduction condition, circularity rejection, machinery accounting, and all 120 component-name permutations establish relative component independence within the frozen framework.

This result leaves sufficiency, global irreducibility, target-class maximality, and the terminal universal theorem unresolved.
