# Universal Theorem Target

Let:

- `S` range over systems in the independently defined target class `C*`;
- `R` range over admissible effective representations in `E*`;
- `P*(S,R)` mean that `R` satisfies the full independently defined reasoning-faithfulness contract for `S`;
- `M(R,S)` denote the complete charged machinery closure required to interpret and use `R` faithfully for `S`;
- `RCCD_equiv(M(R,S))` mean that the closed representation contains, up to the registered commitment-equivalence relation, recoverable commitments, constrained admissible evolution, dependency-sensitive revision, historical identity, and uniform effective recovery.

The principal necessity target is:

```text
forall S in C*, forall R in E*,
  P*(S,R) -> RCCD_equiv(M(R,S)).
```

The full terminal result additionally requires:

```text
RCCD_structure(S) -> exists R in E*, P*(S,R)
```

plus registered proofs of nontriviality, component independence, component irreducibility, representation invariance, and relative maximality.

## Prohibited interpretation

The theorem may not be reported as unrestricted metaphysical universality unless its quantified domain actually contains every entity covered by that phrase and the proof discharges that domain without inaccessible or non-effective exceptions.

## Permitted terminal weakening

If the full target cannot be proved, the terminal result must state the strongest exact theorem that can be proved and identify every removed quantifier, restricted class, added assumption, weakened equivalence, or abandoned RCCD property.
