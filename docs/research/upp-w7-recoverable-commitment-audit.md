# UPP-W7 Recoverable Commitment Necessity Audit

## Scope

This workstream proves only the first registered component lemma. It asks whether full faithfulness over the frozen target class and admissible machinery-closed representations requires an effectively recoverable domain of reasoning commitments.

## Theorem boundary

The antecedent is conjunctive: membership in `C*`, admissibility in `E*`, complete machinery closure, satisfaction of `P*`, and respect for the frozen commitment-equivalence relation. None may be inferred from the conclusion.

The conclusion is operational rather than metaphysical. A recoverable commitment is an effectively identifiable referent for registered reasoning queries whose identity survives permitted representation changes. The theorem does not assert that commitments are fundamental mental objects or that every physical process contains them.

## Witness construction

1. Use the faithful registered query interface to identify commitment-denoting answers.
2. Charge the extraction procedure and all supporting decoders to the machinery-closed package.
3. Quotient identifiers only by bidirectional commitment equivalence.
4. Preserve distinct failure and `Unknown` outcomes.
5. Require totality on the registered query domain, not on arbitrary unregistered questions.

## Contradiction argument

Assume the full antecedent and deny the witness. The denial must produce at least one of: no effective commitment domain, an unanswered registered query, unstable identity across an allowed representation change, or collapse of failure into `Unknown`. Each contradicts a frozen clause of admissibility, closure, equivalence, or full faithfulness. Therefore no registered countermodel preserves the antecedent while denying the conclusion.

## Countermodel campaign

The suite covers final-output equivalence with incompatible commitments, concealed decoders, non-effective selectors, partial interfaces, unstable renaming, empty domains, and error/Unknown collapse. These cases are not discarded; they are classified as antecedent failures or unresolved premises.

## Three-valued adjudication

A proved verdict requires every premise and witness obligation. Any demonstrated failure yields `refuted`. Missing evidence yields `unknown`; it is never silently upgraded to proof or downgraded to failure.

## Result

`recoverable_commitment_necessity_lemma_established_relative_to_frozen_class_contract_representation_closure_and_equivalence`

R2–R5, sufficiency, irreducibility, maximality, and the terminal universal theorem remain unresolved. Public evaluation remains unauthorized.
