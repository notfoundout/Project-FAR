# SC-W5 Held-Out Cross-Context Tests v1.0

## Question

Do reasoning contexts not used to construct the W7-W9 kernel result require a genuine primitive beyond RCCD R1-R5, or produce a faithful counterexample to RCCD under the full reasoning-faithfulness contract?

## Controls

The twelve contexts were held out from the architecture-discovery witnesses used in IKD-W1 through IKD-W9. Their source distinctions and recovery questions were stated before mapping them to RCCD. No context was discarded for poor fit, redundancy, or an unresolved result. This remains an internal held-out test, not independent replication.

A genuine extension requires a reasoning distinction that cannot be represented as commitment content, constrained evolution, dependency-sensitive revision, historical identity, uniform effective recovery, a derived relation, a domain parameter, or charged adapter machinery.

## Contexts

The test covers causal intervention planning, mathematical proof repair, clinical differential diagnosis, cybersecurity incident response, disrupted supply-chain replanning, translation ambiguity resolution, robotic affordance reasoning, institutional policy exceptions, distributed partial-observation coordination, creative hypothesis generation, scientific instrument diagnosis, and counterfactual moral deliberation.

Ten contexts admit faithful RCCD realizations without a sixth kernel primitive. Causal direction, proof modality, probability, risk, adversarial agency, resource flow, similarity, continuous geometry, authority hierarchy, common knowledge, measurement models, and normative salience are represented as domain content, annotations, relations, constraints, weights, or derived procedures rather than new universal commitment types.

Two contexts remain evidentially unresolved: uninstrumented creative generation and tacit moral salience. Their visible artifacts can be represented, but inaccessible generation grounds or unarticulated reasons prevent a full preservation judgment. They are not counted as successes or counterexamples.

## Result

No held-out context produced a faithful RCCD failure or a genuine sixth primitive. Ten passed without extension; two remain Unknown at the evidential boundary.

`held_out_contexts_add_no_genuine_kernel_primitive_and_produce_no_faithful_rccd_counterexample_with_two_evidential_unknowns`

## Limits

This does not sample every possible reasoning context, establish actual-process correspondence, or provide external replication. It authorizes SC-W6 final internal adjudication only.
