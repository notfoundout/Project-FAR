# TUE-W3 deeper-kernel adjudication

## Question

Can RCCD's five components be derived from a strictly smaller, non-vacuous substrate without hiding them in bridges, decoders, schedulers, histories, codebooks, proof objects, closure procedures, or query interfaces?

## Method

The workstream froze six success conditions and six failure conditions before adjudication. Ten candidate substrates were tested against all five registered paired distinctions and the full P* preservation contract. Every required derivation mechanism was charged.

## Result

No candidate satisfied the strict-reduction standard. Three candidates compressed RCCD linguistically or mathematically, but expanded back into R1-R5 when their derivation and recovery obligations were made explicit. Six candidates failed at least one preservation obligation. The universal-interpreter proposal was vacuous because the interpreter and codebook contained the omitted structure.

The strongest summary principle was **effective preservation of revision-relevant distinctions**. It is useful explanatory language, but it is not a strictly deeper kernel: identifying and preserving those distinctions requires recoverable commitments, admissibility constraints, dependency-sensitive revision, historical identity, and uniform effective recovery.

## Terminal outcome

`rccd_irreducible_at_registered_level`

Within C*, P*, the broadened uniform effective representation family, and the frozen machinery-cost rules, RCCD remains the lowest internally justified kernel level.

## Boundary

This is registered-level irreducibility, not proof of metaphysical fundamentality beneath every conceivable ontology. The search is broad but not logically exhaustive. Principled inaccessibility remains neither support nor defeat. Actual-process correspondence and external validation remain unexecuted.
