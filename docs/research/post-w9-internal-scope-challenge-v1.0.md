# Post-W9 Internal Scope Challenge

## Purpose

W9 established a bounded internal theorem: RCCD is necessary for the registered class C, preservation contract P, and effective representation family E. This program tests whether that scope is independently justified as a natural target for reasoning, or whether the theorem is partly construct-loaded by its definitions.

## Controlling question

Can a faithful representation of a naturally justified reasoning class avoid RCCD-equivalent commitments without losing independently specified reasoning facts?

## Program order

1. Audit the neutrality and naturalness of C, P, and E.
2. Adjudicate boundary systems that plausibly count as reasoning but fall outside C, and systems inside C that may not count as reasoning.
3. Execute weaker and alternative preservation contracts and recompute the kernel frontier.
4. Search broader effective representation families for faithful RCCD escape.
5. Run frozen held-out contexts and treat primitive extensions as adverse evidence.
6. Issue the strongest final internal answer.

## Decision discipline

The program may strengthen, narrow, pluralize, weaken, or suspend the W9 answer. It may not preserve RCCD by changing class membership, preservation obligations, adapter accounting, or success criteria after seeing results.

The current W9 theorem remains valid for its exact registered scope unless refuted. The question here is whether that scope deserves to be treated as the correct internal target for Project FAR's universal-structure question.

## External hold

External proof review, technical replication, and adversarial replication remain deferred until the final internal scope adjudication. Internal CI is not external validation.
