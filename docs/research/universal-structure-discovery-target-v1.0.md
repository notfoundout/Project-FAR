# THM-US-TARGET-001 v1.0 — Universal-Structure Discovery Target

## Status

Frozen prospective theorem and discovery target. No theorem in this family is proved.

## Central question

Does an independently defined class of reasoning processes instantiate a nontrivial structure that is invariant under admissible representation changes, survives alternative reconstruction, is not merely a generic property of arbitrary structured systems, and is minimal or characterized up to a declared equivalence?

## Unknown object

The project denotes the unknown candidate kernel by:

\[
K^*
\]

`K*` is not identified in advance with FARA, FARO, PB-001, the FARA candidate primitives, or any current vocabulary.

## Governing inputs

The target depends on:

- `RCS-001`, the reasoning and contrast scope;
- `GREL-001`, the generic typed relational baseline;
- the representation–discovery separation standard;
- a candidate-invariant registry;
- future frozen admissible-representation, reconstruction, equivalence, and cost relations.

## Candidate success conditions

A candidate `K` advances only when the registered evidence or proof establishes the applicable conditions:

1. **Coverage:** every admitted reasoning object in the frozen scope instantiates `K`.
2. **Representation invariance:** admissible changes of vocabulary or encoding preserve the relevant structure.
3. **Nondefinitionality:** the result is not forced solely by how the source class or representation language was defined.
4. **Discrimination:** the candidate has a reasoning-relevant role not discharged by arbitrary structured contrast systems.
5. **Ablation resistance:** removing `K` destroys a frozen reasoning commitment.
6. **Reconstruction resistance:** no admissible alternative basis recovers the same commitments without `K` or an explicitly equivalent replacement.
7. **Economy:** every helper and equivalent reintroduction is counted.
8. **Minimality or equivalence classification:** the candidate is minimal, one of several minima, equivalent to alternatives, incomparable, or nonminimal under a frozen universe and preorder.

No single condition implies the others.

## Relationship to THM-TARGET-001

`THM-TARGET-001` remains the finite representation theorem family.

A proof of `THM-CORE-COMMON-001` or `THM-CORE-REP-001` may establish a common FARA schema or faithful representation for `S_core`. It does not prove any theorem in `THM-US-TARGET-001`.

A universal-structure theorem must cite its own scope, invariance, contrast, reconstruction, and minimality obligations.

## Valid outcomes

The program may conclude:

- one universal kernel;
- one kernel for a proper subclass;
- several translation-equivalent kernels;
- several incomparable kernels;
- only generic structured-system properties;
- no single nontrivial universal kernel;
- unresolved.

Every outcome is admissible.

## Failure conditions

The target must be weakened, refuted, or revised if:

- positive-scope membership depends on the candidate conclusion;
- contrast membership is selected to guarantee discrimination;
- the candidate appears only because of one representation vocabulary;
- generic relational encoding explains the entire result;
- a valid reconstruction eliminates the candidate;
- hidden machinery or equivalent reintroduction is not counted;
- a simpler equivalent candidate exists under the frozen preorder;
- the scope changes after exposure without versioning.

## Nonclaims

This artifact does not establish that:

- a universal structure exists;
- FARA is the universal structure;
- the current candidate primitive set is necessary;
- `S_core` captures all reasoning;
- a generic relational baseline is sufficient;
- any candidate is minimal or unique.
