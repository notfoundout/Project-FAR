# POST-W5-USD-SYN-001 — Terminal Program Synthesis

## Question

What follows from the completed `USD-W1` through `USD-W6` execution when every result is aggregated without broadening any claim beyond its frozen scope?

## Method

The synthesis is conjunctive and claim-bounded. A program-level claim is no stronger than its weakest required gate. Unknown is not pass, local necessity is not global necessity, a frozen candidate universe is not exhaustive, and internal multi-implementation robustness is not independent verification.

## Workstream results

`USD-W1` established faithful representation for six effectively presented feature subclasses. Every positive result remained `proper_subclass_only`. Actual-process correspondence terminated as `new_assumption_required` because representation cannot establish identity with an independently existing process without measurements, error models, bridge rules, discriminating interventions, and alternative-process controls.

`USD-W2` found multiple successful vocabularies. FARA and LTS-PROV survived the complete frozen benchmark, while GREL and ARG-HIST did not remain on the successful Pareto frontier.

`USD-W3` showed that the FARA/LTS-PROV result survives all registered admissible representation transformations. This is bounded invariance, not global invariance over every encoding.

`USD-W4` showed that registered commitments in both successful candidates are locally load-bearing or recoverable only by equivalent reintroduction. This supports local necessity in the declared ablation class, not global necessity across every possible basis.

`USD-W5` classified FARA and LTS-PROV as non-equivalent incomparable minima in the frozen candidate universe. No scalar ranking or hidden helper machinery was permitted. This refutes a unique minimum only inside that universe.

`USD-W6` confirmed the bounded outputs across three isolated internal implementation paths and a separate verifier. It explicitly did not establish human or organizational independence.

## Hypothesis disposition

- Existence receives bounded support for more than one candidate kernel.
- Invariance receives bounded support under the registered transformation class.
- Reasoning discrimination receives bounded support for FARA against GREL and ARG-HIST, but not uniqueness against LTS-PROV.
- Necessity receives local support only.
- Minimality is resolved as multiple incomparable minima in the frozen universe.
- The global no-single-kernel hypothesis remains unresolved.

## Release-gate result

The universal-structure release gate does not pass. Coverage remains partial, invariance is bounded, necessity is local, the candidate universe is non-exhaustive, actual-process correspondence is unresolved, and external review or replication has not occurred.

The correct terminal classification is:

`bounded_incomparable_kernels_external_validation_pending`

This maps to the registered program outcome `incomparable_kernels`, but only for the frozen scope and candidate universe.

## Scientific effect

The program has produced a substantive negative constraint on overclaiming: the current evidence does not select one universal vocabulary. It has also produced a positive bounded result: two materially different architectures remain successful, invariant under registered changes, locally load-bearing, and minimal under the frozen preorder.

The next decisive work is not another internal relabeling of the same result. It is external proof review, R3–R5 replication, candidate-universe expansion, and a separately preregistered empirical bridge for actual-process correspondence.
