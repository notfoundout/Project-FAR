# Universal Proof Assumption Register

Every premise used by the universal theorem must be entered into the machine-readable proof dependency model and assigned exactly one primary class.

## Classes

### Definitional

Introduces notation or fixes the meaning of a project term. A definition may not conceal a substantive RCCD obligation by restating it under different vocabulary.

### Logical

A rule of the selected formal logic or a theorem derived within it.

### Computational

An effectiveness, decidability, computability, termination, complexity, or machine-representation premise.

### Normative

A premise about what counts as correct, justified, admissible, revisable, or reason-responsive.

### Empirical

A premise contingent on observations of actual cognitive, computational, social, or physical systems.

### Methodological

A rule governing evaluation, accounting, equivalence, scope, or evidential treatment.

## Controls

- An assumption may have secondary tags, but one primary class is mandatory.
- Normative and empirical assumptions may not be described as established by formal proof alone.
- Methodological assumptions must be tested for conclusion-loading.
- Definitional expansion must be available for circularity audit.
- Computational assumptions must state the exact effectiveness notion.
- No admitted axiom may be omitted from the terminal theorem dependency report.

The detailed assumption ledger will be populated and frozen by UPP-W1 through UPP-W6 before the five RCCD necessity lemmas are attempted.
