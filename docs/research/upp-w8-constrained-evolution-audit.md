# UPP-W8 constrained-evolution necessity audit

## Scope

This workstream adjudicates only the R2 necessity obligation under the frozen `POST-TUE-UPP-001` framework. It assumes C* membership, E* admissibility, machinery closure, full P* faithfulness, commitment-equivalence preservation, and at least one commitment-changing transition.

## Theorem witness

The witness is a time-indexed, history-sensitive admissibility relation over commitment-state transitions. Its domain and verdicts are recoverable through the registered query interface; every decoder, policy evaluator, oracle, rule store, and reason extractor used to answer those queries is charged to the machinery-closed package.

A positive witness requires both a permitted and a forbidden commitment-changing candidate. This prevents an always-true or always-false predicate from satisfying the theorem by stipulation. The witness also preserves explicit Unknown outcomes when admissibility cannot be resolved.

## Contradiction structure

Under the frozen premises, denying any effective admissibility relation entails at least one of the following: transition permissions are not operationally recoverable; historical context is erased; governing dependencies or constraints are missing; registered queries are not total; equivalent representations disagree; hidden machinery remains outside closure; or failure has been collapsed into Unknown. Each alternative violates a previously frozen premise or preservation clause.

## Separating countermodels

The regression suite covers identical outputs with different transition permissions, unconstrained mutation, hidden policy machinery, history erasure, non-effective admissibility, failure/Unknown collapse, representation-dependent verdicts, and contradictory verdicts for the same historical transition.

## Claim boundary

The terminal result is `constrained_evolution_necessity_lemma_established_relative_to_frozen_class_contract_representation_closure_and_equivalence`.

This is a relative operational necessity theorem. It does not establish R3-R5, sufficiency, irreducibility, maximality, the terminal universal theorem, or unrestricted metaphysical laws of rational change.

Public evaluation remains unauthorized.
