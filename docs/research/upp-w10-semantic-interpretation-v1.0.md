# UPP-W10 Semantic Interpretation Necessity

## Claim

Relative to the frozen target class, faithfulness contract, admissible representation universe, machinery closure, and commitment-equivalence relation, every qualifying representation must expose an effective context-indexed interpretation function for every registered semantically operative item.

The witness maps a represented item and its operative context to a content identity, semantic role, and truth or satisfaction conditions. It must be recoverable, operationally relevant, stable under equivalent representations, and supported entirely by machinery included in the representation closure.

## Why syntax is insufficient

Token identity, serialization shape, graph labels, embeddings, or output equality do not determine interpretation. The same sign may carry different content in different contexts; different signs may express the same commitment; and identical outputs may result from incompatible truth conditions or semantic roles.

## Proof route

Full semantic and information preservation requires registered semantic queries to return preserved answers. Query totality supplies an answer, explicit failure, or Unknown. Machinery closure charges interpreters, context stores, lexicons, decoders, learned parameters, and oracles to the representation. Commitment equivalence requires content and role identity to survive representation changes. These obligations construct the required interpretation witness. Denial of the witness contradicts at least one frozen antecedent.

## Separating cases

The theorem excludes syntax-only encodings, context-free dictionary lookup, hidden external interpreters, post hoc human glosses, constant interpretation functions, output-equivalent systems with different truth conditions, and representations that collapse Unknown into empty content.

## Boundary

This is a relative operational necessity theorem. It does not settle metaphysical realism, reference theory, consciousness, grounding in the unrestricted philosophical sense, or a universal natural-language semantics.
