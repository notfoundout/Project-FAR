# Project FAR Research Priority Reset

## Status

Effective prospectively upon merge.

## Purpose

Project FAR has accumulated substantial repository architecture, certification, mechanization, dashboards, registries, and internally authored validation. Those assets are useful, but they do not answer the central research question. This reset restores a strict priority order in which new work must reduce the principal scientific uncertainties rather than increase internal administrative completeness.

## Controlling Objective

The next major milestone is not another release, dashboard, registry expansion, terminology audit, or broad collection of favorable representations.

The next milestone is a decisive prospective test of whether FAR is nontrivially adequate, locally necessary, and comparatively economical under rules it does not control.

## Current Evidence Position

The project may presently claim:

- a coherent and executable formal framework;
- bounded successful representations;
- deterministic internal implementation paths;
- documented limitations and unresolved cases;
- readiness to conduct stronger comparative research.

The project may not presently claim:

- universal applicability;
- primitive necessity;
- global or architectural minimality;
- superiority over strong alternative vocabularies;
- independent replication;
- genuine external confirmation of the central hypothesis.

## Priority Order

### Priority 1 — External observation contract

Freeze a vocabulary-neutral contract describing the source-system observables that every candidate representation must preserve. It must not use FAR-native success criteria.

Exit condition: independent reviewers agree that the contract describes the source systems rather than FAR's preferred decomposition.

### Priority 2 — Nontriviality and negative controls

Demonstrate that the evaluation rejects output-equivalent but structurally unfaithful encodings, including lookup tables, hidden interpreters, dependency collapse, history erasure, semantic labels without operational commitments, and unrestricted metadata smuggling.

Exit condition: all preregistered negative controls fail for the intended reasons while faithful positive controls pass.

### Priority 3 — Local necessity

Ablate each candidate FAR primitive under structural anti-reintroduction rules. Permit the strongest admissible reconstruction and count all replacement machinery.

Exit condition: every retained primitive has a recorded ablation result. A primitive is not classified as necessary unless removal causes preservation loss or requires an equivalent commitment at equal or greater total cost.

### Priority 4 — Comparative economy

Compare FAR against strong alternatives using full-cost accounting for primitives, derived machinery, operations, semantic description, hidden state, ambiguity policies, implementation assumptions, and adjudication burden.

Exit condition: results are reported as Pareto relations or explicit tradeoffs. No unsupported scalar ranking is introduced after results are known.

### Priority 5 — Independent replication

Obtain implementation or evaluation by people who did not design FAR's theory, cases, mappings, compilers, or verifier. Internal isolated implementations remain valuable but are labeled implementation robustness only.

Exit condition: at least one complete frozen package is executed independently with provenance sufficient to establish the claimed level of independence.

### Priority 6 — Boundary-focused counterexample search

Run a private-holdout and adversarial challenge targeting self-modification, nonmonotonicity, probabilistic revision, distributed reasoning, semantic change, resource bounds, hidden state, circular support, and dynamic representational types.

Exit condition: every case receives an immutable classification, including failures and unresolved outcomes.

## Work Paused by Default

Until Priorities 1–4 have at least one complete prospective pilot, the following work is paused unless it is required to execute or verify that pilot:

- new repository certification layers;
- additional dashboard or status automation;
- broad terminology cleanup;
- new release packaging;
- expansion of favorable case counts;
- generic framework exposition;
- application features unrelated to the active experiment;
- retrospective relabeling of internal evidence as independent or confirmatory.

Paused work is not declared worthless. It is deferred because it does not currently reduce the main uncertainty.

## Admission Test for New Work

Every proposed major task must answer all of the following:

1. Which central claim does it test: existence, bounded sufficiency, universality, necessity, minimality, or nontriviality?
2. What result could count against FAR?
3. Which current uncertainty will be reduced?
4. Why is the task required before the next decisive experiment?
5. Is the work research, enabling engineering, maintenance, or application work?
6. Does it create another FAR-controlled evaluator, metric, or evidence source?
7. Can a smaller task produce the same evidential gain?

A task that cannot answer these questions does not enter the active research queue.

## Active Experiment Sequence

The active sequence is:

1. create and independently review the external observation contract;
2. register positive and negative controls;
3. freeze the full-cost model and anti-reintroduction criteria;
4. assemble a confirmatory package using `docs/methodology/confirmatory-research-package.md`;
5. perform an internal pilot that is explicitly nonconfirmatory;
6. correct only predeclared implementation defects, preserving all failed versions;
7. freeze the final package;
8. execute independent and adversarial replications;
9. publish the complete result distribution and nonclaims;
10. decide whether the evidence supports continuation, theory revision, scope restriction, primitive removal, or rejection.

## Stop Conditions

The project must stop strengthening a claim when any of the following occurs:

- the observation contract cannot be stated without embedding FAR's ontology;
- negative controls pass as valid representations;
- an ablated vocabulary matches FAR without greater total commitment;
- a competitor Pareto-dominates FAR;
- the result depends materially on project-authored ambiguity repair;
- the theory must be changed after exposure to a frozen case;
- independent implementers cannot reproduce the result from the specification;
- the justified scope becomes materially narrower than the published claim.

A stop condition triggers classification and review, not deletion or reinterpretation of the result.

## Success Standard

Getting the project back on track does not mean making FAR win. It means producing a research process capable of determining that FAR is universal, bounded, nonminimal, one useful vocabulary among several, or false as a universal account.
