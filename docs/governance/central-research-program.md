# Central Research Program

## Purpose

Project FAR exists to determine whether reasoning processes share a universal and minimal underlying structure. The project does not assume that such a structure exists, that FARA is that structure, or that representational adequacy alone establishes reasoning-specific necessity.

## Program architecture

The repository separates three evidence tracks:

- **REP — Representation capacity and fidelity.** Bounded construction and obstruction results over frozen source and target classes.
- **ADJ — Adjudication and specificity.** Generic-baseline comparison, specificity, ablation, reconstruction, and machinery/cost accounting.
- **USD/UPP — Universal-structure discovery.** Architecture-neutral discovery followed by the registered Universal Proof Program `POST-TUE-UPP-001`.

Results from one track do not silently discharge obligations in another. Project-authored execution, independent replication, executable corroboration, proof-assistant checking, and application correspondence remain separate evidence dimensions.

## Why the theorem is strictly weakened

The full unrestricted theorem is not claimed. The terminal result is weaker for two material reasons:

1. the complete semantic composition is executable, deterministic, adversarially tested, and cross-artifact checked, but is not represented as one kernel-checked proof object;
2. maximality is relative to frozen extension rules and a finite registered challenge ledger, not an open-world proof that no unregistered alternative exists.

The result does not establish metaphysical universality, consciousness conditions, unique ontology, final vocabulary, open-world maximality, or proof by absence of discovered counterexamples.

## Post-terminal phase

The deductive UPP queue is closed. The active phase is public evaluation, independent criticism, replication, and mechanization strengthening.

The post-terminal program is governed by `POST-TERM-EVAL-001`. It must:

- disclose the exact theorem and all material premises;
- preserve the distinction between the weakened and full outcomes;
- invite countermodels, failed reconstructions, equivalence challenges, and machinery-smuggling objections;
- classify evidence as deductive, executable, empirical, independent, or application-correspondence evidence;
- preserve `Unknown` as unresolved rather than support or defeat;
- require versioned revision or retraction when evidence defeats a premise or construction;
- prevent public summaries from omitting the finite maximality and non-kernel-checked composition boundaries.

Public evaluation is authorized only within that disclosure scope.

## Next legitimate work

There is no `UPP-W16`. New work falls into one of four categories:

1. independent proof review of the existing theorem;
2. kernel-checked reconstruction of the terminal semantic composition;
3. independent empirical replication of bounded evidence packages;
4. a newly registered deductive program addressing an explicitly frozen extension, counterexample, or stronger theorem.

Any new deductive program must have a new identifier, independently frozen definitions, explicit terminal outcomes, a finite stopping rule, and a claim-impact policy before execution begins.

## Proof-status discipline

Repository consistency checks do not constitute mathematical proof verification. CI success establishes repository-level consistency only. Every evaluation-facing claim must identify:

- the exact theorem version;
- its premises and scope;
- the evidence type supporting each dependency;
- the mechanization boundary;
- unresolved assumptions;
- nonclaims and countermodel boundary.

## Counterexample policy

Counterexamples, equivalences, reductions, hidden machinery, failed translations, non-effective reconstructions, and scope failures are primary research objects. They may not be repaired by redefining reasoning after exposure, narrowing the target class post hoc, omitting implementation costs, or treating `Unknown` as absence.

## Historical bounded-program note

They must not be silently rewritten as though they had originally contained the later UPP theorem.

Their historical line anchors and generated reports remain supported for repository navigation.

The later UPP result supersedes repository-wide status only within its own independently frozen premises and claim boundary.

No bounded artifact is promoted into an independent proof review merely because the terminal theorem references related concepts.

No terminal result erases negative evidence, unresolved empirical questions, or application-correspondence gaps.

Historical preservation is part of the audit trail, not a competing current program state.

Post-terminal work should therefore cite both the relevant historical artifact and the terminal disclosure when the distinction matters.
