# Central Research Program

## Purpose

Project FAR exists to determine whether there is a universal and minimal structure underlying reasoning.

The project does not assume that a universal structure exists, that FARA is that structure, or that faithful representation establishes reasoning-specific structure.

## Three-track program

### REP — Representation capacity and fidelity

`THM-TARGET-001`, `FAITHFUL-REP-001`, `P8-ROLE-001`, and W0 through W4 belong to the representation track.

REP asks whether one fixed target architecture can encode, recover, preserve, and reject registered invalid families over a frozen source class. REP success does not establish reasoning-specificity, necessity, minimality, uniqueness, or universality.

### ADJ — Adjudication and specificity

ADJ compares FARA with `GREL-001` and alternative architectures. It resolves dimensioned expressiveness, translation, constraint strength, reasoning specificity, machinery cost, and overall interpretation.

### USD — Universal-structure discovery

`THM-US-TARGET-001` governs architecture-neutral universal-structure discovery. The unknown kernel `K*` is not identified in advance with FARA, PB-001, or the current primitive list.

USD requires independently admitted reasoning and contrast systems, representation invariance, ablation, alternative reconstruction, and explicit equivalence and cost relations.

## Central research question

> Does every reasoning process necessarily instantiate a common underlying structure, and if so, is that structure both universal and minimal?

Finite representation, faithful representation, common-schema existence, reasoning-specificity, necessity, minimality, equivalence, uniqueness, and impossibility remain separate obligations.

## Proof-status discipline

Project-authored human proof, bounded executable corroboration, proof-assistant verification, independent proof review, empirical replication, and application correspondence remain separate.

Repository consistency checks do not constitute mathematical proof verification.

## Counterexample policy

Counterexamples, equivalences, reductions, generic factorizations, failed translations, and no-single-kernel results are primary research objects. They may not be repaired by redefining reasoning, narrowing the contrast class, changing a candidate after exposure, or omitting equivalent reintroduction costs.
