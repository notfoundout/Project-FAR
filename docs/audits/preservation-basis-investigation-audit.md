# Preservation Basis Investigation Audit

## Scope

This audit records the derivation and claim boundaries of PB-001.

## Dependency audit

PB-001 was derived from the frozen reasoning-domain specification and IRD-001. FARA primitives, FARO implementation structure, and prior favorable mappings were excluded as derivation inputs.

## Legacy-basis audit

The inherited six dimensions were not accepted as final:

- structural preservation was retained in narrowed form as configuration preservation;
- semantic preservation was decomposed into commitment, stake/alternatives, and consequence;
- operational preservation was sharpened into admissibility and dynamics;
- dependency preservation was sharpened into grounds and justification;
- historical preservation was retained as history and path;
- information preservation was reclassified as an aggregate no-relevant-loss criterion rather than an independent coordinate.

## Coverage audit

PB-001 assigns explicit axes to IRD-001's state/configuration, commitments, stakes, alternatives, grounds, admissible evolution, commitment consequences, histories, and evidential grades.

This is a derivation result only. Coverage across D1-D16 and the boundary classes remains to be tested in the next milestone.

## Candidate-neutrality audit

PB-001 does not require FARA terminology, symbolic representations, centralized control, deterministic transitions, fixed goals, fixed types, termination, or complete observability.

## Triviality audit

The specification prohibits lookup-only equivalence, metadata smuggling, hidden interpreters, evaluator repair, history recovery from external narrative, evidential upgrading, and universal opaque-state encodings.

## Independence and minimality audit

No axis is currently classified as independently necessary or minimal. P1-P8 are provisionally retained pending paired-system, ablation, and addition-search tests.

## Failure preservation

The registry explicitly permits axis redundancy, basis incompleteness, class-specific bases, no finite basis, IRD revision, and insufficient evidence.

## Scientific nonclaims

PB-001 does not establish a representation theorem or any FARA claim. It does not establish preservation-basis sufficiency, necessity, independence, minimality, or closure.

## Next dependency

The next scientific artifact is a frozen preservation-basis test suite. A FARA representation theorem remains blocked until that suite is executed and interpreted under preregistered rules.
