# IKD-W5 Expanded Invariance Audit

## Scope control

The audit is limited to RCCD-001 on the frozen bounded candidate and cross-feature composition scope. It does not infer global invariance from the registered transformations.

## Required controls

- Bidirectional commitment recovery is required.
- Operational consequences, grounds, dependencies, revisions, and historical identity must survive.
- Observation and probability queries must retain declared precision and epistemic limits.
- All bridges, adapters, filters, proof objects, schedulers, canonicalizers, and certificates are charged.
- Behavioral or observational equivalence is not internal-process identity.
- Unrestricted interpreters, source-specific decoders, and future-information access are forbidden.

## Coverage audit

The transformation suite spans event/state, semantic, causal, proof, type, process, coalgebraic, historical, stochastic, observational, temporal, and factorization changes. This is materially broader than renaming, ordering, annotation, or simple normalization.

## Negative-control audit

The result explicitly preserves failures for dependency erasure, retroactive history rewriting, semantic-version collapse, unjustified hidden-state collapse, scheduler hiding, nonmeasurable stochastic recoding, and proof-ground erasure.

## Claim audit

The accepted classification is bounded expanded invariance support. The audit rejects claims of exhaustive invariance, global necessity, minimality, uniqueness, universality, actual-process correspondence, or external validation.
