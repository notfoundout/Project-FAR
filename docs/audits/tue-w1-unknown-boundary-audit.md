# TUE-W1 Unknown-Boundary Audit

## Scope

This audit is limited to the three Unknown boundaries inherited from SC-W6: instrumentable creative generation, tacit moral salience, and systems whose relevant reasoning distinctions remain inaccessible.

## Anti-confirmation controls

- Source distinctions and measurement requirements are declared before RCCD mapping.
- Unknown is neither Pass nor Fail.
- Retrospective rationalization is not accepted as process-ground evidence without intervention or trace support.
- A visible-output mapping cannot establish full reasoning-faithfulness.
- Prompting, logging, elicitation, replay, decoders, similarity measures, stochastic proposal mechanisms, and interpretation procedures are charged.
- No sixth primitive is rejected merely because it can be named as domain content; it must be tested against all R1-R5 encodings after helpers are exposed.

## Case audit

The instrumented creative case supplies candidate, rejection, constraint, ground-defeat, and lineage evidence and is therefore assessable. It requires no sixth kernel primitive.

The moral-salience case is partially assessable. Articulated reasons and their intervention responses fit RCCD, while residual tacit salience remains inaccessible and is preserved as a boundary rather than silently promoted to Pass.

The general inaccessible-process case cannot support either preservation or failure because its relevant internal distinctions are unavailable to the frozen tests.

## Decision

The registered outcome is `principled_inaccessibility_boundary`. The creative Unknown is resolved only for instrumented cases; tacit and inaccessible remainders stay outside full adjudication. No genuine extension and no faithful RCCD counterexample is established.

## Claim boundary

This audit does not establish unrestricted coverage, actual-process correspondence, universal instrumentability, or external validation.
