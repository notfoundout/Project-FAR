# Audit — USD-W2 alternative-vocabulary competition

## Candidate adequacy

The comparison includes FARA, the required generic relational baseline, and two independently motivated non-FARA families. Candidate definitions are fixed before pairwise classification.

## Common-scope integrity

All candidates are tested against the same bounded scope and preservation commitments. Actual-process correspondence remains excluded because it requires empirical evidence unavailable to a formal vocabulary comparison.

## Machinery accounting

Typed predicates, provenance schemas, numeric interfaces, transition conventions, decoders, invariants, and history machinery are charged rather than treated as free implementation details.

## Aggregation integrity

No scalar score is used. Dominance requires no worse coverage and preservation, no greater registered cost on every cost dimension, and at least one strict improvement. Tradeoffs remain incomparability.

## Result audit

FARA dominates GREL and ARG-HIST on the registered bounded comparison. FARA does not dominate LTS-PROV because LTS-PROV uses fewer operations, while LTS-PROV does not dominate FARA because it requires more derived machinery and semantic description. The correct result is `multiple_incomparable_successful_vocabularies`, not a unique winner.

## Claim boundary

The result does not establish global superiority, exhaustiveness, invariance, necessity, minimality, uniqueness, universal structure, actual-process correspondence, independent review, or replication.
