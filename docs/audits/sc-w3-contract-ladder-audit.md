# SC-W3 Contract-Ladder Audit

## Anti-circularity

The contract clauses are expressed as recoverable judgments about content, admissibility, revision response, supersession, and finite query recovery. None requires an RCCD tuple, RCCD vocabulary, or a preferred encoding.

## Ablation discipline

Each preservation duty is removed separately. The associated RCCD component is then removed from the necessity conclusion rather than reconstructed through an uncharged adapter. Combined and alternative contracts are also evaluated.

## Machinery accounting

Interpretation maps, query-specific decoders, hidden histories, provenance closures, schedulers, lookup tables, canonicalizers, and nonuniform recovery procedures are charged. Existential per-query recovery is not silently treated as uniform recovery.

## Outcome discipline

The analysis does not force the positive conclusion. Outcome-only and prediction-only contracts yield generic-only commonality. Partial contracts yield partial RCCD kernels. The full five-component kernel appears only under the full independently stated reasoning-faithfulness profile.

## Adjudication

The result supports contract-relative necessity and does not establish contract-independent universality. It also does not find that the full contract is construct-loaded, because each duty has independent normative meaning and a separation witness.

External review remains deferred. The next internal test is whether a broader effective representation family can preserve the full contract while avoiding RCCD-equivalent commitments.
