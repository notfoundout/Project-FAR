# IKD-W6 Global Reconstruction Audit

## Audit controls

- The search is described as expanded and registered, not literally exhaustive over every possible ontology.
- A reconstruction must preserve commitments, operational consequences, dependencies, revisions, historical identity, and bounded queries.
- Surface compression is not a cost reduction when equivalent structure moves into decoders, schedulers, canonicalizers, certificates, or synthesis machinery.
- Unrestricted interpreters, source-specific decoders, future-information oracles, hidden schedulers, and retroactive history rewriting are prohibited.
- Failure to find a replacement is not treated as a proof of global nonexistence.

## Negative controls

The audit requires rejection of dependency erasure, final-state-only reconstruction, semantic-version collapse, observational-equivalence collapse, uncharged scheduler hiding, and decoder-based equivalent reintroduction.

## Lower-bound handoff

Each surviving countermodel must be converted in IKD-W7 into an explicit separation obligation identifying the removed commitment, the indistinguishable reduced representations, and the reasoning-relevant query or operational consequence that separates them. Unresolved separations must remain unresolved rather than being counted as necessity results.

## Decision

The registered search found no strictly cheaper non-equivalent reconstruction. Successful alternative bases reconstructed RCCD-equivalent commitments. This supports continuation to lower-bound analysis but does not itself establish global necessity or minimality.
