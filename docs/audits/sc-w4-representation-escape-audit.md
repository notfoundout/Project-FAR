# SC-W4 Representation Escape Audit

## Audit standard

A representation is not credited as an RCCD escape merely because it lacks literal RCCD labels. All semantic maps, decoders, schedulers, proof objects, provenance closures, history processors, normalization procedures, query compilers, indexes, and aggregation rules are charged.

A successful escape must preserve the full contract through one effective family-wide method while omitting the corresponding distinctions from both the declared representation and its charged machinery.

## Coverage

Twelve materially different representation families and twelve adversarial escape mechanisms were registered. The search includes discrete, continuous, probabilistic, distributed, proof-theoretic, behavioral, compressed, executable, categorical, and information-flow realizations.

## Failure controls

The audit rejects:

- output-only equivalence as reasoning faithfulness;
- final-state substitution for historical identity;
- source-specific lookup tables as uniform recovery;
- oracle or uncomputable decoding as effective representation;
- hidden guards as omission of constrained evolution;
- implicit proof annotations as omission of grounds;
- uncharged version tags as omission of history;
- learned decoders without a certified recovery contract as evidence of faithfulness.

## Findings

No registered effective family preserved the full contract without either failing at least one duty or reconstructing all five RCCD-equivalent commitments. Opaque learned representations remain unresolved when the decoder cannot be certified; they are not scored as positive cases.

## Scope control

This is a bounded internal escape search, not a proof that every possible mathematical encoding has been exhausted. Non-effective and oracle encodings are outside the theorem's effective-family scope rather than declared impossible.

External review and independent replication remain deferred. The final internal answer has not yet been issued.
