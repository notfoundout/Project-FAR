# PBTS-001 Independent Replication Package Freeze Audit

## Scope

This audit verifies the prospective freeze of `PBTS-001-REP-001` version 1.0. It evaluates packaging, independence boundaries, prior-result exclusion, response completeness, decision rules, and nonclaims. It does not evaluate any replicator or replication result.

## Protected-input audit

The registry records exact Git blob identifiers for:

- the frozen reasoning-domain specification;
- IRD-001;
- PB-001 and its registry;
- PBTS-001 and its registry;
- the evidence and replication standard;
- the Anti-Self-Validation Standard.

RUN-001 artifacts are listed separately as excluded prior-result materials. They are not participant-facing inputs and are not answer keys.

Result: **Pass for package definition.** Runtime verification remains a later execution gate.

## Independence audit

The package:

- requires three distinct primary paths;
- distinguishes R1-R5 rather than presuming R3;
- caps same-person, same-agent, or same-organization isolation at R2;
- requires separate provenance, exposure, assistance, and conflict declarations;
- requires at least two organizational affiliations unless unaffiliated independence is documented;
- separates replicator and final adjudicator roles;
- preserves downgraded and rejected submissions.

Result: **Pass prospectively.** No person is registered, so independent replication is not established.

## Public-repository exposure limitation

RUN-001 is publicly accessible in the repository. The package therefore cannot guarantee technical blinding. It instead requires:

- clean participant-facing bundle distribution;
- explicit prior-exposure attestation;
- pre-submission embargo among replicators;
- automatic downgrade of exposed submissions for the R3 gate;
- preservation of exposed submissions as evidence at the justified lower layer.

Result: **Known limitation, controlled procedurally.** The package must not claim guaranteed blinding.

## Calibration audit

The six-item calibration is unrelated to PB-001 labels and RUN-001 cases. It checks formal-specification reading, partial-order reasoning, observed-versus-admissible distinctions, history sensitivity, observation-versus-inference separation, hidden repair, and unresolved evidence.

The answer rubric is public and therefore tests documented understanding rather than resistance to answer-key access. Explanations and tool disclosures are required. Passing calibration does not establish independence.

Result: **Pass as standardization; not a secure examination.**

## Response-contract audit

The response schema requires:

- frozen package and run identifiers;
- protected-input verification;
- independence and exposure declarations;
- tool disclosures;
- all eight paired-axis results;
- all eight ablations;
- at least 32 domain records;
- all ten countermodels;
- hidden-recovery records;
- all ten addition-search hypotheses;
- dedicated P8 classification;
- deviations and nonclaims.

Result: **Pass.** Unique-ID and exact coverage constraints require the execution validator in the later run package.

## P8 neutrality audit

The package does not instruct replicators to reproduce RUN-001's P8 concern. It supplies five symmetric classifications and requires arguments, theorem implications, and a revision counterexample.

Result: **Pass.** Disagreement is allowed and may keep the theorem obligation unresolved.

## Aggregation audit

The decision rules preserve full distributions and distinguish:

- existential independent discrimination;
- 3/3 reproducible independent discrimination;
- non-lossless ablation evidence;
- unresolved nonredundancy;
- candidate-new-axis pressure;
- replication-layer classification.

No scalar score or majority vote replaces the registered distributions.

Result: **Pass.**

## Gate audit

Every execution and theorem gate is false. Registrations, calibrations, submissions, rejections, adjudications, and results are empty.

Result: **Pass.** Package freeze does not constitute execution.

## Baseline preservation

This change does not modify:

- the reasoning-domain specification;
- IRD-001;
- PB-001;
- PBTS-001;
- RUN-001 records or interpretation;
- Foundation, FARA, or FARO mathematics;
- CRE-004 controls.

## Nonclaim audit

The package explicitly denies:

- participant registration;
- independent execution;
- PB-001 sufficiency, necessity, independence, minimality, or completeness;
- P8 resolution;
- FARA compliance;
- a representation theorem;
- universality, superiority, comparative economy, or global minimality.

## Decision

`PBTS-001-REP-001` is suitable for prospective freeze as an independently executable replication package. It is not yet an independent replication study because no qualified replicator, assignment, calibration record, or append-only submission channel has been registered.

## Exact next action

Register qualified independent replicators and freeze `PBTS-001-REP-001-RUN-001` assignments, calibration records, exposure declarations, independence audits, and append-only submission channels without adding replication results.
