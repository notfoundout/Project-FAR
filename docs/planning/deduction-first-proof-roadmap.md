# Deduction-First Proof Roadmap

## Purpose

This roadmap defines the primary deductive path for Project FAR while separating finite representation results from universal-structure discovery.

## Dependency graph

```text
source class and theorem scope
        ↓
formal semantics and premises
        ↓
faithful-representation definition and P8 split
        ↓
W0 source normalization
        ↓
W1 target allocation and direct axes
        ↓
W2 dynamics, history, revision, self-modification
        ↓
W3 recovery, semantics, coherence, machinery, composition
        ├──────────────────────────┐
        ↓                          ↓
W4 formal negative controls   W3.5 baseline factorization,
        │                     FARA-specificity, contrast,
        │                     ablation, reconstruction
        └──────────────┬───────────┘
                       ↓
              W5 theorem-or-obstruction assembly
                       ↓
             bounded REP result with specificity class
                       ↓
      THM-US-TARGET-001 universal-discovery program
                       ↓
S_IRD extension, lower bounds, necessity, minimality,
equivalence, no-single-kernel alternatives
                       ↓
mechanization and independent proof review
```

## D1 — Representation theorem target

**Status:** frozen prospectively through `THM-TARGET-001` v1.0.

`THM-TARGET-001` is classified as the REP track. It does not imply reasoning-specificity or universal structure.

## D2 — Premises and semantics

**Status:** complete prospectively through premise ledger v1.6, `FAITHFUL-REP-001`, and `P8-ROLE-001`.

## D3 — Finite faithful-representation construction

**Status:** all finite construction and recovery conjuncts are project-authored as proved; global `Nontrivial` and theorem satisfiability remain unproved.

W0 proves source normalization. W1 proves direct-axis embeddings. W2 proves dynamics and history. W3 proves target-only recovery, semantic agreement, coherence, machinery accounting, uniformity, composition, and witness assembly.

## D3.5 — P8 role

**Status:** selected mode `split`.

`Corr_8E` remains separate and unproved.

## D4 — W4 formal negative controls

**Status:** active.

Resolve `OBS-SC-010` by proving NC-01 through NC-10 fail for their frozen structural reasons, or preserve any valid countermodel or hidden commitment.

A failed instance is insufficient. The result must quantify over the registered control family.

## D4.5 — W3.5 specificity and discovery bridge

**Status:** frozen, not executed.

Resolve:

1. `GREL-001` factorization;
2. FARA-specificity;
3. positive/contrast discrimination;
4. candidate-invariant ablation;
5. alternative reconstruction;
6. complete cost and machinery accounting;
7. central-claim impact.

W4 and W3.5 run in parallel.

## D5 — W5 bounded assembly

**Status:** blocked by `OBS-SC-010` and `W3.5-SDG-001`.

`ASM-SC-001` through `ASM-SC-003` may conclude:

- a scoped FARA representation theorem;
- a generic relational representation theorem;
- a translation-equivalence result;
- a proper-subclass theorem;
- a formal countermodel;
- an impossibility result;
- an unresolved result.

The assembly must state its specificity classification. It may not promote the result into USD.

## D6 — Universal-structure target

**Status:** frozen prospectively through `THM-US-TARGET-001`; unexecuted.

The USD program contains:

- `THM-US-EXIST-001`;
- `THM-US-INV-001[K]`;
- `THM-US-DISC-001[K]`;
- `THM-US-NEC-001[K]`;
- `THM-US-RED-001[K]`;
- `THM-US-MIN-001`;
- `THM-US-EQUIV-001`;
- `THM-US-NO-001`.

No REP theorem proves any USD theorem.

## D7 — Reasoning and contrast execution

Execute `RCS-001` without selecting positive systems for FARA compatibility or contrast systems for candidate absence.

## D8 — Candidate ablation and reconstruction

Classify each candidate as indispensable within a frozen class, derivable, replaceable, architecture-dependent, scope-dependent, generic, unnecessary, or unresolved.

## D9 — S_IRD extension

Attempt extension only under explicit obligations for infinite carriers, continuous dynamics, partial observability, open-ended histories, semantic change, non-finite-support stochasticity, and external correspondence.

An `S_core` result does not advance `S_IRD` automatically.

## D10 — Lower bounds, minimality, and equivalence

Freeze reconstruction classes, candidate universes, equivalence relations, and cost preorders before using necessity, minimality, or uniqueness language.

## D11 — Mechanization

Encode frozen definitions, assumptions, proof packages, bridge results, and theorem chains in a proof assistant or verified formal system.

W0–W3 Python references remain bounded corroboration.

## D12 — Independent review

Qualified reviewers should reconstruct proofs, challenge scope and assumptions, inspect mechanized artifacts, and search for countermodels, reductions, and hidden equivalences.

## Immediate next artifacts

1. Execute W4 formal negative controls.
2. Execute W3.5 generic-baseline factorization and specificity.
3. Execute the initial `RCS-001` contrast set and candidate registry where it does not prejudge W3.5.
4. Preserve failures, equivalences, reductions, and countermodels.
5. Do not begin W5 until W4 and W3.5 resolve.

## Current nonclaims

This roadmap does not establish:

- `Nontrivial` or `Faithful_split`;
- W3.5 factorization or specificity;
- a common-schema or representation theorem;
- a representation-independent universal kernel;
- FARA adequacy for `S_core` or `S_IRD`;
- candidate necessity or minimality;
- proof-assistant verification or independent review of W0–W3.
