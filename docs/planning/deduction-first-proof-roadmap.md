# Deduction-First Proof Roadmap

## Purpose

This roadmap defines the deductive path while separating bounded representation results from universal-structure discovery.

## Dependency graph

```text
source class and theorem scope
        ↓
formal semantics and premises
        ↓
faithful-representation definition and P8 split
        ↓
W0 → W1 → W2 → W3 → W4 complete
                         ↓
        W3.5 corpus freeze, baseline factorization,
        specificity, contrast, ablation, reconstruction,
        machinery cost, and claim-impact evidence
                         ↓
              W5 theorem-or-obstruction assembly
                         ↓
             bounded REP result with specificity class
                         ↓
       THM-US-TARGET-001 universal-discovery program
                         ↓
S_IRD extension, lower bounds, necessity, minimality,
equivalence, no-single-kernel alternatives
                         ↓
mechanization and independent proof review
```

## D1 — Representation theorem target

**Status:** frozen prospectively through `THM-TARGET-001` v1.0.

`THM-TARGET-001` is REP. It does not imply reasoning-specificity or universal structure.

## D2 — Premises and semantics

**Status:** complete prospectively through premise ledger v1.7, `FAITHFUL-REP-001`, and `P8-ROLE-001`.

W4 changed proof progress only. It did not alter a premise, source scope, target interface, or theorem status.

## D3 — Finite construction and recovery

**Status:** complete through W3.

W0 proves source normalization. W1 proves direct axes. W2 proves dynamics and history. W3 proves recovery, semantic agreement, coherence, machinery accounting, uniformity, composition, and witness assembly.

## D3.5 — P8 role

**Status:** selected mode `split`.

`Pres_8I` is internal. `Corr_8E` remains separate and unproved.

## D4 — Formal negative controls

**Status:** complete.

`SCORE-W4-PROOF-001` establishes `OBS-SC-010`: every applicable registered NC-01 through NC-10 family violates at least one frozen faithful-representation conjunct for its registered structural reason.

This proves registered nontriviality only. It does not prove that all invalid representations are rejected or that the constraints are FARA-specific.

## D4.5 — W3.5 specificity and discovery bridge

**Status:** frozen, not executed; sole blocker before W5.

Required execution order:

1. freeze a nonempty concrete positive and contrast corpus prospectively;
2. fix factorization and translation algorithms before comparison;
3. execute `S_core → GREL-001`, `GREL-001 → FARA`, and where defined `FARA → GREL-001`;
4. classify expressiveness, translation direction, constraint strength, reasoning specificity, cost relation, and overall interpretation independently;
5. execute candidate ablation and alternative reconstruction;
6. count semantic bridges, recovery procedures, normalization, hidden equivalences, and verifier machinery;
7. preserve failed translations, counterexamples, reductions, and tradeoffs;
8. emit immutable result artifacts with stable identifiers and matching SHA-256 digests;
9. update every central claim independently.

## D5 — W5 bounded assembly

**Status:** blocked by `W3.5-SDG-001`.

`OBS-SC-010` is resolved and may not remain listed as an active blocker.

`ASM-SC-001` through `ASM-SC-003` may conclude a scoped FARA theorem, generic representation theorem, translation-equivalence result, proper-subclass theorem, formal countermodel, impossibility result, or unresolved result.

The assembly must state its specificity classification and may not promote REP into USD.

## D6 — Universal-structure target

**Status:** frozen prospectively through `THM-US-TARGET-001`; unexecuted.

The USD theorem family includes existence, representation invariance, discrimination, necessity, reduction, minimality, equivalence, and no-single-kernel alternatives. No REP theorem proves any USD theorem.

## D7 — Reasoning and contrast execution

Execute the concrete RCS corpus only after prospective admission. Positive systems may not be selected for FARA compatibility; contrast systems may not be selected for candidate absence.

## D8 — Candidate ablation and reconstruction

Classify each candidate as indispensable within a frozen class, derivable, replaceable, architecture-dependent, scope-dependent, generic, unnecessary, or unresolved.

## D9 — S_IRD extension

Attempt extension only under explicit obligations for infinite carriers, continuous dynamics, partial observability, open-ended histories, semantic change, non-finite-support stochasticity, and external correspondence.

## D10 — Lower bounds, minimality, and equivalence

Freeze reconstruction classes, candidate universes, equivalence relations, and cost preorders before using necessity, minimality, or uniqueness language.

## D11 — Mechanization

Encode frozen definitions, assumptions, proofs, bridge results, and theorem chains in a proof assistant or verified formal system. W0-W4 executable references remain bounded corroboration.

## D12 — Independent review

Qualified reviewers should reconstruct proofs, challenge scope and assumptions, inspect mechanized artifacts, and search for countermodels, reductions, and hidden equivalences.

## Immediate next artifacts

1. Freeze the concrete RCS positive and contrast corpora.
2. Execute dimensioned GREL-FARA factorization.
3. Execute candidate ablation and alternative reconstruction.
4. Complete machinery and cost accounting.
5. Produce the W3.5 claim-impact and authorization audit.
6. Do not begin W5 until all W3.5 evidence gates resolve.

## Current nonclaims

This roadmap does not establish:

- complete `Faithful_split` satisfiability;
- rejection of every invalid representation;
- W3.5 factorization or specificity;
- a common-schema or representation theorem;
- a representation-independent universal kernel;
- FARA adequacy for `S_core` or `S_IRD`;
- candidate necessity or minimality;
- proof-assistant verification or independent review of W0-W4.
