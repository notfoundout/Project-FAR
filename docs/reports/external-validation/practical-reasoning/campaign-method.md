# Phase 2 Campaign 5 Method: Practical Reasoning External Validation

## Campaign purpose
This campaign tests whether the frozen Project FAR Foundation v1.0 can externally represent practical reasoning in four domains: legal reasoning, historical reasoning, intelligence analysis, and public-policy reasoning. The campaign is validation only. It does not amend axioms, definitions, lemmas, propositions, theorems, proofs, dependency metadata, doctrine, architecture, tooling, or automation.

## Frozen Foundation v1.0 baseline
The baseline is treated as fixed. Reports use FAR vocabulary as a reconstruction framework for representations, evidence, assumptions, competing hypotheses, dependency structures, conclusions, and confidence. Any gap is recorded as a limit of representation or domain dependence, not repaired by changing the Foundation.

## Evidence hierarchy
1. Primary legal, governmental, archival, statistical, or official review material.
2. Authoritative institutional summaries and research syntheses.
3. Peer-reviewed or field-recognized secondary interpretation where primary material alone does not resolve domain meaning.
4. No unsourced claims. If evidence is insufficient, the report records uncertainty.

## Source-reliability treatment
Sources are classified by institutional role, proximity to the events or decision, transparency of method, and known limits. Authority is evidence of provenance or domain rule status, not automatic proof of logical or empirical truth.

## Uncertainty treatment
Each reconstruction separates source facts, disputed facts, interpretations, assumptions, inferential steps, alternative explanations, and confidence. Confidence is qualitative unless a source supplies quantitative measures.

## Reconstruction standard
Each case is reconstructed charitably and adversarially. The charitable reconstruction asks whether FAR can represent the stated reasoning. The adversarial reconstruction asks whether the same evidence can support a different rule application, causal model, hypothesis, or policy conclusion.

## FAR mapping
Every case maps the reasoning into: Representation; Representational Structure; Interpretation; Investigation; Reasoning Calculus; Operation; Evidence; Assumptions; Competing hypotheses; Conclusion; Confidence; Dependency structure.

## Outcome classes
- PASS: FAR represents the reasoning without unresolved representational dependence beyond ordinary source citation.
- CONDITIONAL PASS: FAR represents the reasoning only after domain-specific legal rules, historical standards, intelligence tradecraft, policy models, or evaluative assumptions are explicitly supplied.
- FAIL: FAR cannot represent a necessary inferential component without changing Foundation v1.0.
- INCONCLUSIVE: available evidence is insufficient to decide whether representation succeeds.

## Non-claims
This campaign does not claim that FAR determines constitutional meaning, historical causation, intelligence truth, or optimal policy. It does not validate the substantive correctness of any court, historian, intelligence body, or policy model.

## Evaluation criteria
A case succeeds only if the report can identify the object represented, source evidence, inferential operation, hidden assumptions, alternative explanations, uncertainty, and dependency structure while preserving Foundation v1.0 unchanged.
