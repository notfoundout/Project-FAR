# Phase 2 Campaign 3: Scientific Reasoning Validation Summary

## Executive Summary

Project FAR Foundation v1.0 was tested against four concrete scientific reasoning cases:

- Newton's second law in physics;
- the ideal gas law in chemistry;
- Mendelian inheritance in biology;
- the Stroop effect in psychology.

All four case studies produced a CONDITIONAL PASS.

The common condition is that the scientific model, measurement design, operational definitions, inferential rules, and scope assumptions must be explicitly supplied. FAR can organize and audit the reasoning, but it does not independently establish the truth of the scientific model, perform the experiment, or select the correct causal explanation.

## Cross-Disciplinary Findings

### Finding 1 — Scientific inference is model- and design-relative

Measurements do not interpret themselves. A force reading, pressure value, phenotype count, or reaction time becomes evidential only inside a specified model, design, and investigation.

### Finding 2 — Prediction is not unique causal identification

Agreement with a model does not prove that the model is uniquely true. The same observation may be compatible with omitted forces, non-ideal gas behavior, alternative inheritance mechanisms, or multiple cognitive explanations.

### Finding 3 — Scope control is essential

Claims must remain bounded to the tested system, conditions, population, and operational definitions. FAR exposed overgeneralization in all four domains.

### Finding 4 — Measurement and representation choices affect the result

Units, coordinate frames, category definitions, trial exclusions, sampling, and variable construction are part of the reasoning structure, not neutral afterthoughts.

### Finding 5 — FAR does not replace scientific method

FAR provides an explicit architecture for hypotheses, evidence, models, operations, and conclusions. It does not perform instrumentation, experimental control, statistical estimation, replication, or causal discovery by itself.

## Falsification Attempts

The campaign attempted to expose failure through:

- omitted-force reasoning;
- incompatible units and frames;
- overgeneralization from one ideal-gas fit;
- misuse of absolute temperature;
- treating expected genetic ratios as unique causal proof;
- post hoc category changes;
- treating a Stroop effect as a unique mechanism or diagnosis;
- post hoc exclusions and incompatible measurement pooling.

FAR represented and classified the tested failures without requiring a Foundation v1.0 change, provided the scientific assumptions and inference rules were explicit.

## What This Campaign Does Not Establish

This campaign does not establish:

- empirical confirmation of FAR;
- correctness of the target scientific theories in every domain;
- causal sufficiency;
- statistical validity of unperformed analyses;
- replication of the cited phenomena;
- complete coverage of physics, chemistry, biology, or psychology;
- practical superiority over established scientific workflows;
- independent reproduction of the FAR mappings.

## Foundation Impact

No change to Foundation v1.0 is recommended from Campaign 3.

No axiom, definition, lemma, proposition, theorem, proof, dependency, or doctrine was modified.

## Final Campaign Status

SCIENTIFIC REASONING CAMPAIGN CONDITIONALLY PASSED

The frozen Project FAR foundation survived the tested scientific reasoning cases. The result is limited to the documented examples and does not justify unrestricted claims of empirical universality.
