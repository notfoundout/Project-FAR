# Scientific Reasoning External Validation Campaign Method

## Purpose

This campaign tests whether the frozen Project FAR Foundation v1.0 can represent and evaluate empirical scientific reasoning across four disciplines:

- physics;
- chemistry;
- biology;
- psychology.

The campaign uses concrete, established case studies rather than generic summaries.

## Frozen Baseline

Foundation v1.0 is fixed. No axiom, definition, lemma, proposition, theorem, proof, dependency, or doctrine may be changed in this branch.

## Evaluation Questions

Each case study identifies:

1. the hypothesis or model;
2. observations or measurements;
3. represented variables and structures;
4. interpretation of the observations;
5. investigation scope;
6. admissible inferential operations;
7. alternative explanations or model limits;
8. one valid inference;
9. one invalid inference;
10. one malformed or scope-violating case.

## Outcome Classes

- PASS: FAR represents the tested reasoning without relevant loss.
- CONDITIONAL PASS: FAR succeeds only when the scientific model, measurement assumptions, and inferential rules are supplied explicitly.
- FAIL: FAR cannot represent a required distinction without changing the frozen foundation.
- INCONCLUSIVE: the case does not decide the question.

## Source Discipline

The reports use established scientific examples:

- Newton's second law and force-acceleration reasoning;
- the ideal gas law as a model relating pressure, volume, amount, and absolute temperature;
- Mendelian inheritance from controlled pea-cross reasoning;
- the Stroop interference effect in color-word tasks.

The campaign distinguishes the source phenomenon from any broader causal or universal claim.

## Non-Claims

A successful case does not establish that FAR validates the truth of a scientific theory, replaces experimental methodology, performs statistical analysis, proves causality, or generalizes beyond the tested evidence.
