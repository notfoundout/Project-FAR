# Executive Summary

Phase 2 Campaign 4 tested whether the frozen Project FAR Foundation v1.0 can represent and audit five historically important philosophical arguments without changing foundational artifacts.

All five cases produced CONDITIONAL PASS results. FAR represented each argument's structure only after explicit interpretive choices, premise sets, and inferential standards were supplied.

# Campaign Scope

The campaign analyzed:

- Plato's Euthyphro dilemma;
- Aristotle's first-figure categorical syllogism from *Prior Analytics*;
- Hume's problem of induction from *An Enquiry Concerning Human Understanding*;
- Kant's transcendental argument for a priori conditions of objective experience from *Critique of Pure Reason*;
- Popper's falsifiability and verification/falsification asymmetry from *The Logic of Scientific Discovery*.

| Philosopher | Target argument | Outcome |
| --- | --- | --- |

| Aristotle | categorical syllogism | CONDITIONAL PASS |

| Kant | transcendental argument for conditions of objective experience | CONDITIONAL PASS |
| Popper | falsifiability and asymmetry between verification and falsification | CONDITIONAL PASS |

# Cross-Philosopher Findings

FAR successfully represented dilemma reasoning, syllogistic validity, skeptical elimination, transcendental dependence, and methodological-logical demarcation.

The most important shared finding is that philosophical arguments often depend on unstated interpretive and methodological assumptions. FAR made those assumptions explicit and prevented stronger conclusions from being attributed to the primary texts without support.

# Falsification Attempts

The campaign attempted to falsify FAR's representational adequacy through:

- non-exhaustive dilemma objections in Plato;
- equivocation and undistributed-middle cases in Aristotle;
- alternative standards for induction in Hume;
- alternative enabling conditions and uniqueness objections in Kant;
- auxiliary-assumption and theory-ladenness objections in Popper.

These attempts did not require a change to Foundation v1.0. They did require explicit dependency tracking and explicit interpretive commitments.

# Foundation Impact

No Foundation v1.0 artifact was changed.

No axiom, definition, lemma, proposition, theorem, proof object, dependency metadata, doctrine, repository architecture, tooling, or automation was modified.

# Limitations

The campaign does not establish that FAR solves the philosophical problems tested. It does not choose among contested scholarly interpretations, prove any premise sound, or replace detailed historical scholarship.

The campaign also does not establish complete coverage of philosophy. It tests five selected arguments only.

# Final Campaign Status

PHILOSOPHY CAMPAIGN CONDITIONALLY PASSED
