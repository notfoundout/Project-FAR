# Project FAR v0.4.0

## Purpose

Project FAR v0.4.0 records completion of the external-validation milestone that followed the v0.3.0 internal primitive-sufficiency baseline.

The release also records additional methodological work completed beyond the original v0.4.0 plan: adoption of the frozen Comparative Representation Protocol Version 1.0 (CRP v1.0) and registration of Comparative Representation Experiment 1 (CRE-001) as a prepared but unexecuted future comparative study.

## Summary

v0.4.0 preserves the v0.3.0 internal-validation baseline, adds preliminary external-validation evidence against systems, arguments, and practices not constructed for FAR, separates that external evidence from internal fixture evidence, and identifies recurring pressure points.

The release does not modify Foundation v1.0, FAR primitives, FARA primitives, FARO, FARE, FARM, canonical definitions, axioms, lemmas, propositions, theorems, accepted proof objects, accepted mathematical dependency metadata, mechanization behavior, parser behavior, CLI behavior, or accepted doctrine.

## Relationship to v0.3.0

Project FAR v0.3.0 remains the internal primitive-sufficiency evaluation baseline. v0.4.0 does not rewrite v0.3.0 conclusions, renumber v0.3.0 evidence, or retroactively reinterpret internal fixture results as external validation.

v0.4.0 extends the evidence record outward by preserving external-system investigations as preliminary compatibility and pressure-identification evidence. It does not convert successful external mappings into proof of universality, minimality, local necessity, or comparative superiority.

## Original Planned Objectives

The repository originally defined v0.4.0 as the phase that would:

1. preserve v0.3.0 as the internal-validation baseline;
2. execute external validation without silently rewriting v0.3.0 conclusions;
3. separate external evidence from internal fixture evidence;
4. identify recurring external pressure points.

The v0.3.0 release notes also identified related next-step work: resolve or further classify carried-forward unresolved reasoning-system pressures, deepen self-modifying reasoning analysis, expand falsification coverage without changing v0.3.0 conclusions, and promote only evidence-backed derived concepts or conservative extensions.

## Completed Objectives

v0.4.0 completes the historical v0.4.0 objective by:

- preserving the v0.3.0 internal-validation release as historical baseline;
- recording EV-001 through EV-029 in the external-validation registry;
- preserving external investigations separately from internal fixture evidence;
- synthesizing external-validation findings as preliminary, bounded evidence;
- identifying recurring pressure points without modifying protected theory or accepted doctrine;
- making clear that preliminary external validation is not sufficient methodology for stronger comparative claims.

## Additional Accomplishments Beyond the Original Plan

The completed repository work exceeds the original v0.4.0 objective by adding a canonical future-comparative methodology layer:

- CRP v1.0 is adopted as the frozen canonical methodology for future blinded, preregistered comparative representation studies.
- CRE-001 is registered as the first comparative representation experiment.
- CRE-001 has prepared scenario, vocabulary, calibration, evaluator-instruction, submission, CIR, adjudication, provenance, manifest, and pre-exposure checklist artifacts.
- Legacy external validation is explicitly preserved as preliminary evidence and subordinated only for stronger comparative claims.

These additions document methodological evolution. They do not create comparative results.

## Methodological Evolution to CRP v1.0

External validation remains useful for preliminary case description, scoped FAR/FARA mapping, preliminary preservation analysis, candidate-counterexample identification, and exploratory evidence collection.

The repository now distinguishes that preliminary layer from blinded comparative evaluation. Claims about comparative distinctiveness, reproducible structural constraint, relative representation economy, local necessity, or Pareto comparison are governed by CRP v1.0 rather than by legacy external-validation reports.

CRP v1.0 is frozen before evaluator exposure. Any substantive change requires a new protocol version, and Version 1.0 results may not be silently pooled with later versions.

## Preservation of Legacy External Validation

EV-001 through EV-029 are preserved. They are not renumbered, deleted, or reclassified as CRP v1.0 experiments.

Legacy reports retain their original evidential role as preliminary, historical, exploratory evidence. Successful FAR/FARA mappings in those reports are not comparative evidence and must not be used as if they were produced under blinded independent evaluation.

## CRE-001 Status

Comparative Representation Experiment 1: Reflective Discrete Rule-Transition System is registered but unexecuted.

CRE-001 currently has prepared execution inputs and templates. No evaluator has received a vocabulary, no mappings have been collected, no adjudication has occurred, no ablation results exist, no aggregation has been performed, and no comparative conclusions have been drawn.

## Repository Maturity

At v0.4.0, Project FAR has mature repository organization, documentation navigation, release history, internal-validation records, preliminary external-validation records, repository-health automation, and prepared comparative-experiment infrastructure.

Research maturity remains bounded. The repository has stronger methodology for future comparative studies, but the central research questions remain open until supported by executed evidence or formal results.

## Limitations

- v0.4.0 external validation is preliminary and not blinded comparative evidence.
- External-validation preservation judgments were not produced under CRP v1.0 evaluator isolation, canonicalization, adjudication, ablation, or aggregation rules.
- CRP v1.0 has not yet produced experimental results.
- CRE-001 has not yet tested any vocabulary.
- Universality, global minimality, uniqueness, metaphysical necessity, adequacy for all reasoning, and superiority over all possible vocabularies remain unestablished.

## Known Unresolved Questions

- Whether CRE-001 will support existential sufficiency, reproducible sufficiency, comparative structural reproducibility, comparative representation economy, or local necessity for any tested vocabulary.
- Whether future external systems will create genuine counterexamples, new scope boundaries, or unresolved interpretation disputes.
- Whether any simpler competing structure can match or dominate FAR-derived representational vocabulary under CRP v1.0 rules.
- Whether unresolved pressure points identified by preliminary external validation will survive blinded comparative testing.

## Release Boundary

CRP v1.0 is frozen, but CRE-001 has not yet been executed.

This release is a methodology, evidence-framing, and repository-status release. It does not alter protected theory, framework, proof, primitive, theorem, mechanization, parser, CLI, or accepted-doctrine artifacts.
