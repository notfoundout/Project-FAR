# FARE Discovery Investigation

## Identifier

FARE-028

---

# Title

Assessment Dependency

---

## Status

In Progress

---

# Purpose

Determine the necessary and sufficient conditions under which one assessment depends upon another.

The objective is to establish dependency as the foundational relationship between assessments.

---

# Motivation

Previous investigations established the internal architecture of individual assessments.

Complex evaluations frequently involve multiple assessments.

This investigation examines the relationships among those assessments.

---

# Central Question

When does one assessment depend upon another?

---

# Candidate 1

Reference.

Question

Does referencing another assessment establish dependency?

Evaluation

An assessment may reference another without requiring it.

Reference alone is insufficient.

Result

Rejected.

---

# Candidate 2

Agreement.

Question

Do assessments depend upon one another whenever they agree?

Evaluation

Agreement concerns evaluative outcomes.

Dependency concerns necessity.

The concepts are distinct.

Result

Rejected.

---

# Candidate 3

Necessary Support.

Question

Does one assessment depend upon another when the latter is required for the former?

Evaluation

Removing the supporting assessment prevents construction or justification of the dependent assessment.

Current evidence supports this hypothesis.

Result

Supported.

---

# Pattern Analysis

Every investigated dependency contains:

- a supporting assessment;
- a dependent assessment;
- an explicit dependency relation.

Removing the supporting assessment alters or prevents the dependent assessment.

---

# Emerging Hypothesis

Assessment A depends upon Assessment B if Assessment B is required for the construction, justification, or evaluation of Assessment A.

---

# Observation

Dependency is directional.

If Assessment A depends upon Assessment B,

Assessment B need not depend upon Assessment A.

---

# Consequences

If accepted:

Assessments become organized by explicit dependency.

Assessment graphs become investigable.

Dependency chains become traceable.

Circular assessment dependencies become detectable.

Future investigations should determine:
