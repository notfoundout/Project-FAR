# FARE Discovery Investigation

## Identifier

FARE-029

---

# Title

Assessment Support

---

## Status

In Progress

---

# Purpose

Determine the necessary and sufficient conditions under which one assessment supports another.

The objective is to establish support as a formal relationship between assessments.

---

# Motivation

Previous investigations established assessment dependency.

Dependency explains necessity.

Many assessments, however, contribute to other assessments without being strictly required.

This investigation examines that relationship.

---

# Central Question

When does one assessment support another?

---

# Candidate 1

Dependency.

Question

Is every supporting assessment a dependency?

Evaluation

Some assessments strengthen another assessment without being required.

Support appears broader than dependency.

Result

Rejected.

---

# Candidate 2

Agreement.

Question

Does agreement constitute support?

Evaluation

Two assessments may agree while remaining unrelated.

Agreement alone is insufficient.

Result

Rejected.

---

# Candidate 3

Positive Contribution.

Question

Does one assessment support another when it increases the justification or confidence of that assessment?

Evaluation

Current evidence supports this hypothesis.

Support strengthens an assessment without necessarily being indispensable.

Result

Supported.

---

# Pattern Analysis

Every investigated support relationship possesses:

- a supporting assessment;
- a supported assessment;
- an explicitly identifiable contribution.

Removing supporting assessments weakens the supported assessment.

Removal need not eliminate it.

---

# Emerging Hypothesis

Assessment A supports Assessment B if Assessment A contributes positively to the justification or confidence of Assessment B.

---

# Observation

Dependency implies support.

Support does not imply dependency.

Dependency therefore appears to be a stronger relationship.

---

# Consequences

If accepted:

Assessment relationships become more expressive.

Reasoning systems may distinguish:

- essential support;
- supplementary support.

Assessment graphs gain richer semantics.

---

# Relationship to Previous Investigations

Assessment Relationships

├── Dependency

└── Support

Dependency identifies necessity.

Support identifies positive contribution.

---

# Remaining Questions

Future investigations should determine:

- whether support admits degrees;
- whether multiple assessments combine support;
- whether support is transitive;
- whether support possesses algebraic properties.

---

# Current Status

The investigation remains active.

Current evidence supports treating support as positive evaluative contribution distinct from dependency.
