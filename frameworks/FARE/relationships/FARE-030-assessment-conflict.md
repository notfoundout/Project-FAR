# FARE Discovery Investigation

## Identifier

FARE-030

---

# Title

Assessment Conflict

---

## Status

In Progress

---

# Purpose

Determine the necessary and sufficient conditions under which two assessments conflict.

The objective is to establish conflict as a formal relationship between assessments.

---

# Motivation

Previous investigations established positive assessment relationships including dependency and support.

Reasoning systems must also account for assessments that cannot be jointly maintained.

This investigation examines conflict.

---

# Central Question

When do two assessments conflict?

---

# Candidate 1

Disagreement.

Question

Does disagreement constitute conflict?

Evaluation

Assessments may disagree while addressing unrelated evaluation objects.

Disagreement alone is insufficient.

Result

Rejected.

---

# Candidate 2

Logical Contradiction.

Question

Do two assessments conflict whenever accepting both produces contradiction?

Evaluation

Current evidence supports this hypothesis.

Conflict appears to arise from incompatibility rather than mere difference.

Result

Supported.

---

# Candidate 3

Different Confidence.

Question

Do differing confidence levels constitute conflict?

Evaluation

Assessments may assign different confidence while remaining compatible.

Confidence alone is insufficient.

Result

Rejected.

---

# Pattern Analysis

Every investigated conflict possesses:

- two or more assessments;
- incompatible evaluative content;
- inability to jointly maintain the assessments without contradiction.

Conflict concerns incompatibility rather than disagreement.

---

# Emerging Hypothesis

Assessment A conflicts with Assessment B if accepting both produces evaluative incompatibility or contradiction.

---

# Observation

Conflict is symmetric.

If Assessment A conflicts with Assessment B,

then Assessment B conflicts with Assessment A.

This differs from dependency, which is directional.

---

# Consequences

If accepted:

Assessment graphs may explicitly represent conflicting assessments.

Conflict resolution becomes formally investigable.

Alternative reasoning paths become representable.

---

# Relationship to Previous Investigations

Assessment Relationships

├── Dependency

├── Support

└── Conflict

Dependency captures necessity.

Support captures positive contribution.

Conflict captures incompatibility.

---

# Remaining Questions

Future investigations should determine:

- whether conflict admits degrees;
- whether conflicts may be resolved automatically;
- whether local conflicts differ from global conflicts;
- whether conflict graphs possess formal properties.

---

# Current Status

The investigation remains active.

Current evidence supports treating conflict as evaluative incompatibility rather than simple disagreement.
