# FARE Discovery Investigation

## Identifier

FARE-033

---

# Title

Assessment Status

---

## Status

In Progress

---

# Purpose

Determine the possible statuses of an assessment throughout its lifecycle.

The objective is to distinguish the content of an assessment from its current evaluative status.

---

# Motivation

Previous investigations established:

- dependency;
- support;
- conflict;
- refinement;
- resolution.

Resolution produces changes in how assessments are treated.

This investigation examines those resulting states.

---

# Central Question

What statuses may an assessment possess?

---

# Candidate 1

Accepted.

Question

May an assessment be accepted?

Evaluation

Acceptance indicates that the assessment currently satisfies the requirements governing its use.

Acceptance does not imply permanence.

Result

Supported.

---

# Candidate 2

Rejected.

Question

May an assessment be rejected?

Evaluation

Rejected assessments remain part of the investigative record.

Rejection concerns status rather than existence.

Result

Supported.

---

# Candidate 3

Provisional.

Question

May an assessment remain provisional?

Evaluation

Many assessments require additional investigation before final disposition.

Result

Supported.

---

# Candidate 4

Superseded.

Question

May an assessment be replaced by a refined assessment?

Evaluation

Refinement frequently produces improved assessments.

The original assessment remains historically significant.

Result

Supported.

---

# Candidate 5

Unresolved.

Question

May conflicting assessments remain unresolved?

Evaluation

Resolution is not guaranteed.

Some conflicts remain open.

Result

Supported.

---

# Pattern Analysis

Assessment status concerns the position of an assessment within an investigation.

Status does not alter the assessment itself.

Status may change over time.

---

# Emerging Hypothesis

Assessment status is the current evaluative disposition assigned to an assessment within an investigation.

---

# Candidate Statuses

Current candidates include:

- provisional;
- accepted;
- rejected;
- superseded;
- unresolved;
- archived.

Additional statuses may emerge through future investigations.

---

# Observation

Status is independent of assessment identity.

Changing status does not create a new assessment.

Revision changes the assessment.

Status changes only its disposition.

---

# Consequences

If accepted:

Assessment lifecycles become formally representable.

Investigations become explicitly state-based.

Historical assessment evolution becomes traceable.

# Remaining Questions

Future investigations should determine:

- whether status transitions are formally constrained;
- whether every status transition preserves history;
- whether statuses admit partial ordering;
- whether assessment workflows can be formally specified.

---

# Current Status

The investigation remains active.

Current evidence supports treating assessment status as the current evaluative disposition of an assessment rather than as part of the assessment itself.
