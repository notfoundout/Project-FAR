# FARE Discovery Investigation

## Identifier

FARE-031

---

# Title

Assessment Refinement

---

## Status

In Progress

---

# Purpose

Determine the necessary and sufficient conditions under which one assessment refines another.

The objective is to establish refinement as a formal relationship between assessments.

---

# Motivation

Previous investigations established:

- dependency;
- support;
- conflict.

Assessments frequently evolve through improved reasoning rather than replacement.

This investigation examines refinement.

---

# Central Question

When does one assessment refine another?

---

# Candidate 1

Revision.

Question

Is every revision a refinement?

Evaluation

Some revisions correct mistakes.

Others merely reorganize information.

Not every revision improves the assessment.

Result

Rejected.

---

# Candidate 2

Additional Information.

Question

Does adding information constitute refinement?

Evaluation

Additional information may be irrelevant.

Quantity alone is insufficient.

Result

Rejected.

---

# Candidate 3

Improved Assessment.

Question

Does one assessment refine another when it preserves the original evaluative content while increasing its precision, justification, completeness, or robustness?

Evaluation

Current evidence supports this hypothesis.

The refined assessment extends rather than contradicts the original.

Result

Supported.

---

# Pattern Analysis

Every investigated refinement possesses:

- an original assessment;
- a refined assessment;
- preservation of compatible evaluative content;
- improvement along one or more assessment properties.

Refinement strengthens rather than replaces.

---

# Emerging Hypothesis

Assessment A refines Assessment B if Assessment A preserves the evaluative content of Assessment B while improving one or more assessment properties.

---

# Observation

Refinement is directional.

If Assessment A refines Assessment B,

Assessment B does not necessarily refine Assessment A.

Refinement differs from conflict because both assessments remain compatible.

---

# Consequences

If accepted:

Assessment evolution becomes formally representable.

Investigations may explicitly distinguish:

- correction;
- refinement;
- replacement.

Assessment histories become more informative.

---

# Remaining Questions

Future investigations should determine:

- whether refinement admits degrees;
- whether multiple refinements may be composed;
- whether refinement preserves admissibility;
- whether refinement always increases confidence;
- whether refinement graphs possess unique properties.

---

# Current Status

The investigation remains active.

Current evidence supports treating refinement as the compatible improvement of one assessment by another.
