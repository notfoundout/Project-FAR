# FARE Discovery Investigation

## Identifier

FARE-034

---

# Title

Assessment History

---

## Status

In Progress

---

# Purpose

Determine the formal structure of assessment history.

The objective is to establish how the evolution of an assessment is represented throughout its lifecycle.

---

# Motivation

Previous investigations established:

- assessment revision;
- assessment refinement;
- assessment resolution;
- assessment status.

Each changes the state of an assessment over time.

This investigation examines how those changes are represented.

---

# Central Question

What constitutes the history of an assessment?

---

# Candidate 1

Current Status.

Question

Is history identical to the current status?

Evaluation

Status represents only the present disposition.

History records the sequence of prior states.

Result

Rejected.

---

# Candidate 2

Collection of Revisions.

Question

Is history merely the set of revisions?

Evaluation

History includes revisions.

It may also include:

- status changes;
- resolutions;
- refinements;
- relationship changes.

Result

Partially Supported.

---

# Candidate 3

Chronological Evolution.

Question

Is assessment history the ordered sequence of events affecting an assessment throughout its lifecycle?

Evaluation

Current evidence supports this hypothesis.

History captures evolution rather than isolated changes.

Result

Supported.

---

# Pattern Analysis

Every investigated assessment history contains:

- an initial assessment;
- one or more lifecycle events;
- explicit ordering of those events.

Removing the ordering destroys the historical record.

---

# Emerging Hypothesis

Assessment history is the ordered record of lifecycle events affecting an assessment.

---

# Candidate Lifecycle Events

Current candidates include:

- creation;
- revision;
- refinement;
- conflict;
- resolution;
- status transition;
- supersession;
- archival.

Additional events may emerge through future investigations.

---

# Observation

History is immutable.

New events extend history.

Existing events remain part of the permanent investigative record.

---

# Consequences

If accepted:

Assessment evolution becomes fully traceable.

Investigations gain complete evaluative provenance.

Historical reconstruction becomes formally possible.

---

# Relationship to Previous Investigations

Assessment Lifecycle

├── Status

└── History

History records the complete evolution of an assessment.

Status represents its current disposition.

---

# Remaining Questions

Future investigations should determine:

- whether histories admit branching;
- whether histories form directed graphs;
- whether histories possess canonical representations;
- whether histories preserve assessment identity.

---

# Current Status

The investigation remains active.

Current evidence supports treating assessment history as the ordered record of lifecycle events affecting an assessment.
