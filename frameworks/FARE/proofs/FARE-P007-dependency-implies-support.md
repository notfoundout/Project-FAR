# FARE Proof

## Identifier

FARE-P007

---

# Title

Dependency Implies Support

---

## Status

Draft

---

# Objective

Demonstrate that every assessment upon which another assessment depends necessarily supports that assessment.

---

# Definitions Used

- Assessment
- Assessment Dependency
- Assessment Support

---

# Theorem

If Assessment A depends upon Assessment B, then Assessment B supports Assessment A.

---

# Proof

By the definition of assessment dependency, Assessment A depends upon Assessment B when Assessment B is required for the construction, justification, or evaluation of Assessment A.

By the definition of assessment support, Assessment B supports Assessment A when it contributes positively to the justification or confidence of Assessment A.

If Assessment B is required for the construction, justification, or evaluation of Assessment A, then removing Assessment B prevents or weakens the evaluative basis of Assessment A.

Therefore Assessment B contributes positively to Assessment A.

Hence Assessment B supports Assessment A.

Therefore every dependency relationship is also a support relationship.

∎

---

# Corollary 1

Dependency is a stronger relationship than support.

---

# Corollary 2

Support does not necessarily imply dependency.

An assessment may strengthen another assessment without being required for it.

---

# Consequences

Dependency relationships may be treated as a proper subset of support relationships.

Support graphs therefore generalize dependency graphs.

# Notes

This theorem establishes only the implication from dependency to support.

The converse is explicitly false under the current definitions.
