# FARE Mathematical Conjecture

## Identifier

FARE-C012

---

# Title

Minimal Evaluation Information

---

## Status

Conjecture

---

# Question

What is the minimum information required to uniquely determine the evaluation of an assessment?

---

# Motivation

Every evaluation depends upon information.

Some information is essential.

Some information is redundant.

Identifying the smallest information set capable of determining an evaluation establishes a theoretical lower bound for reasoning evaluation.

---

# Informal Statement

Every evaluation possesses one or more minimal determining information sets.

---

# Required Definitions

This conjecture requires canonical definitions for:

- evaluation information;
- determining information;
- information equivalence;
- minimal determining set;
- informational redundancy.

---

# Formal Statement

Pending definition.

---

# Initial Hypothesis

Minimal determining information sets are likely to exist for finite evaluations.

Uniqueness is unlikely.

Multiple incomparable minimal determining sets may exist.

---

# Counterexample Search

Construct evaluations containing:

- redundant evidence;
- duplicated support;
- equivalent dependency paths;
- alternative justification chains.

Determine whether distinct minimal determining information sets exist.

---

# Research Questions

- What information is indispensable?
- What information is redundant?
- Can minimal determining sets be efficiently computed?
- Are they unique?
- Do different investigations admit different minimal information requirements?

---

# Possible Results

## Result 1

Every finite evaluation possesses at least one minimal determining information set.

---

## Result 2

Minimal determining information sets are generally non-unique.

---

## Result 3

Certain evaluations possess no finite determining information set.

---

## Result 4

Minimal determining information depends upon the chosen evaluation semantics.

---

# Notes

This conjecture concerns the informational requirements of evaluation rather than the evaluation procedure itself.

Its resolution would establish lower bounds on the information necessary for reasoning evaluation and could provide a bridge between FARE and information theory.
