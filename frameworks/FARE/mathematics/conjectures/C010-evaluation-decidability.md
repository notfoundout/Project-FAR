# FARE Mathematical Conjecture

## Identifier

FARE-C010

---

# Title

Evaluation Decidability

---

## Status

Conjecture

---

# Question

Does every finite evaluation problem admit a terminating decision procedure?

---

# Motivation

A formal evaluation framework should determine whether evaluation is always computable.

If certain evaluation problems are undecidable, the expressive limits of FARE must be characterized.

---

# Informal Statement

Every finite evaluation problem expressible within FARE may admit a finite decision procedure.

---

# Required Definitions

This conjecture requires canonical definitions for:

- evaluation problem;
- decision procedure;
- termination;
- decidability;
- evaluation algorithm.

---

# Formal Statement

Pending definition.

---

# Initial Hypothesis

Evaluation problems over finite assessment graphs are likely to be decidable.

Whether unrestricted evaluation remains decidable is unknown.

---

# Counterexample Search

Investigate evaluation problems involving:

- recursive assessment structures;
- cyclic dependency systems;
- self-referential assessments;
- infinite evaluation processes.

Determine whether any prevent termination.

---

# Research Questions

- Which evaluation problems are decidable?
- Which require bounded graph assumptions?
- Which become undecidable under unrestricted recursion?
- What structural restrictions guarantee decidability?

---

# Possible Results

## Result 1

Every finite evaluation problem is decidable.

---

## Result 2

Only restricted classes of evaluation problems are decidable.

---

## Result 3

Certain evaluation problems are fundamentally undecidable.

---

## Result 4

Decidability depends entirely upon graph and dependency constraints.

# Notes

This conjecture concerns algorithmic decidability.

It does not address computational efficiency.

A decidable problem may still be computationally intractable.
