# FARE Mathematical Conjecture

## Identifier

FARE-C020

---

# Title

Evaluation Completion

---

## Status

Conjecture

---

# Question

Does every convergent sequence of finite evaluations converge to a well-defined evaluation within the evaluation space?

---

# Motivation

If evaluation spaces admit distance, geodesics, curvature, and convexity, the next natural question is whether they are complete.

Completeness determines whether indefinitely refined evaluations eventually stabilize within the framework or whether refinement can escape the evaluation space.

---

# Informal Statement

Every convergent evaluation sequence possesses a limit that is itself a valid evaluation.

---

# Required Definitions

This conjecture requires canonical definitions for:

- evaluation sequence;

Pending definition.

---

# Initial Hypothesis

Completeness is likely to depend upon the admissible evaluation transformations.

Finite evaluation spaces may be complete, while unrestricted spaces may require completion by introducing ideal evaluation objects.

---

# Counterexample Search

Construct sequences involving:

- successive redundancy elimination;
- repeated normalization;
- infinite refinement;
- recursive dependency expansion;
- progressively smaller evaluation distances.

Determine whether these sequences converge to valid evaluations.

---

# Research Questions

- What does convergence mean for evaluations?
- Does every Cauchy-like evaluation sequence converge?
- Are completion objects required?
- Can incomplete evaluation spaces be completed?
- Is the completion unique?

---

# Possible Results

## Result 1

Every finite evaluation space is complete.

---

## Result 2

Only restricted evaluation spaces are complete.

---

## Result 3

Evaluation spaces admit a unique completion.

---

## Result 4

Some evaluation sequences fail to converge within the current framework.

# Notes

This conjecture investigates the global structure of evaluation spaces.

The notion of convergence should be derived from FARE's own definition of evaluation distance rather than imported from existing metric spaces.

If evaluation spaces are incomplete, their completion may introduce new mathematical objects worthy of independent investigation.
