# FARE Mathematical Conjecture

## Identifier

FARE-C015

---

# Title

Evaluation Invariants

---

## Status

Conjecture

---

# Question

Which properties of an evaluation remain unchanged under evaluation-preserving transformations?

---

# Motivation

Many areas of mathematics classify objects using invariants.

If evaluations possess analogous invariants, then structurally different evaluations may be recognized as representing the same underlying evaluative object.

Such invariants would become fundamental tools for classification, comparison, optimization, and canonicalization.

---

# Informal Statement

Every evaluation-preserving transformation preserves one or more structural invariants.

---

# Initial Hypothesis

Different classes of invariants are likely to exist.

Possible examples include:

- dependency invariants;
- support invariants;
- conflict invariants;
- informational invariants;
- dimensional invariants.

Not every structural property is expected to be invariant.

---

# Counterexample Search

Construct evaluation-preserving transformations that modify:

- node ordering;
- dependency representation;
- redundant support;
- graph layout.

Determine which properties remain unchanged across every transformation.

---

# Research Questions

- Which structural properties are invariant?
- Which numerical measures are invariant?

Every evaluation possesses a finite set of complete invariants.

Only partial invariants exist.

## Result 3

Different equivalence relations induce different invariant families.

---

## Result 4

This conjecture investigates the existence of intrinsic properties of evaluations that survive all accepted evaluation-preserving transformations.

A successful resolution would establish the mathematical foundation for classifying evaluations independently of their particular representation.
