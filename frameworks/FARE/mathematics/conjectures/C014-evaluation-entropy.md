# FARE Mathematical Conjecture

## Identifier

FARE-C014

---

# Title

Evaluation Entropy

---

## Status

Conjecture

---

# Question

Can every finite evaluation be assigned a well-defined measure of evaluation entropy?

---

# Motivation

Some evaluations are highly constrained.

Others admit many equivalent reasoning paths, support structures, or acceptable evaluation sequences.

If these differences can be measured, evaluation entropy may quantify the structural uncertainty or flexibility inherent in an evaluation.

---

# Informal Statement

Every finite evaluation possesses an entropy measure describing the amount of structural freedom remaining after all evaluation constraints have been applied.

---

# Required Definitions

This conjecture requires canonical definitions for:

- evaluation entropy;
- evaluation state;
- structural uncertainty;
- evaluation constraint;
- entropy-preserving transformation.

---

# Formal Statement

Pending definition.

---

# Initial Hypothesis

Evaluation entropy is unlikely to correspond directly to information-theoretic entropy.

Instead, it may represent the number or diversity of structurally valid evaluation states compatible with the same accepted conclusions.

---

# Counterexample Search

Construct evaluations having:

- identical conclusions but different numbers of valid support structures;
- different dependency densities;
- different canonical forms;
- varying levels of redundancy.

Determine whether a single entropy measure distinguishes these evaluations.

---

# Research Questions

- What contributes to evaluation entropy?
- Is entropy invariant under evaluation equivalence?
- Does normalization reduce entropy?
- Does additional evidence always reduce entropy?
- Can entropy increase during investigation?

---

# Possible Results

## Result 1

Every finite evaluation possesses a unique entropy value.

---

## Result 2

Entropy depends upon the chosen equivalence relation.

---

## Result 3

Multiple entropy measures exist for different classes of evaluation.

---

## Result 4

No useful entropy measure exists within the current formal framework.

---

# Applications

A solution would support:

- evaluation comparison;
- reasoning complexity analysis;
- investigation progress measurement;
- optimization of evaluation procedures;
- explainable reasoning metrics.

# Notes

The term "entropy" is used provisionally.

This conjecture does not assume Shannon entropy or any existing mathematical definition.

Instead, it asks whether FARE naturally induces a meaningful quantitative measure of structural uncertainty or evaluative flexibility.

Any resulting measure should arise from FARE's own formal definitions rather than being imported from another mathematical framework.
