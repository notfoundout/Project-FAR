---

# Title

Infinite Distance Implies Non-Reachability

---

# Status

Draft

---

# Objective

Investigate whether `d(E_1, E_2) = infinity` implies that `E_2` is not reachable from `E_1`.

---

# Dependencies

- MDEF-003
- MDEF-004

---

# Statement

If `d(E_1, E_2) = infinity`, then `E_2` is not reachable from `E_1`.

---

# Proof

Proof pending.

---

# Corollaries

None.

---

# Consequences

If accepted, this theorem would connect infinite distance to absence of reachability.

---

# Notes

MDEF-004 states that no admissible path implies infinite distance. The converse is not accepted here without further review of the cost model and path-cost assumptions.
