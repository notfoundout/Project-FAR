# Mathematical Theorem

## Identifier

MT-008

---

# Title

Eventually Constant Sequences Converge Under Valid Neighborhood Systems

---

# Status

Accepted

---

# Objective

Show that a sequence eventually constant at `E` converges to `E` when neighborhoods of `E` contain `E`.

---

# Dependencies

- MDEF-006
- MDEF-007
- MT-007

---

# Statement

If a sequence is eventually constant at `E`, and every neighborhood in `N(E)` contains `E`, then the sequence converges to `E` relative to `N(E)`.

---

# Proof

Let `(E_1, E_2, E_3, ...)` be eventually constant at `E`.

Then there exists an index `N_0` such that for every `n >= N_0`, `E_n = E`.

Let `U` be any neighborhood in `N(E)`.

By MT-007, `E` belongs to `U`.

Therefore for every `n >= N_0`, `E_n` belongs to `U`.

By MDEF-007, the sequence converges to `E` relative to `N(E)`.

---

# Corollaries

None.

---

# Consequences

Eventually constant sequences have a basic convergence guarantee under valid neighborhood systems.

---

# Notes

This theorem does not establish uniqueness of limits.
