# FARE Discovery Investigation

## Identifier

FARE-022

---

# Title

Reliability and Reproducibility

---

## Status

In Progress

---

# Purpose

Determine the relationship between reliability and reproducibility.

The objective is to establish whether these concepts are independent, dependent, or equivalent process properties.

---

# Motivation

Previous investigations established:

- reliability;
- reproducibility.

Both concern repeated evaluation.

Their relationship remains undefined.

---

# Central Question

How are reliability and reproducibility related?

---

# Candidate 1

Equivalent Properties.

Question

Are reliability and reproducibility identical?

Evaluation

Reliability concerns repeated execution of an evaluation process.

Reproducibility concerns independent execution by multiple evaluators.

The concepts appear distinct.

Result

Rejected.

---

# Candidate 2

Reliability Implies Reproducibility.

Question

Does a reliable evaluation necessarily become reproducible?

Evaluation

Repeated success by one evaluator does not guarantee equivalent results by independent evaluators.

Result

Rejected.

---

# Candidate 3

Reproducibility Implies Reliability.

Question

Does reproducibility require reliability?

Evaluation

Independent evaluators cannot consistently reproduce results if the underlying evaluation process is itself unstable.

Reliability therefore appears necessary for reproducibility.

Result

Provisionally Supported.

---

# Candidate 4

Independent Properties.

Question

Are reliability and reproducibility independent?

Evaluation

Current evidence suggests partial dependency.

Reliability appears to contribute to reproducibility without completely determining it.

Result

Partially Supported.

---

# Pattern Analysis

Reliability evaluates:

- stability of an evaluation process under repeated execution.

Reproducibility evaluates:

- stability across independent evaluators.

Reproducibility introduces additional requirements beyond reliability.

---

# Emerging Hypothesis

Reproducibility depends upon reliability but additionally requires evaluator-independent application of the evaluation process.

---

# Observation

Reliability concerns the process.

Reproducibility concerns the process together with evaluator independence.

The latter therefore appears more restrictive.

---

# Consequences

If accepted:

The dependency graph of process properties becomes explicit.

Confidence investigations may distinguish:

- process stability;
- evaluator independence.

---

# Relationship to Previous Investigations

Evaluation Process

│

├── Reliability

│

└── Reproducibility

Reproducibility builds upon reliability by introducing independent replication.

---

# Remaining Questions

Future investigations should determine:

- whether reproducibility admits degrees;
- whether evaluator equivalence requires formal definition;
- whether reproducibility may fail despite perfect reliability;
- whether reproducibility contributes directly to confidence.

---

# Current Status

The investigation remains active.

Current evidence supports treating reproducibility as a stronger process property built upon reliability rather than as an equivalent concept.
