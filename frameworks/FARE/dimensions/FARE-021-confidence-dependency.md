# FARE Discovery Investigation

## Identifier

FARE-021

---

# Title

Confidence Dependency

---

## Status

In Progress

---

# Purpose

Determine whether confidence is an independent assessment dimension or whether it depends upon other evaluative dimensions.

The objective is to establish the dependency relationships governing confidence.

---

# Motivation

Previous investigations established:

- validity;
- justification.

Confidence appears related to these dimensions.

Before defining confidence, its dependencies should be investigated.

---

# Central Question

Upon what does confidence depend?

---

# Candidate 1

Confidence is Primitive.

Question

Is confidence an independent evaluative property?

Evaluation

Current evidence is insufficient.

Its apparent relationships to other dimensions require investigation.

Result

Undetermined.

---

# Candidate 2

Confidence Depends Upon Validity.

Question

Does valid assessment imply confidence?

Evaluation

Validity concerns correct application of evaluation criteria.

Correct procedure alone does not determine confidence.

Result

Not Supported.

---

# Candidate 3

Confidence Depends Upon Justification.

Question

Does stronger justification increase confidence?

Evaluation

Current evidence supports this relationship.

More comprehensive, explicit, and coherent justification generally permits greater confidence.

Result

Supported.

---

# Candidate 4

Confidence Depends Upon Multiple Dimensions.

Question

May confidence depend simultaneously upon:

- validity;
- justification;
- reliability;
- reproducibility?

Evaluation

Current evidence supports this possibility.

Confidence appears to summarize multiple evaluative properties rather than a single one.

Result

Strongly Supported.

---

# Pattern Analysis

Confidence does not appear fundamental.

Instead, confidence appears to depend upon previously established evaluative dimensions.

This distinguishes confidence from validity.

---

# Emerging Hypothesis

Confidence is a derived assessment dimension reflecting the combined contribution of other evaluative dimensions.

---

# Observation

Changes to:

- justification;
- reliability;
- reproducibility;

may alter confidence without changing the assessment itself.

Confidence therefore appears derivative rather than primitive.

---

# Consequences

If accepted:

Confidence should be investigated after its dependencies.

Project FAR minimizes unnecessary primitives.

The dependency architecture of FARE becomes more explicit.

---

# Relationship to Previous Investigations

Assessment

├── Validity

├── Justification

├── Reliability

├── Reproducibility

└── Confidence

Confidence depends upon one or more preceding dimensions.

---

# Remaining Questions

Future investigations should determine:

- the complete dependency graph governing confidence;
- whether confidence admits quantitative representation;
- whether confidence possesses upper and lower bounds;
- whether confidence aggregation is formally defined.

---

# Current Status

The investigation remains active.

Current evidence supports treating confidence as a derived evaluative property rather than an independent primitive.
