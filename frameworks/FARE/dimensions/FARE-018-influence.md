# FARE Discovery Investigation

## Identifier

FARE-018

---

# Title

Influence

---

## Status

In Progress

---

# Purpose

Determine the necessary and sufficient conditions under which one element influences an evaluation.

The objective is to establish the concept of influence independently of relevance.

---

# Motivation

Previous investigations proposed that relevance depends upon whether changing an element changes the resulting assessment.

This definition presupposes the concept of influence.

Influence must therefore be investigated directly.

---

# Central Question

What does it mean for one element to influence an evaluation?

---

# Candidate 1

Correlation.

Question

Is influence merely statistical correlation?

Evaluation

Two elements may correlate without either affecting the other.

Correlation is insufficient.

Result

Rejected.

---

# Candidate 2

Association.

Question

Is influence equivalent to association?

Evaluation

Association does not imply evaluative effect.

Result

Rejected.

---

# Candidate 3

Counterfactual Dependence.

Question

Does one element influence an evaluation if changing that element, while preserving all other relevant elements, changes the resulting assessment?

Evaluation

This directly characterizes evaluative influence.

Current evidence supports this hypothesis.

Result

Supported.

---

# Pattern Analysis

Every investigated instance of influence requires:

- an evaluation;
- an element;
- a hypothetical modification;
- comparison of resulting assessments.

Influence therefore concerns dependency under controlled variation.

---

# Emerging Hypothesis

An element influences an evaluation if modifying that element while preserving all other relevant evaluation conditions changes the resulting assessment.

---

# Observation

Influence is relative.

The same element may influence one evaluation while having no influence upon another.

Influence therefore depends upon the evaluation context.

---

# Consequences

If accepted:

Relevance becomes definable.

Condition equivalence becomes formally characterizable.

Sensitivity analysis becomes investigable.

Evaluation dependencies become explicit.

---

# Relationship to Previous Investigations

Evaluation

├── Conditions

│     ├── Influence

│     └── Relevance

Influence determines whether an element affects an assessment.

Relevance identifies those elements possessing influence.

---

# Remaining Questions

Future investigations should determine:

- whether influence admits degrees;
- whether influence is directional;
- whether influence composes;
- whether influence graphs exist;
- whether influence differs from dependency.

---

# Current Status

The investigation remains active.

Current evidence supports treating influence as counterfactual dependence within an evaluation.
