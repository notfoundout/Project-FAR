# FARE Discovery Investigation

## Identifier

FARE-017

---

# Title

Relevance

---

## Status

In Progress

---

# Purpose

Determine the necessary and sufficient conditions under which one representation is relevant to an evaluation.

The objective is to establish the formal concept of evaluative relevance.

---

# Motivation

Previous investigations introduced:

- evaluation conditions;
- condition equivalence.

Both require distinguishing relevant factors from irrelevant factors.

The concept of relevance has not yet been investigated.

---

# Central Question

What makes something relevant to an evaluation?

---

# Candidate 1

Usefulness.

Question

Is every useful representation relevant?

Evaluation

Something may be useful without influencing the evaluation.

Result

Rejected.

---

# Candidate 2

Association.

Question

Is every associated representation relevant?

Evaluation

Many associated representations have no effect upon the evaluation.

Association alone is insufficient.

Result

Rejected.

---

# Candidate 3

Influence.

Question

Is a representation relevant if changing it can alter the resulting assessment?

Evaluation

Current evidence supports this hypothesis.

Representations capable of influencing the assessment appear relevant.

Representations incapable of influencing the assessment appear irrelevant.

Result

Supported.

---

# Pattern Analysis

Every investigated instance of relevance involves:

- an evaluation;
- one or more candidate representations;
- the possibility that modifying a representation changes the assessment.

Influence appears to distinguish relevant from irrelevant information.

---

# Emerging Hypothesis

A representation is relevant to an evaluation if altering that representation can alter the resulting assessment.

---

# Observation

Relevance is relative.

The same representation may be:

- relevant to one evaluation;
- irrelevant to another.

Relevance therefore depends upon the evaluation objective.

---

# Consequences

If accepted:

Condition equivalence becomes formally definable.

Evidence evaluation becomes investigable.

Framework evaluation gains explicit relevance criteria.

Evaluation complexity may be reduced by excluding irrelevant information.

---

# Relationship to Previous Investigations

Evaluation

├── Object

├── Criteria

├── Conditions

│     └── Relevance

├── Comparison

└── Assessment

Relevance determines which components of the evaluation conditions influence the assessment.

---

# Remaining Questions

Future investigations should determine:

- whether relevance admits degrees;
- whether relevance is transitive;
- whether relevance may be formally proven;
- whether relevance possesses algebraic properties;
- whether causal influence and evaluative relevance are identical.

---

# Current Status

The investigation remains active.

Current evidence supports treating relevance as evaluative influence upon assessment.
