# FARE Discovery Investigation

## Identifier

FARE-016

---

# Title

Condition Equivalence

---

## Status

In Progress

---

# Purpose

Determine the necessary and sufficient conditions under which two evaluation conditions are considered equivalent.

The objective is to establish the formal basis upon which reliability and reproducibility are evaluated.

---

# Motivation

Previous investigations established the concept of evaluation conditions.

Reliability and reproducibility require evaluations performed under equivalent conditions.

The meaning of equivalent conditions remains undefined.

---

# Central Question

When are two evaluation conditions equivalent?

---

# Candidate 1

Identical Conditions.

Question

Must equivalent conditions be identical?

Evaluation

Minor differences may exist without influencing the evaluation.

Identity appears stronger than equivalence.

Result

Rejected.

---

# Candidate 2

Equivalent Relevant Factors.

Question

Are conditions equivalent when every factor relevant to the evaluation remains equivalent?

Evaluation

Irrelevant differences should not alter evaluative equivalence.

Current evidence supports this hypothesis.

Result

Supported.

---

# Candidate 3

Equivalent Outcomes.

Question

Are conditions equivalent whenever they produce the same assessment?

Evaluation

Equivalent outcomes may arise accidentally.

Outcome agreement alone does not establish condition equivalence.

Result

Rejected.

---

# Pattern Analysis

Condition equivalence depends upon preserving every factor capable of affecting the evaluation.

Differences lacking evaluative significance do not destroy equivalence.

---

# Emerging Hypothesis

Two evaluation conditions are equivalent if every evaluatively relevant component of those conditions is equivalent.

---

# Observation

Condition equivalence is relative to the evaluation being performed.

A factor relevant to one evaluation may be irrelevant to another.

Condition equivalence is therefore context-dependent rather than absolute.

---

# Consequences

If accepted:

Reliability and reproducibility become formally definable.

Equivalent conditions no longer require complete identity.

The role of evaluative relevance becomes foundational within FARE.

---

# Remaining Questions

Future investigations should determine:

- how evaluative relevance is established;
- whether condition equivalence admits degrees;
- whether equivalence is transitive;
- whether condition equivalence can be formally proven.

---

# Current Status

The investigation remains active.

Current evidence supports treating condition equivalence as the preservation of all evaluatively relevant conditions rather than complete identity.
