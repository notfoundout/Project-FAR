# FARE Discovery Investigation

## Identifier

FARE-026

---

# Title

Consistency

---

## Status

In Progress

---

# Purpose

Determine the necessary and sufficient conditions under which an assessment is consistent.

The objective is to establish consistency as a formal assessment property within the Formal Architecture of Reasoning Evaluation.

---

# Motivation

Previous investigations established:

- validity;
- justification;
- admissibility;
- completeness;
- robustness.

An assessment may satisfy each of these while containing internally incompatible conclusions or supporting structures.

This investigation examines that distinction.

---

# Central Question

What makes an assessment consistent?

---

# Candidate 1

Validity.

Question

Is consistency identical to validity?

Evaluation

Validity concerns the correctness of evaluation.

Consistency concerns compatibility among the components of an assessment.

The concepts are distinct.

Result

Rejected.

---

# Candidate 2

Truth.

Question

Must every consistent assessment be true?

Evaluation

Consistency concerns internal coherence.

Truth concerns correspondence with reality.

The concepts are independent.

Result

Rejected.

---

# Candidate 3

Internal Compatibility.

Question

Is an assessment consistent when its components can coexist without contradiction?

Evaluation

Current evidence supports this hypothesis.

Consistency appears to concern relationships among the internal components of an assessment.

Result

Supported.

---

# Pattern Analysis

Every investigated consistent assessment possesses:

- compatible conclusions;
- compatible justification;
- compatible evaluation criteria;
- compatible assumptions.

Contradictions reduce consistency.

---

# Emerging Hypothesis

Consistency is the property of an assessment whose components can coexist without internal contradiction.

---

# Observation

Consistency does not imply:

- validity;
- completeness;
- robustness;
- justification.

Likewise, those properties do not guarantee consistency.

Consistency therefore represents an independent assessment property.

---

# Consequences

If accepted:

Project FAR distinguishes:

- correctness;
- support;
- eligibility;
- coverage;
- stability;
- internal coherence.

Each becomes an independent assessment property.

# Remaining Questions

Future investigations should determine:

- whether consistency admits degrees;
- whether local inconsistencies differ from global inconsistencies;
- whether consistency may be formally proven;
- whether consistency contributes directly to confidence.

---

# Current Status

The investigation remains active.

Current evidence supports treating consistency as the internal compatibility of an assessment's components.
