# FARE Discovery Investigation

## Identifier

FARE-020

---

# Title

Justification

---

## Status

In Progress

---

# Purpose

Determine the necessary and sufficient conditions under which an assessment possesses justification.

The objective is to establish justification as an assessment dimension within the Formal Architecture of Reasoning Evaluation.

---

# Motivation

Previous investigations established:

- evaluation;
- assessment;
- assessment structure;
- validity.

Assessments frequently include supporting information explaining why they should be accepted.

This investigation examines that support.

---

# Central Question

What justifies an assessment?

---

# Candidate 1

Evidence.

Question

Is justification identical to evidence?

Evaluation

Evidence contributes to justification.

Evidence alone does not explain how it supports an assessment.

Result

Rejected.

---

# Candidate 2

Reasoning.

Question

Is justification identical to reasoning?

Evaluation

Reasoning explains relationships.

Reasoning alone may occur without supporting a particular assessment.

Result

Rejected.

---

# Candidate 3

Explicit Support.

Question

Is justification the explicit support connecting an assessment to its underlying information and reasoning?

Evaluation

This explanation accommodates:

- evidence;
- logical reasoning;
- mathematical proof;
- methodological arguments;
- operational derivations.

Result

Supported.

---

# Pattern Analysis

Every investigated justified assessment possesses:

- an assessment;
- supporting information;
- explicit reasoning connecting that information to the assessment.

Removing the supporting connection eliminates the justification.

---

# Emerging Hypothesis

Justification is the explicit support connecting an assessment to the information and reasoning from which it is derived.

---

# Observation

Justification concerns support.

Validity concerns correctness.

The two concepts remain independent.

A valid assessment may possess weak justification.

An invalid assessment may possess extensive but defective justification.

---

# Consequences

If accepted:

Assessments become explicitly reviewable.

Independent investigators may evaluate not only assessment outcomes but also their supporting justification.

Justification becomes a reusable component throughout Project FAR.

The investigation remains active.
