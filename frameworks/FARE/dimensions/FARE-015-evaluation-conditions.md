# FARE Discovery Investigation

## Identifier

FARE-015

---

# Title

Evaluation Conditions

---

## Status

In Progress

---

# Purpose

Determine the role of evaluation conditions within the evaluation process.

The objective is to establish the conditions under which assessments may be meaningfully compared.

---

# Motivation

Previous investigations defined:

- reliability;
- reproducibility.

Both concepts require evaluation under equivalent conditions.

The concept of evaluation conditions has not yet been investigated.

---

# Central Question

What constitutes an evaluation condition?

---

# Candidate 1

Environment.

Question

Are evaluation conditions merely the surrounding environment?

Evaluation

Environmental factors may influence evaluation.

They do not completely specify the evaluation.

Result

Rejected.

---

# Candidate 2

Evaluation Context.

Question

Do evaluation conditions consist of every factor capable of influencing the evaluation?

Evaluation

This includes:

- evaluation object;
- evaluation criteria;
- evaluation procedure;
- available information;
- evaluator assumptions.

Current evidence supports this interpretation.

Result

Supported.

---

# Candidate 3

Methodology Only.

Question

Are evaluation conditions determined solely by methodology?

Evaluation

Methodology is necessary.

However, identical methodology applied to different evidence or objects constitutes different conditions.

Result

Rejected.

---

# Pattern Analysis

Every investigated evaluation depends upon a collection of identifiable conditions.

Changing any relevant condition may alter the resulting assessment.

---

# Emerging Hypothesis

Evaluation conditions are the complete collection of factors capable of influencing an evaluation.

---

# Candidate Components

Evaluation conditions may include:

- evaluation object;
- evaluation criteria;
- evaluation procedure;
- available evidence;
- assumptions;
- evaluator state;
- environmental constraints.

The necessity of each component remains under investigation.

---

# Consequences

If accepted:

Reliability becomes:

Repeated evaluation under equivalent evaluation conditions.

Reproducibility becomes:

Independent evaluation under equivalent evaluation conditions.

The concept of equivalent conditions becomes formally investigable.

---

# Remaining Questions

Future investigations should determine:

- which conditions are essential;
- how condition equivalence is established;
- whether conditions possess formal representations;
- whether conditions admit partial equivalence.

---

# Current Status

The investigation remains active.

Current evidence supports treating evaluation conditions as the collection of factors influencing an evaluation.
