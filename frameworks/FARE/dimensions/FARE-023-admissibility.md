# FARE Discovery Investigation

## Identifier

FARE-023

---

# Title

Admissibility

---

## Status

In Progress

---

# Purpose

Determine the necessary and sufficient conditions under which an assessment is admissible within a reasoning system.

The objective is to establish admissibility as a formal assessment property distinct from validity and justification.

---

# Motivation

Previous investigations established:

- validity;
- justification;
- reliability;
- reproducibility.

An assessment may be valid and justified while nevertheless being unsuitable for use within a particular reasoning process.

This investigation examines that distinction.

---

# Central Question

What makes an assessment admissible?

---

# Candidate 1

Validity.

Question

Is every valid assessment admissible?

Evaluation

Validity concerns correct evaluation.

A reasoning system may impose additional requirements before permitting an assessment to participate.

Result

Rejected.

---

# Candidate 2

Justification.

Question

Is every justified assessment admissible?

Evaluation

Justification provides support.

Support alone does not determine whether an assessment satisfies the requirements of a reasoning system.

Result

Rejected.

---

# Candidate 3

Acceptance.

Question

Is admissibility determined by acceptance?

Evaluation

Acceptance is a decision.

Admissibility concerns eligibility.

The concepts are distinct.

Result

Rejected.

---

# Candidate 4

Rule Satisfaction.

Question

Is an assessment admissible when it satisfies the requirements governing participation within a reasoning system?

Evaluation

This definition applies uniformly across:

- proofs;
- investigations;
- reasoning processes;
- framework evaluation.

Result

Supported.

---

# Pattern Analysis

Every investigated instance of admissibility depends upon:

- an assessment;
- a reasoning context;
- explicit admissibility requirements.

Changing the governing requirements may change admissibility without changing the assessment.

---

# Emerging Hypothesis

Admissibility is the property of satisfying the requirements governing participation within a specified reasoning context.

---

# Observation

Admissibility is context-dependent.

An assessment may be:

- admissible in one reasoning system;
- inadmissible in another.

The assessment itself need not change.

---

# Consequences

If accepted:

Project FAR distinguishes:

- correctness (validity);
- support (justification);
- eligibility (admissibility).

These become independent assessment properties.

# Remaining Questions

Future investigations should determine:

- whether admissibility admits degrees;
- whether admissibility depends upon methodology;
- whether admissibility is preserved under assessment revision;
- whether admissibility may be formally proven.

---

# Current Status

The investigation remains active.

Current evidence supports treating admissibility as the property of satisfying the participation requirements of a reasoning system.
