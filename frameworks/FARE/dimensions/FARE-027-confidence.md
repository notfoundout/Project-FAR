# FARE Discovery Investigation

## Identifier

FARE-027

---

# Title

Confidence

---

## Status

In Progress

---

# Purpose

Determine the necessary and sufficient conditions under which confidence may be assigned to an assessment.

The objective is to establish confidence as a derived assessment property within the Formal Architecture of Reasoning Evaluation.

---

# Motivation

Previous investigations established the following assessment properties:

- validity;
- justification;
- admissibility;
- completeness;
- robustness;
- consistency.

Confidence appears related to these properties.

This investigation determines whether confidence is primitive or derived.

---

# Central Question

What determines confidence?

---

# Candidate 1

Confidence is Primitive.

Question

Is confidence an independent assessment property?

Evaluation

Current evidence suggests confidence depends upon previously established evaluative properties.

Result

Not Supported.

---

# Candidate 2

Confidence is Belief.

Question

Is confidence merely the evaluator's belief?

Evaluation

Belief may differ between evaluators despite identical assessments.

Confidence should depend upon explicitly evaluable properties rather than subjective opinion.

Result

Rejected.

---

# Candidate 3

Confidence is Derived.

Question

Is confidence determined by the combined evaluation of previously established assessment properties?

Evaluation

Current evidence supports this hypothesis.

Assessments possessing stronger:

- validity;
- justification;
- consistency;
- completeness;
- robustness;

generally warrant greater confidence.

Result

Supported.

---

# Pattern Analysis

Confidence does not introduce a new evaluative property.

Instead, it summarizes previously established evaluative information.

Changes to underlying assessment properties may alter confidence without changing the assessment itself.

---

# Emerging Hypothesis

Confidence is a derived assessment property representing the degree to which an assessment is warranted by its supporting evaluative properties.

---

# Observation

Confidence depends upon evaluation.

Evaluation does not depend upon confidence.

Confidence therefore occupies a higher level within the assessment architecture.

---

# Consequences

If accepted:

Confidence becomes the principal synthesized assessment property.

Future assessment properties may contribute to confidence without redefining it.

Confidence no longer serves as an independent primitive.

---

# Relationship to Previous Investigations

Assessment

---

# Remaining Questions

Future investigations should determine:

- whether confidence admits quantitative representation;
- whether confidence aggregation possesses formal rules;
- whether different assessment properties contribute equally to confidence;
- whether confidence itself may be evaluated.

---

# Current Status

The investigation remains active.

Current evidence supports treating confidence as a derived assessment property synthesizing previously established evaluative properties.
