# FARE Discovery Investigation

## Identifier

FARE-014

---

# Title

Reproducibility

---

## Status

In Progress

---

# Purpose

Determine the necessary and sufficient conditions under which an evaluation is reproducible.

The objective is to distinguish reproducibility from reliability within the Formal Architecture of Reasoning Evaluation.

---

# Motivation

Previous investigations established reliability as the repeatability of an evaluation process under equivalent conditions.

Reproducibility appears to concern agreement among independent evaluators rather than repeated execution by the same evaluator.

This investigation examines that distinction.

---

# Central Question

What makes an evaluation reproducible?

---

# Candidate 1

Reliability.

Question

Is reproducibility identical to reliability?

Evaluation

Reliability concerns repeated execution of an evaluation process.

Reproducibility concerns independent execution.

These are distinct.

Result

Rejected.

---

# Candidate 2

Agreement.

Question

Is reproducibility merely agreement?

Evaluation

Agreement without equivalent methodology or criteria may occur by coincidence.

Agreement alone is insufficient.

Result

Rejected.

---

# Candidate 3

Independent Replication.

Question

Is an evaluation reproducible when independent evaluators applying equivalent evaluation procedures under equivalent conditions produce equivalent assessments?

Evaluation

Current evidence supports this hypothesis.

Result

Supported.

---

# Pattern Analysis

Every investigated instance of reproducibility requires:

- independent evaluators;
- equivalent evaluation objects;
- equivalent evaluation criteria;
- equivalent evaluation procedures;
- comparison of resulting assessments.

---

# Emerging Hypothesis

Reproducibility is the property of an evaluation whereby independent evaluators applying equivalent evaluation procedures under equivalent conditions produce equivalent assessments.

---

# Observation

Reliability concerns repeated execution.

Reproducibility concerns independent execution.

An evaluation may be:

- reliable but not reproducible;
- reproducible but not reliable;
- both;
- neither.

---

# Consequences

If accepted:

Project FAR distinguishes evaluator dependence from process dependence.

Reproducibility becomes a process property independent of validity and reliability.

---

# Remaining Questions

Future investigations should determine:

- whether reproducibility admits quantitative measurement;
- whether evaluator expertise affects reproducibility;
- whether reproducibility requires identical methodology;
- whether partial reproducibility admits formal characterization.

---

# Current Status

The investigation remains active.

Current evidence supports treating reproducibility as independent replication of equivalent assessments rather than repeated execution of the same evaluation.
