# FARE Discovery Investigation

## Identifier

FARE-013

---

# Title

Reliability

---

## Status

In Progress

---

# Purpose

Determine the necessary and sufficient conditions under which an evaluation process is reliable.

The objective is to establish reliability as an assessment dimension distinct from validity.

---

# Motivation

Previous investigations established validity as the correctness of an individual assessment relative to explicit evaluation criteria.

Reliability appears to concern repeated evaluation rather than individual assessment.

This investigation examines that distinction.

---

# Central Question

What makes an evaluation reliable?

---

# Candidate 1

Validity.

Question

Is reliability identical to validity?

Evaluation

An isolated valid assessment does not establish that repeated evaluations will produce equivalent results.

Reliability therefore concerns something different.

Result

Rejected.

---

# Candidate 2

Truth.

Question

Is reliability determined by truth?

Evaluation

True conclusions may result from unreliable evaluation.

Reliable evaluation may produce incorrect conclusions if supplied with defective evidence.

Truth and reliability remain distinct.

Result

Rejected.

---

# Candidate 3

Repeatability.

Question

Is an evaluation reliable when repeated applications under equivalent conditions consistently produce equivalent assessments?

Evaluation

Current evidence supports this hypothesis.

Reliability concerns stability across repeated evaluation.

Result

Supported.

---

# Pattern Analysis

Every investigated instance of reliability involves:

- repeated evaluations;
- equivalent evaluation conditions;
- equivalent evaluation criteria;
- comparison of resulting assessments.

Reliability therefore concerns consistency across evaluations rather than correctness of any individual evaluation.

---

# Emerging Hypothesis

Reliability is the property of an evaluation process that consistently produces equivalent assessments under equivalent evaluation conditions.

---

# Observation

Validity concerns:

one assessment.

Reliability concerns:

many assessments.

The two dimensions are related but independent.

A process may be:

- valid but unreliable;
- reliable but invalid;
- both valid and reliable;
- neither valid nor reliable.

---

# Consequences

If accepted:

Reliability becomes a property of evaluation processes.

Validity remains a property of individual assessments.

Project FAR explicitly distinguishes correctness from repeatability.

---

# Remaining Questions

Future investigations should determine:

- whether reliability admits quantitative measurement;
- whether reliability depends upon evaluator identity;
- whether reliability requires deterministic evaluation;
- whether reliability possesses upper bounds.

---

# Current Status

The investigation remains active.

Current evidence supports treating reliability as the repeatability of evaluation rather than the correctness of individual assessments.
