# FARE Discovery Investigation

## Identifier

FARE-008

---

# Title

Assessment Identity

---

## Status

In Progress

---

# Purpose

Determine the necessary and sufficient conditions under which two assessments are considered identical.

The objective is to establish the identity conditions governing assessments.

---

# Motivation

Previous investigations established the structure of assessments.

Before introducing specialized assessment dimensions, it is necessary to determine what constitutes assessment identity.

---

# Central Question

When do two assessments represent the same assessment?

---

# Candidate 1

Identical Result.

Question

If two assessments produce the same result, are they identical?

Evaluation

Different criteria may produce identical results.

Different objects may produce identical results.

The result alone is insufficient.

Result

Rejected.

---

# Candidate 2

Identical Object.

Question

If two assessments evaluate the same object, are they identical?

Evaluation

The same object may be evaluated under different criteria.

Result

Rejected.

---

# Candidate 3

Identical Criteria.

Question

If two assessments apply the same criteria, are they identical?

Evaluation

The same criteria may be applied to different objects.

Result

Rejected.

---

# Candidate 4

Identical Structure.

Question

Are two assessments identical only if they possess identical:

- evaluation object;
- evaluation criteria;
- assessment result;
- assessment dimensions?

Evaluation

Current evidence supports this hypothesis.

Result

Supported.

---

# Pattern Analysis

Assessment identity appears to depend upon the complete evaluative structure rather than any individual component.

Changing any structural component produces a distinct assessment.

---

# Emerging Hypothesis

Two assessments are identical if and only if they possess identical evaluative structures.

---

# Observation

Assessment identity is independent of correctness.

An incorrect assessment remains a distinct assessment.

Validity concerns quality.

Identity concerns sameness.

---

# Consequences

If accepted:

- assessments become uniquely identifiable objects;
- assessment comparison becomes formally defined;
- assessment revision may preserve or replace identity;
- assessment versioning becomes investigable.

---

# Remaining Questions

Future investigations should determine:

- whether assessment identity survives revision;
- whether assessments possess canonical representations;
- whether assessment equivalence differs from assessment identity;
- whether partial assessment similarity admits formal characterization.

---

# Current Status

The investigation remains active.

Current evidence supports structural identity as the basis of assessment identity.
