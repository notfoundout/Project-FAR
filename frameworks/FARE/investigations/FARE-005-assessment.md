# FARE Discovery Investigation

## Identifier

FARE-005

---

# Title

Assessment

---

## Status

In Progress

---

# Purpose

Determine the nature of assessment.

The objective is to establish what an assessment is and how it relates to evaluation.

---

# Motivation

Previous investigations established that evaluation consists of:

- an evaluation object;
- one or more evaluation criteria;
- a comparison.

This investigation examines the resulting assessment.

---

# Central Question

What is an assessment?

---

# Candidate 1

Assessment is a decision.

Question

Is every assessment a decision?

Evaluation

Many assessments inform decisions without themselves being decisions.

Result

Rejected.

---

# Candidate 2

Assessment is a conclusion.

Question

Is every assessment a conclusion?

Evaluation

Some assessments produce intermediate results rather than final conclusions.

Result

Partially Supported.

---

# Candidate 3

Assessment is the recorded outcome of evaluation.

Question

Does every evaluation produce an assessment?

Evaluation

Every investigated evaluation produces an explicit or implicit outcome.

The assessment records that outcome.

Result

Supported.

---

# Candidate 4

Assessment changes the evaluated object.

Question

Must assessment modify the object?

Evaluation

Evaluation may leave the object unchanged while producing new information.

Assessment concerns knowledge rather than transformation.

Result

Rejected.

---

# Pattern Analysis

Every investigated assessment possesses:

- an evaluated object;
- one or more evaluation criteria;
- a comparison;
- a recorded outcome.

The assessment represents the outcome of the comparison.

---

# Emerging Hypothesis

An assessment is the explicit representation of the outcome produced by an evaluation.

The assessment records the evaluative result without modifying the evaluated object.

---

# Candidate Taxonomy

Assessments may be:

- binary;
- ordinal;
- categorical;
- numerical;
- probabilistic;
- qualitative.

The assessment format depends upon the evaluation criteria rather than the evaluation process.

---

# Observation

Assessments themselves appear capable of becoming evaluation objects.

An assessment may therefore participate in subsequent evaluations.

This suggests recursive evaluation.

---

# Consequences

If accepted:

Validity becomes a type of assessment.

Reliability becomes a type of assessment.

Confidence becomes a type of assessment.

Admissibility becomes a type of assessment.

Framework quality becomes a type of assessment.

FARE becomes a unified theory of assessment rather than a collection of independent evaluative concepts.

---

# Remaining Questions

Future investigations should determine:

- whether assessments possess formal structure;
- whether assessments admit composition;
- whether assessments possess confidence;
- whether assessments may conflict;
- whether assessments require justification.

---

# Current Status

The investigation remains active.

Current evidence supports treating assessment as the explicit outcome of evaluation.
