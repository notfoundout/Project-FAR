# FARE Discovery Investigation

## Identifier

FARE-001

---

# Title

What Is Evaluation?

---

## Status

In Progress

---

# Purpose

Determine the necessary and sufficient conditions under which an activity constitutes evaluation.

The objective is to establish the foundational concept upon which the remainder of FARE will be constructed.

---

# Motivation

Project FAR distinguishes:

- representation (FARA),
- operation (FARO),
- methodology (FARM).

The remaining foundational concept is evaluation.

Before validity, reliability, justification, or confidence can be investigated, evaluation itself must be defined.

---

# Central Question

What is evaluation?

---

# Candidate 1

Evaluation is assigning a value.

Question

Does every evaluation assign a value?

Evaluation

Many evaluations classify rather than assign numerical values.

Result

Rejected.

---

# Candidate 2

Evaluation is making a judgment.

Question

Is every judgment an evaluation?

Evaluation

Judgments may occur without explicit criteria.

Evaluation appears to require more than judgment alone.

Result

Rejected.

---

# Candidate 3

Evaluation is comparison against criteria.

Question

Does every evaluation compare something against one or more criteria?

Evaluation

Examples include:

- proof against validity;
- reasoning against admissibility;
- conclusions against evidence;
- frameworks against objectives.

Every investigated evaluation appears to compare an object against explicit or implicit criteria.

Result

Supported.

---

# Candidate 4

Evaluation is classification.

Question

Is every evaluation a classification?

Evaluation

Classification is one possible outcome of evaluation.

Evaluation itself appears more general.

Result

Rejected.

---

# Pattern Analysis

Every investigated evaluation possesses:

- an object;
- one or more criteria;
- a comparison;
- a resulting assessment.

The assessment may be:

- binary;
- ordinal;
- quantitative;
- qualitative.

The form of assessment varies.

The underlying comparison remains constant.

---

# Emerging Hypothesis

Evaluation is the comparison of an object against one or more criteria for the purpose of producing an assessment.

---

# Consequences

If accepted:

Validity becomes evaluation against validity criteria.

Reliability becomes evaluation against reliability criteria.

Justification becomes evaluation against justificatory criteria.

Confidence becomes evaluation against confidence criteria.

The remainder of FARE becomes a specialization of one general evaluative process.

---

# Remaining Questions

Future investigations should determine:

- what constitutes a criterion;
- whether every evaluation requires explicit criteria;
- whether criteria admit formal representation;
- whether evaluation possesses formal algebraic properties.

---

# Current Status

The investigation remains active.

Current evidence supports treating evaluation as comparison against explicit or implicit criteria resulting in an assessment.
