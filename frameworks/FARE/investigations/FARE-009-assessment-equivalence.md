# FARE Discovery Investigation

## Identifier

FARE-009

---

# Title

Assessment Equivalence

---

## Status

In Progress

---

# Purpose

Determine the necessary and sufficient conditions under which two distinct assessments are considered evaluatively equivalent.

The objective is to distinguish assessment identity from assessment equivalence.

---

# Motivation

The previous investigation established the concept of assessment identity.

Two assessments may be distinct while nevertheless expressing equivalent evaluative content.

This investigation examines that distinction.

---

# Central Question

When are two assessments equivalent?

---

# Candidate 1

Identical Assessments.

Question

Are equivalent assessments simply identical assessments?

Evaluation

Identity is sufficient for equivalence.

Whether it is necessary remains unresolved.

Result

Rejected.

Identity appears stronger than equivalence.

---

# Candidate 2

Identical Results.

Question

Are two assessments equivalent whenever they produce the same result?

Evaluation

Identical results may arise from:

- different criteria;
- different methodologies;
- different evaluation objects.

Result

Rejected.

---

# Candidate 3

Equivalent Evaluative Meaning.

Question

Are two assessments equivalent when they communicate the same evaluative content despite structural differences?

Evaluation

Current evidence supports this possibility.

Equivalent assessments need not possess identical structures.

Result

Supported.

---

# Pattern Analysis

Assessment identity concerns sameness.

Assessment equivalence concerns evaluative meaning.

Identity therefore implies equivalence.

Equivalence does not imply identity.

---

# Emerging Hypothesis

Two assessments are evaluatively equivalent if they communicate the same evaluative content, even when produced through different evaluative structures.

---

# Observation

Equivalent assessments may differ in:

- representation;
- justification;
- methodology;
- structure.

They agree only in evaluative meaning.

---

# Consequences

If accepted:

Assessment comparison becomes more expressive.

Independent investigations may converge upon equivalent assessments.

Assessment revision may preserve equivalence while changing identity.

---

# Relationship to Previous Investigations

Assessment

├── Structure

├── Identity

└── Equivalence

Identity determines sameness.

Equivalence determines evaluative correspondence.

---

# Remaining Questions

Future investigations should determine:

- whether assessment equivalence admits degrees;
- whether equivalence is transitive;
- whether equivalent assessments require compatible criteria;
- whether equivalence may be formally proven.

---

# Current Status

The investigation remains active.

Current evidence supports distinguishing assessment identity from assessment equivalence.
