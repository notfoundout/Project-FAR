# FARE Discovery Investigation

## Identifier

FARE-010

---

# Title

Assessment Revision

---

## Status

In Progress

---

# Purpose

Determine the conditions under which an assessment may be revised while preserving or changing its identity.

The objective is to establish the formal theory governing assessment evolution.

---

# Motivation

Previous investigations established:

- assessment structure;
- assessment identity;
- assessment equivalence.

Assessments are expected to evolve as investigations progress.

This investigation determines how assessment revision should be represented.

---

# Central Question

What constitutes a revision of an assessment?

---

# Candidate 1

Any Change.

Question

Does every modification constitute a revision?

Evaluation

Minor formatting or representational changes may not alter evaluative content.

Result

Rejected.

---

# Candidate 2

Result Change.

Question

Does changing the assessment result constitute revision?

Evaluation

Changing the assessment outcome alters the evaluative content.

Result

Supported.

---

# Candidate 3

Criterion Change.

Question

Does changing the evaluation criteria constitute revision?

Evaluation

Different criteria produce a different evaluation.

The resulting assessment must therefore be reconsidered.

Result

Supported.

---

# Candidate 4

Justification Change.

Question

Does modifying the supporting justification constitute revision?

Evaluation

Strengthening, correcting, or replacing justification changes the assessment record even when the assessment result remains unchanged.

Result

Supported.

---

# Pattern Analysis

Assessments evolve through changes to:

- criteria;
- justification;
- result;
- dimensions.

Not every representational modification constitutes revision.

Only substantive evaluative changes qualify.

---

# Emerging Hypothesis

Assessment revision is the process of modifying one or more substantive components of an assessment while preserving an explicit historical relationship to the previous assessment.

---

# Observation

Revision does not necessarily preserve identity.

Some revisions create:

- new versions of the same assessment.

Others create:

- entirely new assessments.

The distinction remains under investigation.

---

# Consequences

If accepted:

Assessments become versioned objects.

Investigations gain explicit evaluative history.

Assessment evolution becomes formally traceable.

# Remaining Questions

Future investigations should determine:

- when revision preserves identity;
- whether revisions admit branching;
- whether revisions form directed graphs;
- whether revision history possesses canonical representation.

---

# Current Status

The investigation remains active.

Current evidence supports treating assessment revision as the formal evolution of evaluative structures.
