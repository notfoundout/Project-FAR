# FARE Discovery Investigation

## Identifier

FARE-011

---

# Title

Assessment Composition

---

## Status

In Progress

---

# Purpose

Determine whether multiple assessments may be formally composed into a single assessment.

The objective is to establish the compositional properties of assessments.

---

# Motivation

Previous investigations established:

- assessment structure;
- assessment identity;
- assessment equivalence;
- assessment revision.

Complex investigations frequently produce multiple assessments.

This investigation determines whether those assessments may themselves become components of larger assessments.

---

# Central Question

Can multiple assessments be composed into a single assessment?

---

# Candidate 1

Independent Assessments.

Question

Must every assessment remain independent?

Evaluation

Complex evaluations frequently require integrating multiple assessments.

Result

Rejected.

---

# Candidate 2

Aggregation.

Question

Can multiple assessments simply be aggregated?

Evaluation

Aggregation preserves multiple assessments.

It does not necessarily produce a new assessment.

Result

Partially Supported.

---

# Candidate 3

Composition.

Question

Can multiple assessments together constitute a new assessment?

Evaluation

A framework evaluation, for example, may depend upon assessments of:

- consistency;
- completeness;
- reconstructibility;
- reliability.

These combine into a higher-order assessment.

Result

Supported.

---

# Pattern Analysis

Assessment composition appears to require:

- two or more component assessments;
- an explicit composition procedure;
- a resulting assessment.

The resulting assessment possesses its own identity.

---

# Emerging Hypothesis

Assessment composition is the construction of a new assessment from one or more existing assessments according to explicitly specified composition rules.

---

# Observation

Component assessments remain independently identifiable.

Composition creates an additional assessment rather than replacing its components.

---

# Consequences

If accepted:

Assessments become recursively composable.

Large investigations may be represented as assessment hierarchies.

Framework evaluation becomes a composition of lower-level assessments.

# Remaining Questions

Future investigations should determine:

- whether assessment composition is associative;
- whether composition preserves equivalence;
- whether composed assessments admit decomposition;
- whether assessment composition forms directed graphs.

The investigation remains active.

Current evidence supports treating assessment composition as the recursive construction of higher-order assessments.
