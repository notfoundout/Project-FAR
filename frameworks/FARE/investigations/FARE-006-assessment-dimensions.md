# FARE Discovery Investigation

## Identifier

FARE-006

---

# Title

Assessment Dimensions

---

## Status

In Progress

---

# Purpose

Determine whether assessments possess independent evaluative dimensions.

The objective is to establish whether concepts such as validity, reliability, and confidence are distinct forms of assessment or dimensions of a common assessment architecture.

---

# Motivation

Previous investigations established that evaluation produces an assessment.

Multiple evaluative concepts remain under investigation, including:

- validity;
- reliability;
- confidence;
- justification;
- admissibility.

This investigation determines how these concepts relate.

---

# Central Question

Are validity, reliability, confidence, and related concepts independent kinds of assessment?

Or are they dimensions along which assessments may be evaluated?

---

# Candidate Model 1

Independent Assessment Types.

Question

Is validity fundamentally different from reliability?

Evaluation

Each appears to describe one aspect of an assessment.

They frequently coexist.

Result

Not Supported.

---

# Candidate Model 2

Assessment Dimensions.

Question

Can a single assessment possess:

- validity;
- reliability;
- confidence;
- justification?

Evaluation

Yes.

A single assessment may be evaluated simultaneously along multiple dimensions.

Result

Supported.

---

# Pattern Analysis

Every investigated assessment may possess multiple independent characteristics.

No investigated dimension excludes another.

The dimensions appear orthogonal.

---

# Emerging Hypothesis

An assessment consists of one or more independent evaluative dimensions.

Each dimension measures a different property of the assessment.

---

# Candidate Dimensions

Current candidates include:

- validity;
- reliability;
- justification;
- confidence;
- admissibility;
- completeness;
- robustness;
- consistency.

Additional dimensions may be identified through future investigations.

---

# Observation

Not every assessment requires every dimension.

The applicable dimensions depend upon:

- the evaluation object;
- the evaluation criteria;
- the evaluation objective.

---

# Consequences

If accepted:

Future FARE investigations become investigations of individual assessment dimensions.

The architecture remains stable while dimensions expand independently.

---

# Remaining Questions

Future investigations should determine:

- whether dimensions are independent;
- whether dimensions admit formal dependency;
- whether dimensions possess quantitative representations;
- whether dimensions possess universal ordering.

---

# Current Status

The investigation remains active.

Current evidence supports treating validity, reliability, confidence, and related concepts as assessment dimensions rather than independent assessment types.
