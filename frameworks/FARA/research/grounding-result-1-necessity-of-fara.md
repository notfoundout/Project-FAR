# Grounding Result 1 — Necessity of FARA

## Status

Research

## Question

Can an explicit reasoning investigation be represented without FARA-level structure?

## Objective

Ground FARA by testing whether explicit reasoning can be represented, inspected, compared, audited, and revised without the roles FARA introduces.

## Result

An explicit reasoning investigation requires at least the following functional roles:

1. Investigation
2. Representation
3. Representational Structure
4. Interpretation
5. Reasoning State
6. Transition
7. Candidate
8. Admissibility
9. Resolution

If any role can be eliminated without loss, FARA must be reduced.

This result does not prove that every role is primitive.

It only establishes that the roles are functionally necessary for explicit reasoning investigation.
