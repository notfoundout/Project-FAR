# Representation Theorem — Proof Roadmap

## Status

Research

## Purpose

Convert the current representation theorem conjecture into either a formally proven theorem or a formally rejected conjecture.

## Phase 1 — Definitions

Define precisely:

- explicit
- reasoning investigation
- representation
- admissible representation
- auditable
- scope of Project FAR

No proof may proceed until these definitions are stable.

## Phase 2 — Lemmas

Establish and prove:

1. Representation Existence Lemma
2. Interpretation Association Lemma
3. State Representation Lemma
4. Transition Representation Lemma
5. Outcome Representation Lemma

Each lemma must be independently reviewable.

## Phase 3 — Counterexample Search

Attempt to falsify the conjecture using:

- incomplete investigations
- conflicting interpretations
- non-terminating investigations
- partially observable reasoning
- alternative reasoning calculi
- evolving objectives

Any successful counterexample must revise or reject the conjecture.

## Phase 4 — Main Proof

Only after the lemmas survive review should a complete proof attempt be assembled.

## Acceptance Criteria

The Representation Theorem shall be promoted from conjecture only if:

- all required definitions are stable;
- supporting lemmas are proven;
- no accepted counterexample defeats the theorem within its stated scope;
- every assumption is explicit.

Otherwise the conjecture remains open.
