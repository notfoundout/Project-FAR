# Representation Theorem Investigation

## Status

Research

## Question

Can every explicit reasoning investigation be represented within the FARA representational architecture?

## Objective

Determine the precise conditions under which FARA is representationally complete for its intended scope.

## Motivation

The grounding results establish that a representational architecture is necessary. They do not establish that FARA can represent every reasoning investigation within its intended domain.

The Representation Theorem seeks to prove or refute that claim.

## Target Statement

Every explicit reasoning investigation within the declared scope of Project FAR admits a representation in FARA.

## Proof Obligations

1. Precisely define the scope of 'explicit reasoning investigation'.
2. Specify what constitutes a valid FARA representation.
3. Construct a mapping from investigations to FARA representations.
4. Identify counterexamples, if any.
5. Determine whether additional primitives or derived structures are required.

## Success Criteria

A proof or a counterexample that establishes the representational limits of FARA.
