# Representation Theorem — Proof Attempt 1

## Status

Research / Proof Attempt

## Candidate Statement

Every explicit reasoning investigation within the declared scope of Project FAR admits a representation in FARA.

## Working Definition

An explicit reasoning investigation is a reasoning process whose objective, contents, semantic commitments, transformations, alternatives, evaluation criteria, and outcome status are available for inspection by another agent.

## Assumptions

1. The investigation is explicit.
2. The investigation is auditable.
3. The investigation lies within Project FAR's declared scope.

## Proof Sketch

Given an explicit reasoning investigation:

- its objective maps to Investigation;
- its reasoning objects map to Representations;
- its admissible object-space maps to Representational Structure;
- its meanings map to Interpretation;
- its current condition maps to Reasoning State;
- its permissible changes map to Transition;
- its possible outcomes map to Candidate;
- its evaluation criteria map to Admissibility;
- its terminal or unresolved condition maps to Resolution.

If every required feature of the investigation admits a corresponding FARA role, then the investigation admits a FARA representation.

## Outstanding Objection

This proof may rely on definitions that already align closely with the FARA architecture. Further work must determine whether the theorem remains valid under weaker, independently motivated definitions of explicit reasoning investigation.

## Current Conclusion

The proof is incomplete. The candidate theorem remains unproven pending analysis of stronger counterexamples and weaker input definitions.
