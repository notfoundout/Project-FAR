# Primitive Minimality Theorem Investigation

## Status

Research

## Question

Is the proposed FARA primitive set minimal while preserving the representational power required for explicit reasoning investigations?

## Objective

Determine whether the complete primitive architecture can be reduced without loss of expressive capability.

## Motivation

Primitive independence alone does not establish minimality. A set of independent primitives may still be unnecessarily large if the overall architecture admits a smaller equivalent basis.

## Proof Obligations

1. Define representational equivalence.
2. Define architectural minimality.
3. Construct candidate reduced primitive sets.
4. Compare their expressive power against the current architecture.
5. Identify any loss of representational capability.
6. Determine whether the current primitive architecture is minimal.

## Success Criteria

Either:

- prove that no smaller primitive architecture preserves the required representational power; or
- exhibit a smaller architecture that preserves all required capabilities, requiring revision of the current primitive set.
