# FARO Execution

## Purpose

This document defines execution operations within FARO.

Execution operations support the conduct of FAR investigations without replacing FAR methodology.

---

## Definition

Execution is the operational support of FAR workflow performance through explicit artifact creation, tracking, and organization.

Execution does not determine truth, admissibility, or resolution correctness.

---

## Permitted Execution Operations

Execution operations may:

- instantiate FAR workflow templates;
- track required investigation artifacts;
- mark stages complete, incomplete, or not applicable;
- collect revision records;
- collect closure status;
- prepare artifacts for audit, comparison, disagreement analysis, reporting, or evaluation.

---

## Prohibited Execution Operations

Execution operations shall not:

- redefine FAR workflow stages;
- remove required FAR artifacts;
- silently omit stages;
- replace investigator reasoning;
- determine admissibility unless explicitly acting under a defined FAR reasoning calculus.

---

## Required Inputs

Typical execution operations require:

- investigation objective;
- selected FAR workflow;
- initial artifacts or artifact template;
- stated reasoning calculus when applicable.

---

## Outputs

Execution operations produce explicit investigation artifacts or execution records.

---

## Boundary

Execution supports FAR methodology.

It does not modify FAR methodology.
