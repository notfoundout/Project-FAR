# Grounding Result 3 — Necessity of FARO

## Status

Research

## Question

Given FARA-level structure and FAR-level methodology, is a distinct operational layer required?

## Objective

Ground FARO by testing whether explicit reasoning investigations can be operationally executed, compared, audited, and verified using only FARA and FAR.

## Result

FARA supplies representational architecture.

FAR supplies investigative procedure.

Neither FARA nor FAR, by itself, supplies the operational rules required to execute, compare, audit, and verify reasoning processes as formal operations.

A distinct operational layer is required.

That layer is FARO.

## Distinction Between FARA, FAR, and FARO

FARA answers:

> What must exist for reasoning to be explicitly represented?

FAR answers:

> What must be done to conduct an investigation over those representations?

FARO answers:

> What operations are required to execute, compare, audit, and verify reasoning investigations?

Therefore:

- FARA provides the representational structure.
- FAR provides the procedural methodology.
- FARO provides the operational theory.

## Test Case

Assume a system already has FARA and FAR.

It can represent:

- investigations;
- representations;
- representational structures;
- interpretations;
- reasoning states;
- transitions;
- candidates;
- admissibility;
- resolution.

It can also conduct an investigation through a defined procedure.

Now attempt to perform the following operational tasks:

1. Compare two investigations.
2. Audit whether a transition was valid.
3. Verify whether a reasoning state was preserved under transformation.
4. Determine whether two disagreements differ structurally or semantically.
5. Track whether an investigation satisfies its own declared calculus.
6. Determine whether a resolution follows from admissible candidates.
7. Reconstruct a reasoning process as an ordered operation sequence.

FARA and FAR make these tasks representable and procedurally locatable.

They do not, by themselves, define the operations required to execute them.

## Elimination Tests

### 1. Remove Operational Rules

Without operational rules, the system can describe reasoning objects and procedural stages, but cannot determine how operations are performed on them.

Result:

The investigation can be represented and staged, but not formally operated on.

Operational rules are required.

### 2. Remove Comparison Operations

Without comparison operations, two reasoning states, candidates, interpretations, or investigations can be placed side by side but not formally compared.

Result:

The system cannot determine equivalence, difference, conflict, or structural divergence.

Comparison operations are required.

### 3. Remove Audit Operations

Without audit operations, the system cannot determine whether a transition, candidate selection, admissibility judgment, or resolution followed the declared rules of the investigation.

Result:

The investigation may be procedurally documented, but rule-compliance cannot be checked.

Audit operations are required.

### 4. Remove Verification Operations

Without verification operations, the system cannot determine whether claimed preservation, validity, admissibility, or resolution actually holds.

Result:

The system cannot distinguish asserted correctness from verified correctness.

Verification operations are required.

### 5. Remove Disagreement Operations

Without disagreement operations, the system cannot determine whether two conflicting results differ because of representations, interpretations, calculi, admissibility standards, or resolution rules.

Result:

Disagreement can be observed, but not operationally decomposed.

Disagreement operations are required.

### 6. Remove Reconstruction Operations

Without reconstruction operations, the system cannot rebuild an investigation as an ordered sequence of formally inspectable operations.

Result:

The investigation may have a history, but that history is not operationally reproducible.

Reconstruction operations are required.

## Conclusion

FARO is justified as the operational layer required to execute, compare, audit, verify, decompose, and reconstruct reasoning investigations.

Without FARO, FARA can represent reasoning and FAR can structure investigation, but the system lacks formal operations over reasoning processes.

This result does not establish FARO axioms, inference rules, propositions, theorems, or proofs.

It establishes only that an operational layer distinct from FARA and FAR is necessary.
