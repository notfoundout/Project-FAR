# FARO Operation Taxonomy

## Purpose

This document defines the canonical categories of FARO operations.

It does not define individual operations.

---

## Operation Categories

### Execution

Operations that support the conduct of FAR investigations.

Execution operations help instantiate, track, or structure FAR workflow artifacts without replacing FAR methodology.

---

### Audit

Operations that check investigation artifacts against FAR validation requirements.

Audit operations identify defects, omissions, inconsistencies, and reconstructibility problems.

---

### Comparison

Operations that identify similarities and differences between investigations or reasoning artifacts.

Comparison is descriptive unless paired with explicit evaluation criteria.

---

### Disagreement Analysis

Operations that locate and explain divergences between investigations.

Disagreement analysis traces causes and propagation of disagreement without assigning correctness.

---

### Reporting

Operations that produce structured summaries, audit records, comparison records, disagreement reports, or evaluation reports.

---

### Operational Evaluation

Operations that assess operational qualities of artifacts according to FAR-grounded criteria.

Operational evaluation does not determine truth unless the relevant investigation explicitly defines truth-evaluation criteria.

---

## Category Rule

Every FARO operation must belong to exactly one primary category.

An operation may cite secondary categories when necessary, but its primary category determines its interface and validation standard.
