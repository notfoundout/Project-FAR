# FARM Design Principles

## Purpose

This document defines design principles for FARM.

FARM is a meta-framework coordination layer, not a replacement for any stable Project FAR layer.

---

## Principle 1 — Coordinate, Do Not Redefine

FARM coordinates cross-framework requirements.

It shall not redefine FARA, FAR, FARO, or FARE.

---

## Principle 2 — Requirement-Driven Change

A stable-layer change may be proposed only when a concrete defect, inconsistency, or downstream requirement is recorded.

---

## Principle 3 — Explicit Routing

Every cross-framework requirement should be routed to a specific target layer.

Unrouted requirements remain incomplete.

---

## Principle 4 — Boundary Preservation

FARM must preserve the boundaries of stable layers.

Meta-framework convenience is not a valid reason to alter stable definitions, methodology, operations, or mathematics.

---

## Principle 5 — Defect Before Revision

A revision should identify the defect or requirement that motivates it before any stable-layer document is changed.

---

## Principle 6 — Integration Records

Cross-framework decisions should be recorded explicitly so future investigators can reconstruct why a change was proposed, accepted, rejected, or deferred.

---

## Principle 7 — No Catch-All Governance

FARM shall remain narrow.

Tasks already handled by project status, milestones, FARO audits, FAR methodology, FARA architecture, or FARE mathematics shall remain there.
