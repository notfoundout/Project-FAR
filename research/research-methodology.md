# Research Methodology

## Purpose

This document defines the principles governing the development of Project FAR.

Its purpose is to ensure that theoretical progress is systematic, transparent, and self-correcting.

---

# Guiding Principle

Project FAR seeks to discover the simplest architecture capable of representing reasoning within its stated scope.

The objective is not to defend existing formulations but to improve them.

---

# Development Process

Research should proceed according to the following cycle.

1. Identify a problem.
2. Formulate a proposed solution.
3. Analyze dependencies.
4. Search for hidden assumptions.
5. Attempt falsification.
6. Revise or reject the proposal.
7. Incorporate verified results into the formal theory.

---

# Preference Ordering

When competing formulations are available, preference should be given to the formulation that:

- Uses fewer primitive concepts.
- Requires fewer axioms.
- Preserves expressive power.
- Eliminates redundancy.
- Improves clarity.
- Increases generality.

---

# Burden of Proof

The burden of proof rests upon the introduction of:

- New primitive concepts.
- New axioms.
- New assumptions.
- Increased complexity.

Reduction requires less justification than expansion.

---

# Falsification

Every proposed result should be subjected to rigorous attempts at refutation.

Verification should seek reasons why a proposal might fail rather than reasons why it appears correct.

A proposal that survives repeated attempts at falsification earns increased confidence but is never regarded as beyond revision.

---

# Repository Policy

The repository distinguishes between:

- Established theory.
- Proposed theory.
- Active research.
- Deferred research.
- Historical development.

These categories should remain clearly separated.

---

# Revision Policy

Every component of Project FAR remains open to revision.

No definition, primitive concept, axiom, proposition, or theorem is immune from reconsideration if stronger theoretical results become available.

---

# Success Criterion

Progress is measured by improving the explanatory power, simplicity, internal consistency, and rigor of the framework rather than by preserving previous formulations.
