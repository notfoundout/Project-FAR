# FARO Discovery Investigation

## Identifier

FARO-022

---

# Title

Minimal Operational Validity

---

## Status

In Progress

---

# Purpose

Determine the minimal necessary condition for an operation to qualify as a reasoning operation.

The objective is to identify the most fundamental operational requirement without introducing unnecessary assumptions.

---

# Dependencies

- FARO-001 through FARO-021

---

# Central Question

What is the weakest condition that distinguishes a reasoning operation from an arbitrary operation?

---

# Background

Previous investigations proposed several candidate validity conditions.

Those conditions have not yet been shown to be fundamental.

This investigation seeks the smallest necessary criterion from which stronger conditions may later be derived.

---

# Candidate 1

An operation modifies a representation.

Evaluation:

Insufficient.

Many modifications of representations are arbitrary and have no reasoning significance.

---

# Candidate 2

An operation modifies a reasoning state.

Evaluation:

Insufficient.

A random alteration of a reasoning state does not necessarily constitute reasoning.

---

# Candidate 3

An operation preserves investigation identity.

Evaluation:

Insufficient.

Identity preservation alone does not distinguish reasoning from arbitrary manipulation.

---

# Candidate 4

An operation preserves logical correctness.

Evaluation:

Insufficient.

Incorrect reasoning remains reasoning.

Correctness therefore cannot define reasoning.

---

# Candidate 5

An operation is governed by an explicitly defined reasoning calculus.

Evaluation:

Promising.

An operation performed according to an explicit calculus is distinguishable from arbitrary change.

Whether this condition is sufficient remains unresolved.

---

# Observation

Several previously proposed validity conditions appear to concern the quality of reasoning rather than the existence of reasoning.

The existence of reasoning and the correctness of reasoning may therefore be distinct concepts.

---

# Emerging Hypothesis

The minimal requirement for a reasoning operation may be:

> The operation is performed according to an explicitly defined reasoning calculus.

If true, stronger concepts such as admissibility, traceability, reproducibility, and auditability become properties of particular calculi rather than defining properties of reasoning itself.

---

# Decision Criteria

If every reasoning operation necessarily presupposes a reasoning calculus, while arbitrary operations do not, the calculus requirement constitutes the minimal operational validity condition.

---

# Provisional Conclusion

No conclusion has been reached.

Current evidence suggests that explicit conformity to a reasoning calculus is the strongest candidate for the minimal defining property of a reasoning operation.

---

# Remaining Questions

Future investigations should determine:

- whether a reasoning calculus itself requires formal validity criteria;
- whether different calculi induce different notions of operational validity;
- whether reasoning can exist without an explicit calculus.

---

# Current Status

The investigation remains active.

The minimal condition for operational validity has not yet been established.
