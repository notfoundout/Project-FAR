# Central Question

Does every reasoning system necessarily possess an operational layer?

---

# Background

Previous investigations identified a coherent collection of operational responsibilities.

This investigation asks whether those responsibilities arise necessarily from reasoning itself or only from one particular design.

---

# Method

Consider multiple classes of reasoning systems.

Examples include:

- formal mathematical proof;
- scientific investigation;
- legal reasoning;
- diagnostic reasoning;
- software verification;
- everyday informal reasoning;
- artificial intelligence reasoning.

Determine whether each system requires operational execution in addition to architectural representation.

---

# Evaluation

## Case 1

Formal mathematics.

Question:

Does representing axioms and propositions alone produce proofs?

Result:

No.

A proof procedure is required.

Operational layer:

Required.

---

## Case 2

Scientific investigation.

Question:

Does representing observations and hypotheses alone produce conclusions?

Result:

No.

Evidence must be evaluated and investigations must proceed through explicit procedures.

Operational layer:

Required.

---

## Case 3

Legal reasoning.

Question:

Does representing statutes and evidence alone determine judicial conclusions?

Result:

No.

Procedures govern admissibility, burden of proof, and evaluation.

Operational layer:

Required.

---

## Case 4

Software verification.

Question:

Does representing program specifications alone establish correctness?

Result:

No.

Verification procedures must execute.

Operational layer:

Required.

---

## Case 5

Artificial intelligence reasoning.

Question:

Does representing knowledge alone produce reasoning?

Result:

No.

Operational layer:

Required.

---

# Pattern Analysis

Across every reasoning domain examined, two distinct components appear.

First, a structure describing what exists.

Second, procedures specifying how reasoning proceeds.

No investigated reasoning system consists solely of representations.

---

# Competing Hypotheses

## H1

Operational execution is implementation-specific.

Different reasoning systems may or may not possess an operational framework.

---

## H2

Operational execution is a universal requirement of reasoning.

Every reasoning system requires an operational framework acting upon its architectural structure.

---

# Evaluation

Every investigated reasoning domain requires operational execution.

The specific procedures differ.

The existence of operational execution does not.

This suggests that operationality is a universal property of reasoning rather than an implementation detail.

---

# Provisional Conclusion

Current evidence supports the hypothesis that every reasoning system possesses both:

- an architectural structure; and
- an operational structure.

Accordingly, an operational framework appears to be a universal requirement rather than an optional implementation choice.

If this conclusion continues to withstand investigation, FARO is not merely one reasoning engine.

It is the universal operational layer corresponding to the architectural layer defined by FARA.
