# FARO Discovery Investigation

## Identifier

FARO-024

---

# Title

Operational Regularity

---

## Status

In Progress

---

# Purpose

Determine whether operational regularity constitutes the most fundamental operational concept in reasoning.

The objective is to determine whether operational regularity is primitive or whether it can itself be reduced to more basic concepts.

---

# Dependencies

- FARO-001 through FARO-023

---

# Central Question

What is operational regularity?

---

# Background

The previous investigation challenged the necessity of explicitly defined reasoning calculi.

Current evidence suggests that reasoning may require operational regularity rather than explicit formal rules.

This investigation seeks to determine the nature of that regularity.

---

# Candidate Definition 1

Operational regularity is repeated behavior.

Evaluation:

Insufficient.

Random repetition also exhibits repeated behavior.

Repetition alone does not distinguish reasoning.

---

# Candidate Definition 2

Operational regularity is deterministic behavior.

Evaluation:

Insufficient.

Previous investigations suggest that reasoning may be nondeterministic while remaining operationally coherent.

---

# Candidate Definition 3

Operational regularity is behavior constrained by stable operational principles.

Evaluation:

Promising.

Different operational paths may occur while remaining governed by common principles.

---

# Test Case 1

A mathematician proves a theorem.

The exact proof differs each time.

Question:

Is the reasoning regular?

Observation:

Yes.

The sequence changes.

The governing principles do not.

---

# Test Case 2

A scientist investigates two unrelated phenomena.

Different operational decisions occur.

Question:

Does regularity persist?

Observation:

Yes.

Different actions remain constrained by consistent investigative principles.

---

# Test Case 3

An AI system evaluates two different arguments.

Different operational paths occur.

Question:

Is operational regularity lost?

Observation:

No.

The operational policy remains unchanged despite different execution.

---

# Pattern Analysis

The investigated cases suggest that regularity concerns constraints rather than specific operational sequences.

Different executions may satisfy identical operational principles.

---

# Competing Hypotheses

## H1

Operational regularity is repeated operational behavior.

---

## H2

Operational regularity is conformity to stable operational constraints.

---

# Evaluation

Current evidence favors H2.

Operational regularity appears independent of the specific sequence of operations.

Instead, regularity appears to arise from invariant operational constraints governing execution.

---

# Decision Criteria

If different operational executions consistently satisfy the same governing constraints, operational regularity is best understood as conformity to invariant operational principles rather than repetition.

---

# Provisional Conclusion

Current evidence suggests that operational regularity consists in the preservation of invariant operational constraints across different reasoning executions.

Whether those constraints can themselves be reduced remains unresolved.

---

# Remaining Questions

Future investigations should determine:

- whether operational constraints are primitive;
- whether operational constraints are derivable from FARA;
- whether operational constraints admit formal proof;
- whether all reasoning systems share the same operational constraints.

---

# Current Status

The investigation remains active.

The nature of operational regularity has not yet been fully established.
