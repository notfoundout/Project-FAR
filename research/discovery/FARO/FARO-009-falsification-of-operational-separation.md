# FARO Discovery Investigation

## Identifier

FARO-009

---

# Title

Falsification of Operational Separation

---

## Status

In Progress

---

# Purpose

Attempt to falsify the hypothesis that FARO is a distinct framework.

The objective is to determine whether every operational responsibility identified thus far can instead be incorporated into FARA without violating its purpose.

---

# Dependencies

- FARO-001 — Operational Necessity
- FARO-002 — Inferential Evaluation
- FARO-003 — Operational Rule Selection
- FARO-004 — Investigation Control
- FARO-005 — Investigation Termination
- FARO-006 — Operational Synthesis
- FARO-007 — Operational Responsibilities
- FARO-008 — Operational Universality

---

# Central Question

Can FARA absorb every identified operational responsibility while remaining a purely architectural framework?

---

# Null Hypothesis

A separate operational framework is unnecessary.

Every operational capability can be incorporated into FARA.

---

# Test

Suppose the following responsibilities are added directly to FARA.

- inference execution;
- rule selection;
- investigation control;
- termination policy;
- operational state progression.

---

# Evaluation

## Question 1

Would FARA continue to describe only the architecture of reasoning?

Result:

Undetermined.

---

## Question 2

Would FARA now prescribe how reasoning executes?

Result:

Yes.

---

## Question 3

Would the distinction between architectural specification and operational execution remain?

Result:

No.

---

## Question 4

Would FARA's stated purpose change?

Result:

Potentially.

The framework would become both a specification of reasoning structures and an execution specification.

---

# Analysis

The previous investigations consistently identified operational deficiencies rather than missing architectural primitives.

Adding operational procedures directly into FARA changes the responsibilities assigned to the framework.

The distinction previously observed between architecture and operation would disappear.

---

# Decision Criteria

If incorporating every operational responsibility changes the purpose of FARA, then the null hypothesis fails.

If FARA's purpose remains unchanged, FARO is unnecessary.

---

# Provisional Conclusion

Current evidence suggests that operational execution cannot be incorporated into FARA without fundamentally changing the scope of the framework.

This does not prove FARO exists.

It does, however, strengthen the case that architecture and operation are distinct aspects of reasoning.

---

# Current Status

The null hypothesis has not been sustained.

Further attempts to falsify operational separation remain encouraged.
