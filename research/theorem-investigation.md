## Research Principle

A primitive concept should not be regarded as globally independent until every plausible reduction strategy has been explicitly investigated and found unsuccessful.

Failure of a single reduction attempt does not establish primitive status.

# T-MINIMAL-PRIMITIVE-ARCHITECTURE

## Test 1 — Global Independence of Representation

### Status

Completed

---

### Research Question

Can Representation be derived from the remaining primitive architecture consisting of:

- Investigation
- Representational Structure
- Interpretation
- Reasoning Calculus

---

### Hypothesis

Representation is not primitive and can be derived from the remaining primitive architecture.

---

### Attempted Reduction

Representation was removed from the primitive architecture.

The remaining primitive concepts were analyzed to determine whether explicit representations could be reconstructed without assuming their prior existence.

---

### Analysis

Representational Structure specifies explicit relations.

However, relations require objects to relate.

Without representations, no relata exist for the structure to organize.

Interpretation assigns meaning.

However, meaning must be assigned to explicit objects.

Without representations, there is nothing to interpret.

Reasoning Calculus governs admissible reasoning transitions.

However, transitions operate upon explicit representations.

Without representations, no reasoning transitions can be defined.

Investigation specifies the bounded context of a reasoning process.

However, a bounded reasoning process requires explicit objects that participate within that context.

Without representations, an investigation contains no explicit objects of reasoning.

Every attempted reconstruction therefore presupposed the existence of representations rather than deriving them.

---

### Counterexample Analysis

No counterexample was identified.

No construction was found in which Investigation, Representational Structure, Interpretation, and Reasoning Calculus existed while Representation did not.

---

### Hidden Assumption Analysis

No hidden assumptions were introduced during the attempted reduction.

Instead, the analysis revealed that every remaining primitive implicitly depends upon the existence of representations.

---

### Methodological Observation

Unlike previous pairwise investigations, this analysis attempted a reduction from the complete remaining primitive architecture.

Accordingly, the result provides evidence concerning global independence rather than pairwise independence.

---

### Provisional Conclusion

The attempted reduction was unsuccessful.

Current evidence supports the provisional conclusion that Representation is globally independent of the remaining primitive architecture.

This conclusion remains subject to future attempts at reduction.

---

### Implications

If this conclusion survives further investigation, Representation cannot be removed from the primitive architecture without introducing an equivalent primitive or reducing the expressive power of Project FAR.

This investigation therefore provides preliminary support for T-MINIMAL-PRIMITIVE-ARCHITECTURE.

- Investigation

---

### Hypothesis

Representational Structure is not primitive and can be derived from the remaining primitive architecture.

---

### Attempted Reduction

Representational Structure was removed from the primitive architecture.

The remaining primitive concepts were analyzed to determine whether the relationships among representations could be reconstructed without assuming an explicit representational structure.

---

### Analysis

Representations specify the explicit objects participating in reasoning.

Interpretation assigns meaning to those representations.

Reasoning Calculus governs the admissible transformations of representations.

Investigation specifies the bounded context within which reasoning occurs.

None of these concepts specifies how representations are explicitly related.

Consider two investigations containing:

- identical representations;
- identical interpretations;
- identical reasoning calculi; and
- identical investigation boundaries.

In the first investigation:

- R₁ supports R₂.
- R₂ contradicts R₃.

In the second investigation:

- R₁ contradicts R₂.
- R₂ supports R₃.

Every remaining primitive is identical.

Only the relationships among the representations differ.

Accordingly, the remaining primitive architecture does not uniquely determine the organization of the representations.

Representational Structure therefore cannot be reconstructed from the remaining primitive concepts.

---

### Counterexample Analysis

The two investigations described above constitute a counterexample to the proposed reduction.

Identical investigations, representations, interpretations, and reasoning calculi may possess different organizations of representations.

Therefore, Representational Structure is not uniquely determined by the remaining primitive architecture.

---

### Hidden Assumption Analysis

No hidden assumptions were introduced during the attempted reduction.

The failure resulted from the absence of information describing explicit relationships among representations.

---

### Methodological Observation

This investigation tested global rather than pairwise reduction.

The analysis demonstrates that the complete remaining primitive architecture is insufficient to reconstruct Representational Structure.

---

### Provisional Conclusion

The attempted reduction was unsuccessful.

Current evidence supports the provisional conclusion that Representational Structure is globally independent of the remaining primitive architecture.

This conclusion remains subject to future attempts at reduction.
