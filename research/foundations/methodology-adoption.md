# Methodology Adoption Investigation

## Purpose

Determine the conditions under which a methodology should be adopted within Project FAR.

The objective is to distinguish methodological consistency from methodological justification.

---

# Motivation

Previous investigations attempted to determine whether the Project FAR methodology can justify itself.

However, this presupposes that self-justification is the appropriate criterion for adopting a methodology.

This investigation examines that assumption.

---

# Central Question

What justifies adopting a methodology?

---

# Candidate Criterion 1

Self-Proof.

Question

Must a methodology prove itself?

Evaluation

A methodology defines procedures for investigation.

Those procedures cannot generally be established before the methodology exists.

Requiring self-proof therefore appears excessively restrictive.

Result

Not Supported.

---

# Candidate Criterion 2

Internal Consistency.

Question

Must a methodology avoid internal contradiction?

Evaluation

A contradictory methodology cannot reliably guide investigation.

Result

Necessary.

---

# Candidate Criterion 3

Reproducibility.

Question

Must independent investigators applying the methodology under equivalent conditions reach equivalent conclusions?

Evaluation

Without reproducibility, methodology cannot reliably support investigation.

Result

Necessary.

---

# Candidate Criterion 4

Traceability.

Question

Must conclusions be traceable to explicit investigative steps?

Evaluation

Without traceability, methodological evaluation becomes impossible.

Result

Necessary.

---

# Candidate Criterion 5

Correctability.

Question

Must the methodology permit revision when defects are discovered?

Evaluation

A methodology incapable of correction cannot improve through investigation.

Result

Necessary.

---

# Candidate Criterion 6

Utility.

Question

Must the methodology successfully achieve its stated objectives?

Evaluation

A methodology that consistently fails to achieve its intended purpose lacks practical justification.

Result

Necessary.

---

# Comparative Analysis

The investigated criteria separate into two categories.

## Self-Referential

- self-proof.

---

## Performance-Based

- consistency;
- reproducibility;
- traceability;
- correctability;
- utility.

Current evidence favors performance-based criteria.

---

# Emerging Hypothesis

Methodologies are justified by demonstrated performance rather than self-proof.

The role of a methodology is not to prove itself.

Its role is to reliably guide investigation.

---

# Consequences

If accepted:

- methodological evaluation becomes empirical rather than self-referential;
- Project FAR avoids requiring impossible forms of self-foundation;
- future methodological revisions become governed by performance rather than tradition.

---

# Remaining Questions

Future investigations should determine:

- whether the identified criteria are jointly sufficient;
- whether methodologies admit formal comparison;
- whether one methodology can dominate another under explicit evaluation criteria;
- whether methodological optimality can be formally characterized.

---

# Current Status

The investigation remains active.

Current evidence supports evaluating methodologies by their demonstrated performance rather than by self-justification.
