# Conclusion Reliability Investigation

## Purpose

Determine the conditions under which a conclusion is considered reliable within Project FAR.

The objective is to establish the meaning of reliability independently of truth, belief, or authority.

---

# Motivation

Previous investigations concluded that methodologies should be evaluated according to their ability to produce reliable conclusions.

However, reliability itself has not been formally investigated.

This investigation addresses that omission.

---

# Central Question

What makes a conclusion reliable?

---

# Candidate Criterion 1

Truth.

Question

Must a reliable conclusion be true?

Evaluation

Reliability concerns the quality of the investigative process.

Truth concerns correspondence with reality.

A conclusion may be reliably obtained yet later shown to be false because the available evidence was incomplete.

Conversely, a true conclusion may arise through unreliable reasoning.

Result

Rejected.

Truth and reliability are distinct.

---

# Candidate Criterion 2

Justification.

Question

Must a reliable conclusion be justified?

Evaluation

A conclusion lacking justification cannot be independently evaluated.

Justification therefore appears necessary.

Result

Supported.

---

# Candidate Criterion 3

Reproducibility.

Question

Must independent investigators applying the same methodology under equivalent conditions reach equivalent conclusions?

Evaluation

Without reproducibility, reliability cannot be distinguished from coincidence.

Result

Supported.

---

# Candidate Criterion 4

Traceability.

Question

Must every inferential step be reviewable?

Evaluation

Without traceability, reliability cannot be assessed.

Result

Supported.

---

# Candidate Criterion 5

Revisability.

Question

Must reliable conclusions remain open to revision when contradictory evidence appears?

Evaluation

An irreversible conclusion cannot respond to new information.

Result

Supported.

---

# Pattern Analysis

Reliability concerns the properties of the reasoning process rather than the metaphysical status of the conclusion.

Reliable investigation does not guarantee truth.

It guarantees that the conclusion was reached according to explicitly evaluable procedures.

---

# Emerging Hypothesis

A reliable conclusion is one obtained through a justified, traceable, reproducible, and revisable investigation.

Truth remains an independent question.

---

# Consequences

If accepted:

- Project FAR separates process quality from factual correctness;
- conclusions become evaluable independently of later discoveries;
- methodological evaluation becomes operationally well-defined.

# Remaining Questions

Future investigations should determine:

- whether reliability admits quantitative measures;
- whether reliability is domain-dependent;
- whether different reasoning systems possess different notions of reliability;
- whether reliability itself possesses formal invariants.

---

# Current Status

The investigation remains active.

Current evidence supports treating reliability as a property of investigations rather than a guarantee of truth.
