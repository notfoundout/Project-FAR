# Meta-Principle Self-Justification Investigation

## Purpose

Determine whether the proposed methodological meta-principle of Project FAR can justify itself.

The objective is to establish whether the methodology possesses internal coherence or whether it ultimately depends upon an unexamined assumption.

---

# Motivation

Previous investigations proposed the following meta-principle:

> Introduce no distinction, assumption, or commitment until it has been justified by explicit investigation.

This principle governs every subsequent methodological decision.

Accordingly, it must itself be subjected to its own standard.

---

# Central Question

Can the methodological meta-principle justify its own adoption?

---

# Candidate Position 1

The meta-principle is accepted without investigation.

Evaluation

This violates the principle itself.

An unjustified methodological commitment contradicts the proposed methodology.

Result

Rejected.

---

# Candidate Position 2

The meta-principle is investigated using itself.

Evaluation

The investigation applies the proposed principle to its own adoption.

The methodology therefore becomes self-applicable.

This introduces the possibility of circularity.

Result

Requires further analysis.

---

# Candidate Position 3

The meta-principle is derived from more fundamental principles.

Evaluation

No more fundamental methodological principles have yet been identified.

Result

Currently unsupported.

---

# Circularity Analysis

Question

Does self-application necessarily produce vicious circularity?

Observation

Not every self-reference is logically defective.

A self-applicable rule may remain coherent provided its application does not presuppose its own conclusion.

Accordingly, the existence of self-reference alone does not invalidate the methodology.

---

# Consistency Test

Procedure

Assume the meta-principle.

Apply it to itself.

Question

Does the investigation require assumptions inconsistent with the principle?

Current Observation

No inconsistency has yet been identified.

Further analysis remains necessary.

---

# Falsification Test

The meta-principle fails if any of the following occur:

- it requires an unjustified exception for itself;
- it cannot be investigated by its own methodology;
- its adoption depends upon assumptions prohibited by the principle.

---

# Emerging Hypothesis

The methodological meta-principle may be self-consistent without being self-proving.

The distinction between consistency and proof therefore becomes significant.

---

# Consequences

If the principle survives investigation:

- Project FAR possesses a self-consistent methodological foundation.

If the principle fails:

- the methodology must be revised before additional foundational work proceeds.

---

# Remaining Questions

Future investigations should determine:

- whether methodological self-reference admits formal characterization;
- whether self-application requires fixed-point constructions;
- whether methodological consistency is sufficient for adoption;
- whether the methodology possesses a minimal independent axiom.

---

# Current Status

The investigation remains active.

The methodological foundation of Project FAR has not yet been conclusively established.
