# Architectural Meta-Principle Investigation

## Purpose

Determine whether the recurring architectural principles identified throughout Project FAR derive from a single underlying meta-principle.

The objective is to minimize the number of independent methodological assumptions required by the theory.

---

# Motivation

Previous investigations identified several recurring principles.

Examples include:

- Primitive Economy;
- Dependency First;
- Structure Before Semantics.

Rather than accepting these as independent principles, this investigation asks whether they are consequences of a deeper organizing principle.

---

# Central Question

Do the architectural principles of Project FAR share a common foundation?

---

# Candidate Principle 1

Primitive Economy.

Observation

The theory minimizes irreducible assumptions.

Purpose

Reduce explanatory complexity.

---

# Candidate Principle 2

Dependency First.

Observation

Concepts are established through explicit dependency relations.

Purpose

Replace assertion with demonstrable structure.

---

# Candidate Principle 3

Structure Before Semantics.

Observation

Formal structure is defined independently of interpretation.

Purpose

Prevent semantic assumptions from influencing formal analysis.

---

# Comparative Analysis

Each principle seeks to separate what is necessary from what is contingent.

Primitive Economy separates:

Necessary assumptions

↓

Unnecessary assumptions

---

Dependency First separates:

Demonstrated dependence

↓

Undemonstrated assertion

---

Structure Before Semantics separates:

Formal organization

↓

Assigned meaning

---

# Emerging Pattern

Each investigated principle delays commitment.

No distinction is accepted until it is justified through explicit investigation.

This pattern appears throughout Project FAR.

---

# Candidate Meta-Principle

Introduce no distinction, assumption, or commitment until it has been justified by explicit investigation.

Under this principle:

Primitive Economy follows because unnecessary primitives remain unjustified.

Dependency First follows because dependencies require explicit proof.

Structure Before Semantics follows because semantic commitments are postponed until structural analysis is complete.

---

# Competing Hypotheses

## H1

The architectural principles are independent.

Each must be accepted separately.

---

## H2

The architectural principles are consequences of a common methodological commitment to justified distinction.

---

# Evaluation

Current evidence favors H2.

The investigated principles appear to share the same methodological objective:

Delay commitment until justification is established.

---

# Consequences

If accepted:

- Project FAR requires fewer independent methodological assumptions.
- Future architectural principles may be evaluated by determining whether they follow from the meta-principle.
- The methodology becomes more unified and internally coherent.

---

# Remaining Questions

Future investigations should determine:

- whether every existing architectural principle follows from the proposed meta-principle;
- whether additional principles emerge from the same foundation;
- whether the meta-principle itself is reducible or primitive.

---

# Current Status

The investigation remains active.

Current evidence supports the existence of a unifying methodological meta-principle underlying the architectural principles of Project FAR.
