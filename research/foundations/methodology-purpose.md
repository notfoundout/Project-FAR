# Methodology Purpose Investigation

## Purpose

Determine the fundamental purpose of a methodology within Project FAR.

The objective is to establish the function that methodologies serve and thereby provide a basis for evaluating methodological success.

---

# Motivation

Previous investigations proposed several criteria for evaluating methodologies.

Those criteria presuppose that the purpose of a methodology is already known.

This investigation examines that assumption.

---

# Central Question

What is the purpose of a methodology?

---

# Candidate Purpose 1

Produce True Conclusions.

Question

Is the purpose of a methodology to guarantee truth?

Evaluation

No methodology can guarantee truth independently of its premises, evidence, or domain.

Methodologies guide investigation rather than determine reality.

Result

Rejected.

---

# Candidate Purpose 2

Produce Consistent Conclusions.

Question

Is consistency alone sufficient?

Evaluation

A consistently applied defective methodology may consistently produce incorrect conclusions.

Consistency is necessary but not sufficient.

Result

Rejected.

---

# Candidate Purpose 3

Organize Investigation.

Question

Is methodology fundamentally organizational?

Evaluation

Methodologies prescribe:

- investigative procedures;
- admissible reasoning;
- evidence handling;
- revision procedures.

They organize investigative activity.

Result

Supported.

---

# Candidate Purpose 4

Reduce Investigative Error.

Question

Does methodology exist to minimize error?

Evaluation

Methodological rules frequently reduce:

- ambiguity;
- unjustified assumptions;
- hidden dependencies;
- irreproducible reasoning.

Error reduction appears to be a major consequence.

Whether it is the primary purpose remains unresolved.

Result

Partially Supported.

---

# Candidate Purpose 5

Produce Reliable Investigation.

Question

Does methodology exist to improve investigative reliability?

Evaluation

Reliability encompasses:

- reproducibility;
- traceability;
- consistency;
- correction.

These correspond closely to previous investigations.

Result

Strongly Supported.

---

# Pattern Analysis

The investigated purposes suggest that methodology is not a mechanism for producing truth directly.

Instead, methodology structures investigation so that conclusions are:

- reproducible;
- reviewable;
- revisable;
- reliable.

Truth remains the subject of investigation rather than a guarantee of methodology.

---

# Emerging Hypothesis

The fundamental purpose of methodology is to organize investigation in a manner that maximizes the reliability of its conclusions.

---

# Consequences

If accepted:

- methodological evaluation becomes purpose-driven;
- methodological criteria derive from reliability rather than tradition;
- future methodological improvements may be evaluated according to their contribution to investigative reliability.

---

# Relationship to Previous Investigations

This investigation provides the objective against which:

- consistency;
- reproducibility;
- traceability;
- correctability;
- utility;

are evaluated.

These become means rather than ends.

---

# Remaining Questions

Future investigations should determine:

- whether reliability can be formally characterized;
- whether reliability admits quantitative measures;
- whether multiple methodologies may optimize different forms of reliability;
- whether methodological reliability possesses formal limits.

---

# Current Status

The investigation remains active.

Current evidence supports reliability as the primary objective of methodology within Project FAR.
