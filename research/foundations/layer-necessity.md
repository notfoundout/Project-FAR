# Layer Necessity Investigation

## Purpose

Determine whether the layered organization of Project FAR arises necessarily from the requirements of reasoning or whether it is merely a convenient architectural choice.

The objective is to establish whether the structural, process, and evaluation layers are fundamental.

---

# Motivation

Previous investigations suggest that Project FAR naturally separates into three layers:

- Structure
- Process
- Evaluation

This investigation determines whether those layers are necessary.

---

# Central Question

Can reasoning exist without each proposed layer?

---

# Layer 1

Structure

Question

Can reasoning occur without representations, relations, or distinguishable entities?

Evaluation

Reasoning requires objects about which reasoning occurs.

Without formal structure, reasoning possesses no subject.

Result

Necessary.

---

# Layer 2

Process

Question

Can reasoning occur without operations or investigative progression?

Evaluation

Static representations alone cannot produce conclusions.

Reasoning requires execution.

Result

Necessary.

---

# Layer 3

Evaluation

Question

Can reasoning occur without evaluating its own conclusions?

Evaluation

Processes may produce conclusions.

Without evaluation there is no distinction between justified and unjustified conclusions.

Reasoning therefore lacks self-assessment.

Result

Necessary.

---

# Dependency Analysis

Structure provides:

what exists.

---

Process provides:

what happens.

---

Evaluation provides:

how results are assessed.

Each layer answers a fundamentally different question.

No investigated layer duplicates another.

---

# Candidate Hypothesis

The three layers arise necessarily because every reasoning system requires:

- entities;
- operations over entities;
- assessment of operational outcomes.

---

# Competing Hypotheses

## H1

The layered architecture is arbitrary.

Alternative organizations are equally fundamental.

---

## H2

The layered architecture reflects the minimal functional decomposition of reasoning.

---

# Evaluation

Current evidence favors H2.

Each layer introduces capabilities unavailable in the preceding layers.

Removing any layer produces an incomplete reasoning system.

---

# Consequences

If accepted:

- every future Project FAR framework should identify:
  - structural components;
  - operational components;
  - evaluative components.

Layering becomes an architectural principle rather than a documentation convention.

---

# Remaining Questions

Future investigations should determine:

- whether additional fundamental layers exist;
- whether layers possess universal interfaces;
- whether layers admit independent axiomatization;
- whether layer composition possesses formal laws.

---

# Current Status

The investigation remains active.

Current evidence supports the layered architecture as a fundamental organizational principle of Project FAR.
