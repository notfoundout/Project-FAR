# Investigation Falsification

## Purpose

Attempt to falsify the hypothesis that investigation is the fundamental methodological unit of Project FAR.

The objective is to determine whether any genuine reasoning activity exists that cannot be expressed as an investigation.

---

# Motivation

The previous investigation proposed that every reasoning activity is an investigation or a specialization of one.

If a single counterexample exists, this hypothesis fails.

Accordingly, the hypothesis shall be subjected to explicit falsification.

---

# Central Question

Does any reasoning activity exist that is not an investigation?

---

# Null Hypothesis

At least one genuine reasoning activity exists independently of investigation.

---

# Alternative Hypothesis

Every reasoning activity is an investigation or a specialization of investigation.

---

# Test 1

## Passive Observation

Question:

Is merely observing a representation reasoning?

Evaluation:

Observation alone does not attempt to establish, evaluate, derive, classify, explain, or resolve anything.

Observation therefore appears to be data acquisition rather than reasoning.

Result:

Not a counterexample.

---

# Test 2

## Memory Recall

Question:

Is recalling previously known information reasoning?

Evaluation:

Simple retrieval reproduces an existing representation.

No reasoning objective is necessarily pursued.

Result:

Not a counterexample.

---

# Test 3

## Arithmetic Execution

Question:

Is executing an established arithmetic algorithm reasoning?

Evaluation:

If execution merely follows a predetermined procedure without evaluating alternatives, it may constitute computation rather than reasoning.

If uncertainty or evaluation exists, the activity becomes investigative.

Result:

Not a clear counterexample.

---

# Test 4

## Formal Proof

Question:

Can a proof exist without an investigation?

Evaluation:

Every proof seeks to establish a proposition.

The proposition defines an explicit objective.

Result:

Not a counterexample.

---

# Test 5

## Falsification

Question:

Can falsification occur independently of investigation?

Evaluation:

No.

Falsification explicitly investigates whether a claim survives attempted refutation.

Result:

Not a counterexample.

---

# Test 6

## Validation

Question:

Can validation occur independently of investigation?

Evaluation:

No.

Validation evaluates whether specified criteria are satisfied.

Result:

Not a counterexample.

---

# Test 7

## Classification

Question:

Can classification occur without an investigation?

Evaluation:

Classification determines whether an object satisfies specified criteria.

This constitutes an explicit reasoning objective.

Result:

Not a counterexample.

---

# Pattern Analysis

The examined activities divide into two categories.

## Category A

Activities involving no evaluation.

Examples:

- passive observation;
- memory retrieval.

These do not appear to constitute reasoning.

---

## Category B

Activities involving evaluation toward an objective.

Examples:

- proof;
- validation;
- falsification;
- classification;
- explanation.

Each appears expressible as an investigation.

---

# Observation

Current evidence suggests that investigation characterizes reasoning rather than cognition in general.

Not every cognitive activity is an investigation.

Every investigated reasoning activity appears to be.

---

# Decision Criteria

The investigation hypothesis fails if a genuine reasoning activity cannot be modeled as an investigation.

It survives if every genuine reasoning activity examined satisfies the structure of an investigation.

---

# Provisional Conclusion

No counterexample has been identified.

Current evidence supports the hypothesis that investigation is the universal methodological unit of reasoning within Project FAR.

---

# Remaining Questions

Future investigations should determine:

- the formal definition of reasoning;
- the relationship between reasoning and cognition;
- whether every investigation necessarily contains reasoning;
- whether investigations admit composition and decomposition.

---

# Current Status

The hypothesis remains provisional.

No successful falsification has yet been achieved.
