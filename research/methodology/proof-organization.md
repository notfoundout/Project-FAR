# Proof Organization

## Purpose

Determine the fundamental organizational principle underlying proofs.

The objective is to identify what transforms one or more justified derivations into a coherent proof.

---

# Motivation

Previous investigations suggest that proofs consist of organized derivations.

The nature of that organization remains undefined.

This investigation seeks to identify the principle responsible for proof organization.

---

# Central Question

What organizes derivations into a proof?

---

# Candidate Organization 1

Temporal Order.

Question:

Is a proof merely a sequence of derivations?

Evaluation:

No.

Many proofs branch, revisit assumptions, or organize derivations hierarchically.

Temporal order alone is insufficient.

Result:

Rejected.

---

# Candidate Organization 2

Dependency.

Question:

Are derivations organized by dependency?

Evaluation:

Each derivation depends upon:

- premises;
- previous derivations;
- assumptions.

Dependency determines which derivations support others.

Result:

Supported.

---

# Candidate Organization 3

Proof Strategy.

Question:

Does proof strategy organize derivations?

Evaluation:

A strategy determines which derivations are constructed.

However, the derivations remain connected through dependency.

Strategy appears to guide construction rather than define organization.

Result:

Partially Supported.

---

# Candidate Organization 4

Goal.

Question:

Does the conclusion organize the proof?

Evaluation:

The desired conclusion determines direction.

It does not determine the relationships among derivations.

Result:

Partially Supported.

---

# Pattern Analysis

Every investigated proof possesses:

- derivations;
- dependencies among derivations.

The dependency structure appears invariant across proof strategies.

Proof strategy influences construction.

Dependency determines organization.

---

# Emerging Hypothesis

A proof consists of a dependency graph whose nodes are justified derivations.

The proof strategy determines how the graph is constructed.

The dependency graph constitutes the proof itself.

---

# Competing Hypotheses

## H1

Proof organization is sequential.

---

## H2

Proof organization is dependency-based.

---

# Evaluation

Current evidence favors H2.

Sequential presentation appears to be one representation of a more fundamental dependency structure.

The underlying proof is the dependency graph.

---

# Consequences

If accepted:

- proofs become graph structures;
- proof strategies become graph construction procedures;
- proof checking becomes dependency verification;
- proof equivalence becomes graph equivalence rather than textual similarity.

---

# Remaining Questions

Future investigations should determine:

- whether proof graphs possess canonical forms;
- whether multiple graph representations may encode the same proof;
- whether dependency graphs admit algebraic operations;
- whether proof graphs are a specialization of reasoning graphs.

---

# Current Status

The investigation remains active.

Current evidence supports dependency as the fundamental organizational principle of proofs.
