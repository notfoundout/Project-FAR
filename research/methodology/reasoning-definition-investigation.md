# Reasoning Definition Investigation

## Purpose

Determine the necessary and sufficient conditions under which an activity constitutes reasoning.

The objective is to establish a formal definition of reasoning independent of intuition or common usage.

---

# Motivation

Previous investigations suggest that every reasoning activity may be modeled as an investigation.

However, this conclusion depends upon correctly identifying what counts as reasoning.

Accordingly, the concept of reasoning itself must be investigated.

---

# Central Question

What distinguishes reasoning from other cognitive or computational activities?

---

# Candidate Activity 1

Passive Observation.

Question:

Is passive observation reasoning?

Evaluation:

Observation acquires information.

It does not necessarily evaluate, derive, compare, infer, justify, or resolve.

Result:

Not reasoning.

---

# Candidate Activity 2

Memory Retrieval.

Question:

Is recalling existing information reasoning?

Evaluation:

Retrieval reproduces an existing representation.

No evaluation is necessarily performed.

Result:

Not reasoning.

---

# Candidate Activity 3

Random Modification.

Question:

Is randomly altering a representation reasoning?

Evaluation:

The modification lacks evaluation or justification.

Result:

Not reasoning.

---

# Candidate Activity 4

Comparison.

Question:

Is comparing two representations reasoning?

Evaluation:

Comparison evaluates relationships between representations.

Result:

Candidate reasoning activity.

---

# Candidate Activity 5

Inference.

Question:

Is deriving one representation from others reasoning?

Evaluation:

Inference explicitly evaluates representational relationships.

Result:

Reasoning.

---

# Candidate Activity 6

Classification.

Question:

Is determining whether a representation satisfies specified criteria reasoning?

Evaluation:

Classification evaluates representations relative to explicit conditions.

Result:

Reasoning.

---

# Candidate Activity 7

Explanation.

Question:

Is constructing an explanation reasoning?

Evaluation:

Explanation evaluates candidate relations among representations.

Result:

Reasoning.

---

# Pattern Analysis

Activities identified as reasoning share a common characteristic.

Each evaluates one or more representations relative to an explicitly or implicitly defined objective.

Activities lacking evaluation consistently fail to qualify.

---

# Competing Hypotheses

## H1

Reasoning is any cognitive activity.

---

## H2

Reasoning is evaluative activity directed toward an objective.

---

# Evaluation

Current evidence rejects H1.

Many cognitive activities involve no evaluation.

Current evidence favors H2.

Evaluation appears to distinguish reasoning from cognition more generally.

---

# Decision Criteria

A proposed definition of reasoning is acceptable only if it:

- classifies every investigated reasoning activity correctly;
- excludes non-reasoning activities;
- introduces no unnecessary assumptions.

---

# Provisional Conclusion

Current evidence suggests that reasoning is evaluative activity directed toward an objective.

Whether evaluation itself admits further reduction remains unresolved.

---

# Remaining Questions

Future investigations should determine:

- whether evaluation is primitive;
- whether objectives are necessary;
- whether every investigation necessarily contains evaluation;
- whether evaluation can be formally represented within FARA.

---

# Current Status

The investigation remains active.

No final definition of reasoning has yet been established.
