# Investigation Validity

## Purpose

This document establishes the criteria under which an investigation is considered a legitimate investigation within Project FAR.

Its objective is to distinguish systematic investigation from arbitrary inquiry.

---

# Motivation

Project FAR derives conclusions through investigations.

If investigations themselves are unconstrained, the reliability of every subsequent result becomes questionable.

Accordingly, investigations require explicit validity criteria.

---

# Central Question

What conditions must be satisfied for an investigation to be considered valid?

---

# Candidate Criterion 1

Clearly Defined Objective.

Question:

Does the investigation possess an explicit objective?

Evaluation:

Without an objective, there is no criterion for determining success or completion.

Status:

Necessary.

---

# Candidate Criterion 2

Explicit Scope.

Question:

Does the investigation define what is and is not being investigated?

Evaluation:

Without scope, conclusions may extend beyond the intended subject.

Status:

Necessary.

---

# Candidate Criterion 3

Explicit Methodology.

Question:

Does the investigation specify how conclusions will be reached?

Evaluation:

Without methodology, results cannot be independently evaluated.

Status:

Necessary.

---

# Candidate Criterion 4

Falsifiability.

Question:

Can the investigation produce a result contrary to its initial expectations?

Evaluation:

If every possible outcome confirms the initial hypothesis, the investigation cannot discriminate among alternatives.

Status:

Necessary.

---

# Candidate Criterion 5

Traceability.

Question:

Can every conclusion be traced to explicit reasoning steps?

Evaluation:

Without traceability, conclusions cannot be independently reviewed.

Status:

Necessary.

---

# Candidate Criterion 6

Revision Policy.

Question:

Can conclusions be revised when contrary evidence is discovered?

Evaluation:

Without revision, investigations become dogmatic rather than investigative.

Status:

Necessary.

---

# Pattern Analysis

Each criterion governs the conduct of the investigation rather than the content of its conclusions.

Together they define the minimum structure required for a legitimate investigation.

---

# Decision Criteria

An investigation is valid only if all required criteria are satisfied.

Failure of any criterion invalidates the investigation regardless of the correctness of its conclusions.

---

# Consequences

The validity of conclusions depends upon the validity of the investigations from which they arise.

Investigation validity therefore precedes theorem validity, proof validity, and framework validation.

---

# Current Status

The proposed validity criteria remain subject to investigation and revision.

No criterion shall be considered final until independently validated.
