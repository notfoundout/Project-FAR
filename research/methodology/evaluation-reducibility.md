# Evaluation Reducibility

## Purpose

Determine whether evaluation is a primitive concept or whether it is reducible to more fundamental methodological concepts.

The objective is to identify whether evaluation should remain a foundational concept within Project FAR.

---

# Motivation

The previous investigation identified evaluation as the strongest candidate for distinguishing reasoning from other activities.

Before accepting evaluation as primitive, every reasonable reduction shall be investigated.

---

# Central Question

Can evaluation be completely explained using more fundamental concepts?

---

# Candidate Reduction 1

Evaluation as Comparison.

Question:

Does every evaluation compare something against one or more criteria?

Observation:

Acceptance compares against admissibility.

Classification compares against definitions.

Proof compares against logical validity.

Validation compares against requirements.

Result:

Promising.

---

# Candidate Reduction 2

Evaluation as Decision.

Question:

Is evaluation simply decision making?

Observation:

Many evaluations occur without immediately producing decisions.

Result:

Rejected.

---

# Candidate Reduction 3

Evaluation as Measurement.

Question:

Can every evaluation be expressed as measurement?

Observation:

Many reasoning activities are qualitative.

Measurement appears to be a special case.

Result:

Rejected.

---

# Candidate Reduction 4

Evaluation as Relation Identification.

Question:

Is evaluation merely identifying relations?

Observation:

Relations may be identified without determining their significance.

Evaluation appears to require something additional.

Result:

Insufficient.

---

# Pattern Analysis

Every investigated evaluation appears to require comparison.

Not every comparison appears to constitute evaluation.

Comparison therefore appears more fundamental than evaluation.

---

# Competing Hypotheses

## H1

Evaluation is primitive.

---

## H2

Evaluation consists of comparison performed relative to explicit or implicit criteria.

---

# Evaluation

Current evidence favors H2.

Evaluation appears reducible.

Comparison appears to be the stronger candidate for fundamentality.

---

# Decision Criteria

Evaluation is reducible if every evaluation necessarily contains comparison while comparison can occur without evaluation.

---

# Provisional Conclusion

Current evidence suggests that evaluation is not primitive.

Instead, evaluation appears to be comparison performed relative to criteria.

Further investigation is required to determine whether comparison itself is reducible.

---

# Remaining Questions

Future investigations should determine:

- whether comparison is primitive;
- whether criteria are primitive;
- whether comparison requires representations;
- whether comparison alone is sufficient for reasoning.

---

# Current Status

The investigation remains active.

Evaluation remains a reducible candidate concept.
