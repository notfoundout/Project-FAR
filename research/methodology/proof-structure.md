# Proof Structure

## Purpose

Determine whether every proof possesses the same underlying structural form.

The objective is to establish whether derivation is the universal structure of proofs or merely one proof strategy.

---

# Motivation

The previous investigation proposed that proofs are justified derivations.

Before accepting this definition, it must be determined whether every proof can be reduced to derivation.

---

# Central Question

Do all proofs possess the same structural form?

---

# Candidate Structure 1

Forward Derivation.

Description

Successively derive conclusions from premises.

Examples

- deductive proof;
- theorem proving;
- symbolic logic.

Status

Supported.

---

# Candidate Structure 2

Proof by Contradiction.

Description

Assume the negation of the conclusion.

Derive an inconsistency.

Reject the assumption.

Question

Is this merely forward derivation?

Evaluation

The internal reasoning is a derivation.

The overall proof structure differs.

Status

Partially Supported.

---

# Candidate Structure 3

Proof by Induction.

Description

Establish a base case.

Establish an inductive step.

Question

Can induction be reduced to ordinary derivation?

Evaluation

The proof contains multiple coordinated derivations.

Its overall structure differs.

Status

Partially Supported.

---

# Candidate Structure 4

Proof by Exhaustion.

Description

Demonstrate every admissible case.

Question

Is exhaustion simply derivation?

Evaluation

Each case contains a derivation.

The proof additionally requires complete case coverage.

Status

Partially Supported.

---

# Pattern Analysis

Every investigated proof contains derivations.

Not every proof is completely characterized by derivation alone.

Additional structural organization appears necessary.

---

# Emerging Hypothesis

Proof consists of:

- one or more derivations;
- an organizational strategy governing those derivations.

Derivation therefore appears necessary but not sufficient.

---

# Competing Hypotheses

## H1

Proof is identical to derivation.

---

## H2

Proof consists of organized derivations.

---

# Evaluation

Current evidence favors H2.

Derivation appears to be a fundamental component of proof.

Proof itself appears to possess higher-order structure.

---

# Consequences

If accepted:

- derivation becomes a primitive proof component;
- proof strategies become organizational patterns over derivations;
- contradiction, induction, exhaustion, construction, and case analysis become specialized proof organizations rather than independent proof types.

---

# Remaining Questions

Future investigations should determine:

- the minimal organizational structure required for proof;
- whether every proof strategy can be formally classified;
- whether proof strategies possess common invariants;
- whether proof identity depends upon derivation or organization.

---

# Current Status

The investigation remains active.

Current evidence supports treating derivation as a necessary component of proof but not as a complete characterization of proof.
