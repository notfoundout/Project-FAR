# Primitive Identification

## Purpose

This document establishes the methodology used throughout Project FAR for identifying primitive concepts.

Its purpose is to prevent infinite conceptual regress while minimizing the number of irreducible concepts admitted into the theory.

---

# Motivation

Every attempt to define a concept introduces additional concepts.

Unless constrained, repeated reduction produces an infinite regress.

A foundational theory therefore requires explicit criteria for identifying primitives.

---

# Principle 1 — Necessity

A concept shall not be treated as primitive unless every attempted reduction fails.

Successful reduction eliminates the concept as a primitive.

---

# Principle 2 — Non-Circularity

A reduction shall not invoke the concept being reduced.

Circular definitions do not constitute reductions.

---

# Principle 3 — Explanatory Economy

A reduction introducing greater conceptual complexity than the original concept shall be rejected.

A valid reduction should decrease, or at minimum preserve, theoretical simplicity.

---

# Principle 4 — Derivability

If a concept can be derived from previously accepted primitives, it shall not be introduced as an additional primitive.

---

# Principle 5 — Independence

Each accepted primitive shall be logically independent of every other accepted primitive.

No primitive shall be derivable from another primitive.

---

# Principle 6 — Sufficiency

The accepted primitives shall collectively suffice to reconstruct the remainder of the theory.

---

# Primitive Identification Procedure

For every candidate primitive:

1. Attempt reduction.
2. Reject circular reductions.
3. Reject complexity-increasing reductions.
4. Reject derivable concepts.
5. Test independence.
6. Verify reconstructive sufficiency.

Only concepts surviving every stage may be accepted as primitives.

---

# Consequences

The objective of Project FAR is not to minimize definitions.

The objective is to minimize irreducible assumptions.

Accordingly, primitive concepts should be admitted only when every reasonable attempt at reduction has failed.

---

# Current Status

This methodology governs all future foundational investigations within Project FAR.

No concept shall be accepted as primitive without satisfying these criteria.
