# Distinguishability Sufficiency

## Purpose

Determine whether distinguishability is sufficient to reconstruct the foundational concepts of Project FAR.

The objective is to validate distinguishability as a primitive by determining whether the remainder of the theory can be systematically derived from it.

---

# Motivation

Previous investigations suggest that distinguishability is the deepest primitive presently required by Project FAR.

A primitive is justified not merely by irreducibility, but by explanatory sufficiency.

Accordingly, distinguishability must be tested for reconstructive power.

---

# Central Question

Can the foundational concepts of Project FAR be derived from distinguishability?

---

# Reconstruction Target 1

Representation.

Question:

Can representations exist without distinguishability?

Evaluation:

A representation must be distinguishable from at least one other representation or from the absence of representation.

Otherwise it cannot function as a representation.

Result:

Representation appears to require distinguishability.

---

# Reconstruction Target 2

Relation.

Question:

Can relations exist without distinguishable entities?

Evaluation:

Relations require identifiable relata.

Without distinguishability there are no identifiable relata.

Result:

Relation appears derivable.

# Evaluation

The current reconstruction indicates that distinguishability may explain every foundational concept introduced thus far.

However, operation remains incompletely reconstructed.

Further investigation is required before sufficiency can be established.

---

# Decision Criteria

Distinguishability shall be accepted as a foundational primitive only if every core concept of Project FAR can ultimately be reconstructed from it without introducing additional irreducible concepts.

---

# Provisional Conclusion

Current evidence supports distinguishability as a candidate foundational primitive.

Its ultimate acceptance depends upon successful reconstruction of the remaining operational concepts.

---

# Remaining Questions

Future investigations should determine:

- whether operation is fully reconstructible;
- whether investigations require additional primitives;
- whether any core concept resists reconstruction from distinguishability.

---

# Current Status

The investigation remains active.

The reconstructive sufficiency of distinguishability has not yet been established.
