# Operation–Investigation Dependency

## Purpose

Determine the logical dependency between operation and investigation.

The objective is to establish whether one concept presupposes the other or whether both derive from a more fundamental concept.

---

# Motivation

Previous investigations produced the provisional dependency graph:

However, the dependency between investigation and operation has not been established.

This investigation attempts to derive the dependency from first principles.

---

# Central Question

Which concept logically presupposes the other?

---

# Candidate Dependency 1

Operation depends upon Investigation.

---

## Test

Suppose an operation exists.

The operation:

- evaluates;
- compares;
- verifies;
- infers.

Question:

Must an investigation already exist?

Evaluation:

Not obviously.

The operation appears capable of existing independently.

Result:

Current evidence does not support this dependency.

---

# Candidate Dependency 2

Investigation depends upon Operation.

---

## Test

Suppose an investigation exists.

The investigation possesses:

- an objective;
- representations;
- reasoning.

Question:

Can the investigation progress without operations?

Evaluation:

No.

Without operations, reasoning cannot proceed.

The investigation becomes static.

Result:

Supported.

---

# Candidate Dependency 3

Neither Depends Upon the Other.

---

## Test

Suppose operations and investigations are independent.

Question:

Can operations occur with no investigation?

Potentially yes.

Question:

Can investigations occur with no operations?

Current evidence:

No.

Result:

Asymmetry observed.

---

# Pattern Analysis

Operations appear capable of existing independently.

Investigations require operations in order to progress.

This dependency is one-directional.

# Consequences

If supported:

Operations become more fundamental than investigations.

Investigations become organized structures of operations directed toward explicit objectives.

Operations cease to be defined relative to investigations.

Instead, investigations are defined in terms of operations.

---

# Decision Criteria

The dependency shall be accepted if:

- operation can exist independently of investigation;
- investigation cannot progress without operation.

---

# Provisional Conclusion

Current evidence favors the hypothesis that investigations depend upon operations rather than operations depending upon investigations.

Operations therefore appear to occupy a more fundamental position within the Project FAR architecture.

---

# Remaining Questions

Future investigations should determine:

- whether operations possess independent identity;
- whether investigations are graphs of operations;
- whether reasoning consists of operations or merely organizes them;
- whether operations admit formal composition.

---

# Current Status

The investigation remains active.

The dependency between operation and investigation has not yet been conclusively established.
