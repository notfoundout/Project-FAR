# Methodology Falsification

## Purpose

This investigation attempts to falsify the primitive identification methodology established within Project FAR.

Its objective is to determine whether the methodology is capable of producing incorrect classifications or admitting unnecessary primitives.

---

# Motivation

A methodology that cannot fail cannot be validated.

Accordingly, every accepted methodological principle shall be subjected to explicit attempts at falsification.

---

# Central Question

Under what conditions would the primitive identification methodology produce an incorrect result?

---

# Falsification Test 1

## Hypothesis

The methodology incorrectly identifies a reducible concept as primitive.

### Procedure

Suppose a concept is accepted as primitive.

A later investigation discovers a successful non-circular reduction.

### Expected Result

The methodology shall reject the previous classification.

The concept shall cease to be primitive.

---

# Falsification Test 2

## Hypothesis

The methodology incorrectly rejects a genuine primitive.

### Procedure

Suppose every attempted reduction fails.

The methodology nevertheless classifies the concept as reducible.

### Expected Result

The classification is inconsistent with the methodology.

The methodology has failed.

---

# Falsification Test 3

## Hypothesis

The methodology accepts redundant primitives.

### Procedure

Suppose two accepted primitives are later shown to be logically dependent.

### Expected Result

The dependency violates the independence criterion.

At least one primitive must be removed.

---

# Falsification Test 4

## Hypothesis

The methodology produces different classifications when applied by different investigators.

### Procedure

Independent investigators apply the methodology to the same candidate primitive.

### Expected Result

Equivalent evidence should produce equivalent classifications.

Systematic disagreement indicates methodological ambiguity.

---

# Evaluation Criteria

The methodology fails if any of the following occur:

- a reducible concept is accepted as primitive;
- an irreducible concept is rejected;
- dependent primitives are accepted;
- independent applications produce inconsistent classifications.

---

# Consequences

If the methodology fails, the methodology itself becomes the subject of investigation.

Methodological principles are not exempt from revision.

---

# Provisional Conclusion

No failures have yet been identified.

The methodology remains provisional pending continued attempts at falsification.

---

# Guiding Principle

No methodological principle within Project FAR is immune from revision.

The methodology itself is subject to the same standards of investigation as every other component of the project.

---

# Status

Active.

The primitive identification methodology remains under continuous validation.
