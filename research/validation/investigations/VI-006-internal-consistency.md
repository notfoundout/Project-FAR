# Validation Investigation

## Investigation ID

VI-006

---

## Title

Internal Consistency

---

## Status

Research

---

# Purpose

This investigation evaluates whether the current formulation of the Foundational Architecture of Reasoning Analysis (FARA) is internally consistent.

The objective is to identify contradictions, circular dependencies, incompatible definitions, and violations of established architectural principles.

Unlike previous investigations, which evaluate representational capability, this investigation evaluates the internal coherence of the architecture itself.

---

# Research Question

Does the current formulation of FARA contain any internal contradiction?

---

# Hypothesis

The current architecture is internally consistent.

Every concept, dependency, and architectural principle should coexist without contradiction.

---

# Relationship to Previous Investigations

Validation Investigation VI-001 demonstrated cross-domain representational capability.

Validation Investigation VI-002 investigated primitive reduction.

Validation Investigation VI-003 established the methodology for independence investigations.

Validation Investigation VI-004 evaluates expressive power.

Validation Investigation VI-005 evaluates expressive boundaries.

This investigation evaluates the logical consistency of the architecture itself.

---

# Methodology

Every canonical architectural artifact shall be examined.

The investigation shall evaluate:

1. canonical definitions;
2. primitive relationships;
3. derived concepts;
4. architectural documents;
5. dependency structure;
6. validation investigations;
7. methodological assumptions.

Each artifact shall be compared against every other artifact for consistency.

---

# Consistency Criteria

The investigation shall identify:

- contradictory definitions;
- circular definitions;
- circular dependencies;
- incompatible architectural principles;
- category collapses;
- inconsistent terminology;
- contradictory validation results;
- unresolved ambiguity.

# Success Criteria

The investigation succeeds if every identified inconsistency is either:

- resolved;
- demonstrated to be non-contradictory; or
- explicitly documented as an open research problem.

---

# Failure Criteria

The investigation fails if unresolved contradictions remain within the accepted architecture.

---

# Expected Outcomes

The investigation should establish:

- the internal consistency of the current architecture;
- remaining unresolved contradictions;
- architectural revisions required before future theoretical development.
