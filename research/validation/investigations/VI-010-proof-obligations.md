# Validation Investigation

## Investigation ID

VI-010

---

## Title

Proof Obligations

---

## Status

Research

---

# Purpose

This investigation identifies every formal claim made by the Foundational Architecture of Reasoning Analysis (FARA) that requires proof.

Its objective is to establish a complete inventory of outstanding proof obligations and their current evidential status.

This investigation does not attempt to prove those claims.

It identifies what remains to be proven.

---

# Research Question

What formal claims of FARA remain unproven?

---

# Hypothesis

Every significant architectural claim can be explicitly associated with one or more proof obligations.

No accepted architectural claim should exist without its corresponding proof obligations being identified.

---

# Relationship to Previous Investigations

Previous validation investigations evaluated:

- representational capability;
- primitive reduction;
- primitive independence methodology;
- expressive power;
- expressive boundaries;
- internal consistency;
- architectural sufficiency;
- universality;
- architectural completeness.

This investigation identifies the formal work that remains before stronger conclusions may be accepted.

---

# Definition of a Proof Obligation

A proof obligation is a formally stated claim that must be demonstrated before a theoretical result may be regarded as established.

Failure to satisfy a proof obligation does not invalidate the theory.

It identifies incomplete justification.

---

# Methodology

The investigation shall:

1. enumerate every explicit architectural claim;
2. identify every implicit architectural claim;
3. determine whether each claim has already been proven;
4. identify the remaining proof obligations;
5. classify each obligation according to its current status.

---

# Categories of Proof Obligations

Proof obligations may include, but are not limited to:

- primitive independence;
- primitive minimality;
- architectural sufficiency;
- architectural completeness;
- universality;
- representational equivalence;
- preservation under transformation;
- admissibility preservation;
- dependency correctness;
- consistency of canonical definitions.

Additional proof obligations may be added as research progresses.

---

# Evaluation Criteria

Each proof obligation shall receive one of the following classifications.

**Satisfied**

A complete proof has been accepted.

**Partially Satisfied**

Supporting evidence exists, but a complete proof has not been established.

**Unsatisfied**

No satisfactory proof currently exists.

**Unknown**

The proof status has not yet been determined.
