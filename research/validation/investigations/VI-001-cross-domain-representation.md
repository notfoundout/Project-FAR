# Validation Investigation

## Investigation ID

VI-001

---

## Title

Cross-Domain Representation

---

## Status

Research

---

## Purpose

This investigation evaluates whether the current FARA architecture can represent substantially different reasoning processes without introducing additional architectural concepts.

The investigation tests representational adequacy rather than the correctness of any domain-specific conclusion.

---

## Research Question

Can FARA represent mathematical proof, scientific investigation, legal reasoning, and software debugging using only its existing candidate primitives and derived concepts?

---

## Hypothesis

The current FARA architecture is sufficient to represent the selected reasoning domains without ad hoc architectural expansion.

---

## Attempted Falsification

This investigation attempts to falsify the hypothesis by selecting reasoning domains with different methods, standards, artifacts, and resolution procedures.

A failure in any domain may indicate that FARA lacks expressive power, relies on hidden assumptions, or requires additional architectural distinctions.

---

## Methodology

For each domain, the investigation shall identify:

1. the investigation;
2. the representations;
3. the representational structure;
4. the interpretation;
5. the reasoning calculus;
6. the reasoning states;
7. the transition signatures;
8. the candidates;
9. the admissibility structure (Ω);
10. the resolution rule;
11. the resolution;
12. any architectural deficiency.

No additional architectural concepts may be introduced during execution.

If an additional concept appears necessary, it must be recorded as a deficiency rather than silently incorporated.

---

## Test Cases

The initial test cases are:

- mathematical proof;
- scientific investigation;
- legal reasoning;
- software debugging.
