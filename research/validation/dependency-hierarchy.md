# Dependency Hierarchy

## Purpose

Determine whether the dependency classes identified within Project FAR possess hierarchical relationships.

The objective is to establish whether some dependency types imply others or whether every dependency type is logically independent.

---

# Motivation

Previous investigations identified several dependency classes:

- Logical
- Definitional
- Ontological
- Methodological
- Operational

The relationships among these dependency classes remain unknown.

---

# Central Question

Are dependency classes independent?

---

# Candidate Relationship 1

Definitional ⇒ Logical

Question:

Can a definition depend upon another concept without logically depending upon it?

Evaluation:

If Concept B is defined using Concept A, understanding B presupposes understanding A.

Logical dependence therefore appears unavoidable.

Result:

Provisionally Supported.

---

# Candidate Relationship 2

Ontological ⇒ Logical

Question:

Can one entity require another for existence without logically depending upon it?

Evaluation:

Existence presupposition appears to imply logical presupposition.

Logical dependence therefore appears weaker than ontological dependence.

Result:

Provisionally Supported.

---

# Candidate Relationship 3

Operational ⇒ Methodological

Question:

Does operational dependence necessarily imply methodological dependence?

Evaluation:

Operations may occur outside organized methodologies.

Methodological dependence therefore appears stronger.

Result:

Not Supported.

---

# Candidate Relationship 4

Methodological ⇒ Operational

Question:

Can a methodology progress without operations?

Evaluation:

Methodological progression requires execution.

Execution therefore appears necessary.

Result:

Provisionally Supported.

---

# Candidate Relationship 5

Logical ⇒ Definitional

Question:

Does logical dependence imply definitional dependence?

Evaluation:

No.

Two concepts may be logically related without one defining the other.

Result:

Rejected.

---

# Candidate Relationship 6

Logical ⇒ Ontological

Question:

Does logical dependence imply ontological dependence?

Evaluation:

No.

Logical dependence concerns conceptual coherence rather than existence.

Result:

Rejected.

---

# Preliminary Hierarchy

Logical dependency appears to be the weakest dependency class.

Definitional and ontological dependency appear to strengthen logical dependency.

Methodological dependency appears to require operational dependency.

No reverse implications have been identified.

---

The relationships between these two groups remain unresolved.

---

# Consequences

If dependency classes form a hierarchy rather than a flat taxonomy:

- dependency reasoning becomes more precise;
- some dependency claims become automatically derivable;
- redundancy within the dependency graph is reduced.

---

# Decision Criteria

A dependency hierarchy is accepted only if every implication survives independent investigation.

No implication shall be accepted solely because it appears intuitive.

---

# Provisional Conclusion

Current evidence suggests that dependency classes are not independent.

Some dependency classes appear to imply weaker forms of dependency.

Further investigation is required before the hierarchy is accepted.

---

# Remaining Questions

Future investigations should determine:

- whether the proposed hierarchy is complete;
- whether additional dependency classes exist;
- whether dependency implication is transitive;
- whether dependency classes admit formal algebraic properties.

---

# Current Status

The investigation remains active.

The relationships among dependency classes have not yet been fully established.
