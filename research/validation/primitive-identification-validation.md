# Primitive Identification Validation

## Purpose

This investigation evaluates the primitive identification methodology by applying it to previously proposed primitive concepts.

Its objective is to determine whether the methodology consistently identifies the same primitive concepts without relying upon prior conclusions.

---

# Validation Objective

A methodology should produce the same result regardless of whether the conclusion is already known.

This investigation therefore ignores previous conclusions and reapplies the primitive identification procedure from first principles.

# Analysis

The methodology reproduces the conclusions reached during the FARO investigations.

Every investigated concept except operation remains reducible.

Operation remains the only surviving candidate primitive.

---

# Validation Criteria

The methodology is considered successful if:

- reducible concepts are consistently eliminated;
- irreducible concepts consistently survive;
- no contradictions are introduced.

---

# Current Conclusion

The primitive identification methodology successfully reproduces the current theoretical structure of Project FAR.

Further validation shall be performed whenever new candidate primitives are proposed.
