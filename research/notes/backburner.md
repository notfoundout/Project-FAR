# Backburner

## Purpose

This document records ideas, investigations, and potential improvements that are intentionally deferred.

Items listed here are not part of the current research program but may become relevant as Project FAR develops.

Unlike `research/open-problems/open-questions.md`, which records active research, the Backburner records ideas that have been postponed.

---

# Formal Theory

## Primitive vs. Derived Definitions

Investigate whether the canonical definitions should eventually distinguish between primitive concepts and derived concepts after formal derivations have been established.

**Reason for Deferral**

The distinction should not be introduced until the relevant derivations have been formally proved.

---

## Confidence, Uncertainty, and Degrees of Support

Determine whether confidence, uncertainty, or degrees of support deserve formal treatment within Project FAR.

Questions include:

- Are these primitive or derived concepts?
- Are they properties of representations?
- Are they properties of reasoning states?
- Can they be represented independently of probability?

**Reason for Deferral**

Current work focuses on establishing the core architecture before extending the framework.

---

## Formal Dependency Graph

Investigate whether the formal theory would benefit from an automatically generated dependency graph linking definitions, axioms, propositions, theorems, and proofs.

**Reason for Deferral**

The formal theory is currently too small to justify maintaining such a graph manually.

---

## Formal Statement Identifiers

Evaluate whether stable symbolic identifiers should eventually be assigned to every formal statement.

Examples:

```text
D-REPRESENTATION
A-CONSISTENCY
P-INVESTIGATION-RELATIVITY
T-UNIVERSAL-ARCHITECTURE
```

**Reason for Deferral**

The identifier scheme should be adopted only after the formal theory becomes sufficiently stable.

# Review Policy

Items should remain on the Backburner until:

- They become active research questions.
- They are incorporated into the framework.
- They are rejected as unnecessary.

The Backburner should be reviewed periodically to determine whether deferred investigations should be promoted to the active research program.
