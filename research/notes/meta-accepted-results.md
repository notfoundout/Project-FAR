# Meta-Theory Accepted Results

## Purpose

This document records research results that have survived investigation and presently guide development of the Meta-Theory.

Acceptance within this document does not imply canonical status.

Rather, accepted results constitute the current working foundation pending formal incorporation into the canonical theory.

---

# Accepted Result 1

## Primitive Status is Theory-Relative

Current investigations support the conclusion that primitive status is relative to a formal theory.

A concept may be primitive within one theory while remaining derivable within another.

Accordingly, every claim of primitiveness must specify the theory under consideration.

This result remains subject to future revision.

---

# Accepted Result 2

## Primitive Status Requires Objective Criteria

Primitive status cannot be determined solely by declaration.

Current investigations support objective criteria including:

- irreducibility;
- necessity;
- independence; and
- foundationality.

The precise characterization remains under investigation.

---

# Accepted Result 3

## Universal Properties Precede Primitive Architecture

The investigation of primitive architecture depends upon prior identification of universal properties.

Accordingly, universal properties should be investigated before primitive reduction.
