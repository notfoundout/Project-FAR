# Research Methodology Notes

## Purpose

This document records the research methodology employed throughout Project FAR.

Its purpose is to distinguish established proof techniques from active methodological research.

Unlike the canonical theory, this document concerns how theoretical claims are investigated rather than the claims themselves.

---

# Guiding Principles

The methodology of Project FAR is governed by the following principles.

- Definitions precede conclusions.
- Primitive concepts should be minimized whenever possible.
- Reduction is preferred over expansion.
- Every significant assumption should be made explicit.
- Every proposed result should be subjected to attempted falsification.
- Methodological assumptions should themselves remain open to investigation.

---

# Current Methodologies

## Reduction

Investigate whether a concept can be derived from more fundamental concepts.

---

## Model Separation

Attempt to construct two models that agree on every defining component except the concept under investigation.

If successful, model separation may establish that the concept is not uniquely determined by the remaining architecture.

The logical foundations of this methodology remain under investigation.

---

## Counterexample Construction

Attempt to invalidate proposed definitions, derivations, or proofs by constructing explicit counterexamples.

---

## Hidden Assumption Analysis

Identify assumptions introduced without explicit justification.

---

## Dependency Analysis

Determine the explicit logical dependencies of every theoretical result.

---

# Active Methodological Research

Current methodological questions include:

- Does model separation establish non-derivability?
- Under what conditions is model separation applicable?
- Do all primitive concepts admit the same proof strategy?
- Can proof strategies themselves be formally justified?

---

# Relationship to Theory

Methodological investigations should not be regarded as established theory.

They exist to improve the reliability and rigor of future theoretical results.
