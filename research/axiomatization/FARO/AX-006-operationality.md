# FARO Axiomatization

## Identifier

AX-006

---

# Title

Operationality

---

## Status

Draft

---

# Purpose

Determine the necessary and sufficient conditions under which an entity possesses operationality.

The objective is to identify the primitive characteristic underlying every reasoning operation.

---

# Central Question

What distinguishes an operational entity from a non-operational entity?

---

# Dependencies

- AX-001 through AX-005

---

# Background

Previous investigations attempted to classify operations as events.

However, classifying operations does not explain what makes them operational.

This investigation therefore examines operationality itself.

---

# Candidate 1

Operationality consists in change.

Evaluation

Insufficient.

Some operations preserve the reasoning state.

Operationality therefore cannot be identified solely with change.

Status

Rejected.

---

# Candidate 2

Operationality consists in execution.

Evaluation

Potentially circular.

Execution presupposes operation.

Status

Rejected pending clarification.

---

# Candidate 3

Operationality consists in causation.

Evaluation

Insufficient.

Many causal events are unrelated to reasoning.

Status

Rejected.

---

# Candidate 4

Operationality consists in producing an operational effect within an investigation.

Evaluation

Potentially circular.

Operational effect presupposes operationality.

Status

Rejected pending clarification.

---

# Observation

Every attempted definition presupposes an operational concept somewhere in its explanation.

Current evidence suggests that operationality may itself be primitive.

---

# Competing Hypotheses

## H1

Operationality is reducible.

A more fundamental concept remains undiscovered.

---

## H2

Operationality is primitive.

Every operational theory necessarily presupposes it.

---

# Evaluation

No successful reduction has yet been identified.

Every candidate explanation ultimately invokes another operational concept.

---

# Decision Criteria

Operationality is primitive if every attempted reduction presupposes operationality.

Operationality is reducible only if a complete explanation exists without operational vocabulary.

---

# Provisional Conclusion

Current evidence favors treating operationality as primitive.

Operation would then be defined as an entity possessing operationality rather than operationality being derived from operation.

No final conclusion has been reached.

---

# Remaining Questions

Future investigations should determine:

- whether operationality admits formal axioms;
- whether operationality possesses invariants;
- whether operationality can be recognized independently of reasoning;
- whether operationality exists outside reasoning altogether.

---

# Current Status

The investigation remains active.

Operationality remains the deepest unresolved concept identified during the FARO axiomatization program.
