# FARO Axiomatization

## Identifier

AX-005

---

# Title

Reasoning Events

---

## Status

Draft

---

# Purpose

Determine what distinguishes reasoning events from arbitrary events.

The objective is to establish whether operation can be identified with a special class of events.

---

# Central Question

What makes an event a reasoning event?

---

# Dependencies

- AX-001 — Primitive Operation
- AX-002 — Properties of Operation
- AX-003 — Property Independence
- AX-004 — Ontology of Operation

---

# Background

AX-004 provisionally classified operations as events.

This investigation asks whether that classification is sufficient.

---

# Candidate Event 1

A rock falls.

Question:

Is this a reasoning event?

Evaluation:

No.

Although an event occurs, nothing participates in reasoning.

Result:

Rejected.

---

# Candidate Event 2

A computer copies a file.

Question:

Is this a reasoning event?

Evaluation:

Not necessarily.

The event manipulates information without necessarily participating in reasoning.

Result:

Rejected.

---

# Candidate Event 3

An investigator evaluates a hypothesis.

Question:

Is this a reasoning event?

Evaluation:

Yes.

The event participates directly in an investigation.

Result:

Supported.

---

# Candidate Event 4

A theorem prover applies an inference rule.

Question:

Is this a reasoning event?

Evaluation:

Yes.

The event contributes to reasoning progression.

Result:

Supported.

---

# Pattern Analysis

Not every event is a reasoning event.

The investigated reasoning events possess a common characteristic.

Each contributes to the progression of an investigation.

Events unrelated to investigation fail to qualify.

---

# Emerging Hypothesis

A reasoning event is an event whose execution contributes to the progression of an investigation.

The contribution may:

- preserve the current reasoning state;
- transform the reasoning state;
- establish a result;
- reject a possibility;
- verify a conclusion.

The specific operational effect is secondary.

Participation in investigation progression is primary.

---

# Competing Hypotheses

## H1

Operations are simply events.

---

## H2

Operations are reasoning events.

Reasoning events form a proper subset of all events.

---

# Evaluation

Current evidence favors H2.

Operations appear distinguishable from arbitrary events by their participation in reasoning.

---

# Decision Criteria

If every operation is a reasoning event while many events are not operations, operation belongs to the class of reasoning events rather than the broader class of events.

---

# Provisional Conclusion

Current evidence suggests that operations are reasoning events.

The defining feature of a reasoning event is its participation in the progression of an investigation.

---

# Remaining Questions

Future investigations should determine:

- whether participation in investigation progression is sufficient;
- whether reasoning events possess identity conditions;
- whether reasoning events admit composition;
- whether reasoning events require explicit operational effects.

---

# Current Status

The investigation remains active.

Current evidence favors classifying operations as reasoning events rather than events in general.
