# FARO Axiomatization

## Identifier

AX-004

---

# Title

Ontology of Operation

---

## Status

Draft

---

# Purpose

Determine the ontological category occupied by operation within FARO.

The objective is to establish what an operation fundamentally is before attempting to axiomatize its properties.

---

# Central Question

What kind of entity is an operation?

---

# Dependencies

- AX-001 — Primitive Operation
- AX-002 — Properties of Operation
- AX-003 — Property Independence

---

# Background

Previous investigations suggest that operation may be the unique primitive introduced by FARO.

Before identifying axioms governing operation, its ontological category must first be established.

---

# Candidate 1

Operation as Object.

Question:

Is an operation simply another object within FARA?

Evaluation:

Objects exist independently.

Operations appear to occur.

Objects and occurrences appear conceptually distinct.

Status:

Not supported.

---

# Candidate 2

Operation as Relation.

Question:

Is an operation merely a relation between reasoning states?

Evaluation:

Relations describe connections.

Operations appear to produce or realize those connections.

Status:

Not supported.

---

# Candidate 3

Operation as Event.

Question:

Is an operation fundamentally an event?

Evaluation:

Operations occur at identifiable moments during reasoning.

Events naturally capture occurrence.

However, not every event constitutes reasoning.

Status:

Promising.

---

# Candidate 4

Operation as Process.

Question:

Is an operation itself a process?

Evaluation:

Processes consist of multiple events or stages.

Many operations appear atomic.

Status:

Not supported.

---

# Candidate 5

Operation as Transformation.

Question:

Is operation identical to transformation?

Evaluation:

Some operations preserve the reasoning state.

Transformation therefore cannot characterize every operation.

Status:

Rejected.

---

# Pattern Analysis

Current evidence suggests that operations are occurrences rather than static entities.

They are neither objects nor relations.

They appear most closely aligned with events executed within an investigation.

---

# Emerging Hypothesis

Operation is a primitive event occurring within a reasoning process.

The event may:

- preserve the reasoning state;
- transform the reasoning state;
- branch reasoning;
- terminate reasoning;
- produce other operational effects.

The effects are secondary.

The event itself is primitive.

---

# Decision Criteria

If every reasoning operation is an event while not every event is a reasoning operation, operation belongs to the ontological category of events with additional operational constraints.

---

# Provisional Conclusion

Current evidence favors treating operation as a primitive operational event.

Operational effects derive from events rather than defining them.

---

# Remaining Questions

Future investigations should determine:

- whether operations possess internal structure;
- whether operations are composable;
- whether operations admit identity conditions;
- whether operations can be formally typed.

---

# Current Status

The ontology of operation remains under investigation.

Current evidence favors classifying operation as a primitive event.
