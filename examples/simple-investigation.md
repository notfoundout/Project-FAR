# Example — Simple Investigation

## Purpose

This example demonstrates the complete FAR methodology using a minimal investigation.

The objective is to illustrate the architectural components of Project FAR rather than the complexity of the reasoning itself.

---

# Investigation

## Question

Should an umbrella be carried today?

---

## Objective

Determine whether carrying an umbrella is justified based on the available information.

---

# Representations

The investigation begins with the following representations.

- Weather forecast predicts rain.
- Current sky is overcast.
- Umbrella is available.

---

# Representational Structure

The representations are organized according to their relevance to the investigation.

Environmental conditions support evaluating the possibility of rain.

Umbrella availability determines whether carrying one is feasible.

---

# Interpretation

The weather forecast is interpreted as evidence regarding future weather.

The overcast sky is interpreted as additional supporting evidence.

Umbrella availability is interpreted as an available action.

---

# Reasoning Calculus

The investigation employs ordinary practical reasoning.

Evidence supporting rain increases the justification for carrying an umbrella.

---

# Initial Reasoning State

The initial reasoning state consists of the available representations before any conclusion has been reached.

---

# Transformations

The reasoning process proceeds through the following transformations.

1. Consider the weather forecast.
2. Consider the observed sky conditions.
3. Combine both sources of evidence.
4. Evaluate the available actions.

Each transformation produces a new reasoning state.

---

# Candidates

The investigation admits two candidates.

- Carry an umbrella.
- Do not carry an umbrella.

---

# Admissibility Structure (Ω)

Candidate | Admissibility
--- | ---
Carry an umbrella | Admissible
Do not carry an umbrella | Not Admissible

The admissibility classifications are determined according to the reasoning calculus applied during the investigation.

---

# Resolution Rule

Select the admissible candidate that best satisfies the objective of the investigation.

---

# Resolution

Carry an umbrella.

---

# Discussion

This example illustrates the complete reasoning process using every major architectural component of Project FAR.

Although the investigation is intentionally simple, the same methodology scales to substantially more complex reasoning domains.
