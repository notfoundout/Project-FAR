# Canonicalization Standard (CS)

## Purpose

This document defines the conditions under which a result becomes part of the canonical Project FAR framework.

No result shall be regarded as canonical until it satisfies this standard.

---

# Eligible Results

The following artifacts may become canonical:

- Definitions
- Investigations
- Constructions
- Proofs
- Principles
- Theorems
- Formal Objects

---

# Canonical Acceptance Requirements

A result becomes canonical only if all of the following conditions are satisfied.

---

## Requirement 1 — Explicit Specification

The result shall be stated unambiguously.

---

## Requirement 2 — Accepted Dependencies

Every dependency shall already be canonical.

---

## Requirement 3 — Successful Construction

If the result requires construction,

its construction shall be complete.

---

## Requirement 4 — Successful Proof

If the result makes a formal claim,

that claim shall satisfy the Canonical Proof Standard.

---

## Requirement 5 — Dependency Verification

Every dependency shall appear within the Canonical Dependency Graph.

---

## Requirement 6 — Reconstruction

An independent investigator shall be capable of reproducing the result using only accepted dependencies.

---

## Requirement 7 — Consistency

The result shall not contradict any canonical result.

---

## Requirement 8 — Reduction

No simpler equivalent formulation shall presently exist.

---

# Canonical Status

A result satisfying every requirement shall be assigned the status:

Canonical.

Until then,

its status remains one of:

- Proposed
- Under Investigation
- Under Construction
- Provisionally Proven

---

# Revision

Canonical status is not irrevocable.

Discovery of:

- contradiction;
- hidden assumptions;
- circularity;
- successful counterexample;

immediately reopens the result for investigation.

Canonical status is suspended until resolution.
