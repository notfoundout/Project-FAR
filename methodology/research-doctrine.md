# Project FAR Research Doctrine

## Status

Canonical Methodology

## Purpose

This document records the governing research doctrine for Project FAR.

It does not define canonical theory.

It defines the standards that any candidate theory, definition, primitive, axiom, lemma, theorem, framework component, or methodology revision must satisfy before promotion.

## Core Doctrine

Project FAR does not exist to defend FAR, FARA, FARO, or any previously proposed architecture.

Project FAR exists to discover the structure of explicit reasoning under explicitly stated scope and assumptions.

If the existing framework is wrong, incomplete, non-minimal, or replaceable, the methodology must expose that rather than conceal it.

## Meta-Principles

### 1. Truth Over Framework Preservation

The objective is not to preserve the current framework.

The objective is to discover what is true under stated assumptions.

### 2. Explicit Everything

Every definition, assumption, scope boundary, dependency, inference, proof obligation, uncertainty, and limitation shall be explicit.

No hidden premise may be treated as established.

### 3. Minimum Commitment

Project FAR shall accept only the weakest claim justified by the current evidence, proof, and assumptions.

Claims shall not be strengthened for elegance, ambition, simplicity, rhetorical force, or framework preservation.

### 4. Earn Every Concept

No concept, primitive, axiom, definition, theorem, or framework component is accepted because it is useful, intuitive, familiar, elegant, or previously proposed.

Every accepted component must justify its role.

### 5. Complete Traceability

Every accepted result shall have a justification chain.

It must be possible to identify:

- what the result means;
- why it is included;
- what assumptions it uses;
- what definitions it depends on;
- what proof or evidence supports it;
- what could falsify or revise it;
- what later results depend on it.

### 6. Permanent Revisability

No result is immune from revision.

Definitions, primitives, axioms, proofs, theorems, methodology rules, and framework components may be revised if stronger analysis requires it.

Revision shall be explicit and traceable.

### 7. Self-Application

The standards used to evaluate reasoning shall themselves be available for evaluation wherever applicable.

Project FAR's research process is itself subject to Project FAR's standards of explicitness, traceability, falsification, and revision.

## Promotion Hierarchy

Claims shall not skip maturity levels.

The standard promotion sequence is:

1. Observation
2. Question
3. Candidate
4. Hypothesis
5. Conjecture
6. Research Result
7. Lemma
8. Corollary
9. Theorem
10. Principle
11. Framework Component

Promotion requires satisfying the applicable acceptance requirements for the target level.

## Acceptance Requirements

A claim may be accepted only to the extent that it satisfies all applicable requirements:

- precise definitions;
- explicit scope;
- explicit assumptions;
- internal consistency;
- necessity analysis where applicable;
- sufficiency analysis where applicable;
- reduction analysis;
- independence analysis;
- replaceability analysis;
- counterexample analysis;
- minimal-model analysis where applicable;
- alternative-model analysis where applicable;
- proof or explicit proof limitation;
- dependency traceability;
- revision traceability;
- boundary specification;
- failure characterization.

Failure to refute a claim is not proof.

Acceptance requires positive justification under stated assumptions.

## Rejection Requirements

A claim shall be rejected, revised, or demoted if any of the following is established:

- contradiction;
- circular dependence;
- unjustified hidden assumption;
- overstated scope;
- stronger conclusion than the evidence supports;
- successful counterexample;
- redundancy;
- derivability from weaker accepted results;
- lack of unique explanatory contribution;
- incompatibility with accepted results under the same assumptions.

## Research Invariants

The following invariants shall be preserved during Project FAR research:

- no accepted theorem depends on an unproven conjecture;
- every accepted statement has a justification chain;
- every theorem states its assumptions;
- every theorem states its scope;
- every proof exposes its dependencies;
- every primitive remains open to reduction and replacement unless proven otherwise;
- every negative result is preserved;
- every open problem is tracked;
- every revision records affected downstream results;
- every dependency graph is acyclic unless recursion is intentional and explicitly analyzed.

## Methodology Revision

This doctrine is itself revisable.

A methodology revision may be proposed when a missing failure mode, ambiguity, circularity, or practical defect is discovered.

A revision to methodology does not retroactively validate earlier accepted results.

Previously accepted results must be re-evaluated if the new methodology imposes stronger requirements relevant to those results.

## Governing Standard

Every accepted statement in Project FAR must be the weakest statement that survives explicit definition, stated assumptions, rigorous proof attempts, systematic attempts at refutation, dependency analysis, and continuous re-evaluation under the current methodology.
