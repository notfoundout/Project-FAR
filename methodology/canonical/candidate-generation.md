# Canonical Candidate Generation Procedure (CCGP)

## Purpose

This document defines the objective procedure for proposing candidate concepts within Project FAR.

No candidate concept shall enter evaluation unless generated according to this procedure.

---

# Fundamental Principle

Candidate concepts shall not be proposed by intuition.

Every candidate shall arise from an objectively demonstrated deficiency in the accepted framework.

---

# Candidate Generation Procedure

## Step 1

Attempt construction using only accepted results.

---

## Step 2

If construction succeeds,

no new candidate is generated.

---

## Step 3

If construction fails,

identify the precise point of failure.

---

## Step 4

Attempt to resolve the failure using only accepted concepts.

---

## Step 5

If resolution succeeds,

no new candidate is generated.

---

## Step 6

If every accepted concept fails,

identify the minimum capability absent from the framework.

Only this capability becomes a candidate.

---

# Acceptance Criterion

A proposed candidate is admissible only if:

- it resolves the demonstrated failure;
- no weaker candidate resolves the failure;
- no accepted concept already provides the required capability.

---

# Consequences

Failure alone does not justify a new concept.

Only demonstrated necessity justifies a new concept.
