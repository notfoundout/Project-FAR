# Canonical Primitive Evaluation Framework (CPEF)

## Purpose

This framework defines the objective procedure for determining the primitive basis of any domain.

It applies equally to:

- Root primitives
- Formal objects
- Formal principles
- Operations
- Relations
- Structures
- Future Project FAR extensions

---

## Phase 1 — Candidate Identification

Identify every objectively characterizable candidate primitive.

Unknown candidates are not evaluated until objectively proposed.

---

## Phase 2 — Independent Characterization

Attempt to characterize every candidate independently.

Reject candidates requiring stronger primitives.

---

## Phase 3 — Comparative Evaluation

Evaluate every surviving candidate according to:

- Independence
- Necessity
- Minimality
- Sufficiency
- Non-Circularity
- Reconstructability

---

## Phase 4 — Elimination

Reject every candidate failing any criterion.

---

## Phase 5 — Reduction

Merge equivalent candidates.

Eliminate reducible candidates.

Repeat until no further reduction succeeds.

---

## Phase 6 — Reconstruction

Attempt independent reconstruction using only the surviving primitive basis.

---

## Phase 7 — Falsification

Attempt to identify:

- missing primitives;
- redundant primitives;
- alternative primitive bases.

---

## Phase 8 — Canonicalization

Accept the primitive basis only if:

- exactly one objectively justified basis survives;
- no successful reduction exists;
- no successful falsification exists.
