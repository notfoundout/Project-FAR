# Canonical Proof Standard (CPS)

## Purpose

This document defines the requirements for establishing the validity of any proposition, construction, theorem, or formal object within Project FAR.

No result becomes canonical until it satisfies the Canonical Proof Standard.

---

# Scope

The Canonical Proof Standard applies to:

- Definitions
- Constructions
- Propositions
- Theorems
- Principles
- Formal Objects

---

# Fundamental Principle

A derivation and a proof are distinct.

A derivation specifies how a result is obtained.

A proof establishes that the derivation is valid.

Accordingly,

no derivation shall be regarded as valid until successfully proven.

---

# Proof Requirements

Every proof shall satisfy all of the following requirements.

---

## Requirement 1 — Explicit Proposition

The proposition being proved shall be stated unambiguously.

Its intended meaning shall not change during the proof.

---

## Requirement 2 — Accepted Dependencies

Every dependency shall already be accepted.

Dependencies may include:

- Definitions
- Accepted Results
- Previously Proven Theorems
- Accepted Constructions
- Accepted Methodological Principles

No future result may be cited.

---

## Requirement 3 — Explicit Derivation

Every step of the derivation shall be explicitly stated.

No implicit reasoning is permitted.

Every step shall identify the accepted result upon which it depends.

---

## Requirement 4 — Dependency Traceability

Every dependency appearing within the derivation shall be explicitly identified.

No hidden dependency is permitted.

Discovery of an undeclared dependency immediately suspends the proof.

---

## Requirement 5 — Non-Circularity

Neither the proposition nor any dependency may depend directly or indirectly upon the proposition being proved.

Circular justification invalidates the proof.

---

## Requirement 6 — Hidden Assumption Audit

Every assumption shall be explicitly identified.

Each assumption shall either:

- already be accepted; or
- become the subject of a new investigation.

No unidentified assumption is permitted.

---

## Requirement 7 — Counterexample Audit

Attempt to construct a counterexample.

A successful counterexample invalidates the proof.

Failure to identify a counterexample does not by itself establish correctness.

---

## Requirement 8 — Reduction Audit

Attempt to remove every step of the derivation.

If a step can be removed without affecting validity,

that step shall be eliminated.

The proof shall contain no redundant steps.

---

## Requirement 9 — Reconstruction Audit

An independent investigator using only the accepted dependencies shall be capable of reproducing the proof.

Failure of reconstruction invalidates the proof.

---

## Requirement 10 — Consistency Audit

The proved result shall not contradict any accepted result.

Any contradiction suspends canonical acceptance until resolved.

---

# Canonical Acceptance

A result becomes canonical only if:

- every proof requirement is satisfied;
- every dependency is accepted;
- every dependency is explicit;
- no hidden assumptions remain;
- no circularity exists;
- no contradiction exists;
- independent reconstruction succeeds.

---

# Failure Conditions

A proof immediately fails if:

- an undeclared dependency is discovered;
- circular reasoning is identified;
- hidden assumptions remain;
- a successful counterexample is demonstrated;
- reconstruction fails;
- contradiction with accepted results is established.

---

# Consequences

Successful completion of the Canonical Proof Standard establishes only the validity of the demonstrated proposition.

It does not establish the truth of any proposition not explicitly proved.

Every subsequent proof shall depend only upon previously accepted results.
