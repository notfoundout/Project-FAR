# Methodology Audit Protocol

## Status
Canonical Methodology

## Purpose

This protocol evaluates the research methodology itself after every foundational investigation.

A candidate investigation and the methodology governing it are evaluated independently.

## Principle

An investigation may fail because:

- the candidate is incorrect;
- the methodology is incomplete;
- both.

These possibilities shall remain distinct.

## Required Audit

Every foundational investigation shall conclude with a Methodology Audit answering:

1. Did the methodology expose hidden assumptions?
2. Did it prevent circular reasoning?
3. Did it prevent premature conclusions?
4. Were any required tests inapplicable or insufficient?
5. Did a new class of methodological failure emerge?
6. Does the doctrine require revision?
7. If revised, which previous investigations require re-evaluation?

## Outcomes

- No revision required.
- Minor clarification.
- New methodological rule.
- Revision to existing protocol.
- New research program for methodology.

## Rule

Methodology improvements do not retroactively validate previous investigations.

Any investigation materially affected by a methodology revision shall be flagged for re-audit.
