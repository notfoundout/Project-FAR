# Research Methodology Investigation

This document investigates the methodology used to develop formal theories throughout Project FAR.

Its purpose is to establish a complete, objective, and minimal research methodology.

---

# Investigation M1 — Nature of Justification

## Status

In Progress

---

## Research Question

What constitutes the justification of a proposition?

---

## Motivation

Project FAR evaluates hypotheses, promotes accepted results, and rejects unsupported claims.

Each of these activities presupposes a notion of justification.

Accordingly, justification itself requires independent investigation.

---

## Current Hypothesis

Justification consists in satisfying objective criteria independent of personal belief or authority.

This hypothesis remains provisional.

---

## Candidate Characterizations

Current candidates include:

- Logical proof
- Empirical evidence
- Explanatory power
- Internal consistency
- Necessary consequence
- Successful falsification attempts
- Minimal assumptions

No characterization has yet been accepted.

---

## Investigation Strategy

Evaluate each candidate independently.

Reject every characterization that fails to distinguish justified propositions from unjustified propositions.

---

## Current Assessment

Under Investigation.

# Investigation M2 — Nature of Termination

## Status

In Progress

---

## Research Question

What constitutes the completion of an investigation?

---

## Motivation

Project FAR progresses through structured investigations.

However, no objective criterion presently determines when an investigation has been completed.

Accordingly, the nature of investigation termination requires independent investigation.

---

## Current Hypothesis

An investigation is complete when every unresolved question necessary to answer its research question has been resolved or explicitly classified as an open problem.

This hypothesis remains provisional.

---

## Candidate Characterizations

Current candidates include:

- Exhaustion of evidence
- Absence of counterexamples
- Stable conclusions
- Satisfaction of research objectives
- Resolution of every necessary dependency

No characterization has yet been accepted.

---

## Investigation Strategy

Evaluate each candidate independently.

Reject every characterization that either permits premature termination or prevents termination altogether.

---

## Current Assessment

Under Investigation.

---

## Candidate 1 — Exhaustion of Evidence

### Claim

An investigation terminates when no further evidence can be obtained.

### Analysis

It is generally impossible to determine that no further evidence exists.

Accordingly,

this criterion provides no objective termination condition.

### Assessment

Rejected.

---

## Candidate 2 — Absence of Counterexamples

### Claim

An investigation terminates when no counterexamples have been identified.

### Analysis

The absence of known counterexamples does not imply that none exist.

Accordingly,

this criterion cannot justify termination.

### Assessment

Rejected.

---

## Candidate 3 — Stable Conclusions

### Claim

An investigation terminates when its conclusions remain unchanged.

### Analysis

Stability depends upon time rather than logical completion.

A stable conclusion may nevertheless remain unjustified.

### Assessment

Rejected.

---

## Candidate 4 — Satisfaction of Research Objectives

### Claim

An investigation terminates when its research question has been answered.

### Analysis

An apparent answer may rely upon unresolved assumptions.

Accordingly,

answering the research question alone is insufficient.

### Assessment

Provisionally Rejected.

---

## Candidate 5 — Resolution of Every Necessary Dependency

### Claim

An investigation terminates when every dependency necessary to answer the research question has either:

- been resolved;
- been explicitly accepted as an axiom;
- been explicitly classified as an open problem.

### Analysis

This criterion prevents both premature termination and infinite regress.

Every unresolved dependency is made explicit.

No hidden assumptions remain.

### Assessment

Provisionally Accepted.

---

## Interim Conclusion

Current evidence suggests that investigation termination is determined by the status of its necessary dependencies rather than by time, confidence, or the apparent stability of its conclusions.

Further investigation is required.

---

## Counterexample Strategy

Attempt to identify investigations that satisfy the proposed termination criterion despite remaining incomplete.

Attempt to identify investigations that fail the proposed termination criterion despite being complete.

Any successful example requires revision of the criterion.

---

## Hidden Assumptions

Current investigations assume:

- every investigation possesses a determinate research question;
- every necessary dependency can be identified;
- unresolved dependencies can be objectively classified;
- investigations admit objective completion.

Each assumption requires independent investigation.

---

## Dependency Analysis

Depends upon:

- Investigation M1 — Nature of Justification

Supports:

- Investigation M3 — Nature of Acceptance
- Investigation M4 — Nature of Rejection
- Every future Project FAR investigation

---

## Self-Audit

Evaluate the proposed termination criterion against representative cases.

### Case 1

The research question has been answered.

Several hidden assumptions remain unidentified.

Result:

The investigation shall not terminate.

Reason:

Necessary dependencies remain unresolved.

---

### Case 2

Every dependency has been resolved.

One dependency remains intentionally classified as an open problem.

Result:

The investigation may terminate.

Reason:

The unresolved dependency has been explicitly identified and classified.

No hidden assumption remains.

---

### Case 3

The investigation continues indefinitely despite resolving every dependency.

Result:

The criterion rejects indefinite continuation.

Reason:

Once every dependency has been resolved or explicitly classified,

additional investigation no longer contributes to answering the research question.

---

## Stress Test

Suppose a future investigation uncovers a dependency previously believed unnecessary.

Question:

Does this invalidate the completed investigation?

Analysis:

No.

It demonstrates that the dependency analysis was incomplete.

Accordingly,

the investigation may be reopened.

Termination therefore represents the current justified state of investigation rather than irreversible completion.

---

## Provisional Theorem

### Statement

An investigation terminates if and only if every dependency necessary to answer its research question has either:

- been resolved;
- been explicitly accepted as an axiom;
- been explicitly classified as an open problem.

---

### Current Status

Provisionally Accepted.

Further investigation should attempt to refute this theorem.

---

## Open Problems

The following questions remain unresolved.

1. How are necessary dependencies identified?

2. What constitutes an open problem?

3. When is acceptance of an axiom justified?

4. Can dependencies change as investigations progress?

Each question requires independent investigation.

---

## Interim Conclusion

Current evidence suggests that investigation termination is determined by the state of its dependency graph rather than by confidence, elapsed time, apparent stability, or exhaustion of available evidence.

Accordingly,

termination appears to be an objective structural property of investigations rather than a subjective judgment.

# Investigation M3 — Nature of Acceptance

## Status

In Progress

---

## Research Question

What constitutes the acceptance of a proposition?

---

## Motivation

Project FAR promotes propositions from investigation into accepted results.

However, no objective criterion presently determines when such promotion is justified.

Accordingly, the nature of acceptance requires independent investigation.

---

## Current Hypothesis

A proposition is accepted when every known objection has either:

- been resolved;
- been refuted; or
- been explicitly classified as an open problem that does not prevent acceptance.

This hypothesis remains provisional.

---

## Candidate Characterizations

Current candidates include:

- Consensus
- Confidence
- Probability
- Survival of criticism
- Satisfaction of objective acceptance criteria

No characterization has yet been accepted.

---

## Investigation Strategy

Evaluate each candidate independently.

Reject every characterization that permits arbitrary or subjective acceptance.

---

## Current Assessment

Under Investigation.

---

## Candidate 1 — Consensus

### Claim

A proposition is accepted when sufficient agreement exists.

### Analysis

Consensus concerns belief rather than justification.

A proposition may be unanimously accepted despite being false.

Likewise,

a justified proposition may initially receive little agreement.

### Assessment

Rejected.

---

## Candidate 2 — Confidence

### Claim

A proposition is accepted when investigators possess sufficient confidence.

### Analysis

Confidence is psychological.

Justification is methodological.

Accordingly,

confidence cannot determine acceptance.

### Assessment

Rejected.

---

## Candidate 3 — Probability

### Claim

A proposition is accepted when its probability exceeds a specified threshold.

### Analysis

No objective probability threshold has been established.

Furthermore,

many formal propositions are demonstrated rather than assigned probabilities.

### Assessment

Rejected.

---

## Candidate 4 — Survival of Criticism

### Claim

A proposition is accepted if repeated attempts to refute it fail.

### Analysis

Failure to identify objections does not imply their nonexistence.

Accordingly,

survival alone is insufficient.

### Assessment

Provisionally Rejected.

---

## Candidate 5 — Satisfaction of Objective Acceptance Criteria

### Claim

A proposition is accepted when it satisfies every objective criterion applicable to its proposition type.

### Analysis

Different proposition types require different forms of justification.

For example:

- Primitive concepts require Root Theory justification.
- Axioms require satisfaction of axiom-selection criteria.
- Theorems require valid proofs.
- Empirical propositions require empirical evidence.

Acceptance therefore depends upon satisfying the appropriate justification standard rather than a universal procedure.

### Assessment

Provisionally Accepted.

---

## Counterexample Strategy

Attempt to identify:

- an accepted proposition failing its applicable justification criteria;
- an unjustifiably rejected proposition satisfying every applicable criterion.

Either example requires revision.

---

## Hidden Assumptions

Current investigation assumes:

- proposition types can be objectively identified;
- each proposition type possesses objective justification criteria;
- satisfaction of those criteria can be objectively determined.

Each assumption requires independent investigation.

---

## Dependency Analysis

Depends upon:

- Investigation M1 — Nature of Justification
- Investigation M2 — Nature of Termination

Supports:

- Investigation M4 — Nature of Rejection
- Canonicalization of accepted results

---

## Self-Audit

### Case 1

A theorem possesses a complete proof.

Every attempted objection fails.

Result:

Acceptance justified.

---

### Case 2

An axiom satisfies every established axiom-selection criterion.

No unresolved objection prevents its adoption.

Result:

Acceptance justified.

---

### Case 3

A proposition survives criticism solely because no one investigated it.

Result:

Acceptance rejected.

Reason:

Absence of criticism does not constitute justification.

---

## Provisional Theorem

### Statement

A proposition is accepted if and only if it satisfies every objective justification criterion applicable to its proposition type.

### Current Status

Provisionally Accepted.

Further investigation should attempt to refute this theorem.

---

## Open Problems

The following questions remain unresolved.

1. What constitutes a proposition type?

2. How are justification criteria assigned to proposition types?

3. May acceptance be provisional?

4. Under what conditions should accepted propositions be reopened?

Each question requires independent investigation.

---

## Interim Conclusion

Current evidence suggests that acceptance is not determined by belief, consensus, confidence, or the absence of criticism.

Rather,

acceptance appears to consist in satisfying the objective justification criteria appropriate to the proposition under investigation.

# Investigation M4 — Nature of Rejection

## Status

In Progress

---

## Research Question

What constitutes the rejection of a proposition?

---

## Motivation

Project FAR rejects propositions that fail objective standards of investigation.

However, no objective criterion presently determines when rejection is justified.

Accordingly, the nature of rejection requires independent investigation.

---

## Current Hypothesis

A proposition is rejected when it has been demonstrated to fail one or more objective justification criteria applicable to its proposition type.

This hypothesis remains provisional.

---

## Candidate Characterizations

Current candidates include:

- Disagreement
- Failure to obtain evidence
- Failure of justification
- Demonstrated contradiction
- Demonstrated inconsistency

No characterization has yet been accepted.

---

## Investigation Strategy

Evaluate each candidate independently.

Reject every characterization permitting arbitrary or subjective rejection.

---

## Current Assessment

Under Investigation.

---

## Candidate 1 — Disagreement

### Claim

A proposition is rejected when investigators disagree with it.

### Analysis

Disagreement concerns opinion rather than justification.

A justified proposition may initially be controversial.

Likewise,

an unjustified proposition may receive widespread agreement.

### Assessment

Rejected.

---

## Candidate 2 — Failure to Obtain Evidence

### Claim

A proposition is rejected when supporting evidence cannot be obtained.

### Analysis

Absence of evidence may indicate:

- insufficient investigation;
- unavailable evidence;
- falsehood.

These possibilities cannot presently be distinguished.

Accordingly,

failure to obtain evidence alone is insufficient.

### Assessment

Rejected.

---

## Candidate 3 — Failure of Justification

### Claim

A proposition is rejected when it fails one or more objective justification criteria applicable to its proposition type.

### Analysis

This criterion directly complements the acceptance criterion established in Investigation M3.

Failure of applicable justification prevents rational acceptance.

### Assessment

Provisionally Accepted.

---

## Candidate 4 — Demonstrated Contradiction

### Claim

A proposition is rejected whenever a contradiction has been demonstrated.

### Analysis

Contradiction justifies rejection only if contradiction is relevant to the proposition type.

For example,

formal theorems may fail through contradiction,

whereas empirical hypotheses may fail through contrary observation.

Accordingly,

contradiction is not universally applicable.

### Assessment

Provisionally Rejected.

---

## Candidate 5 — Demonstrated Inconsistency

### Claim

A proposition is rejected whenever it is inconsistent with accepted results.

### Analysis

Inconsistency may indicate:

- an error in the proposition;
- an error in previously accepted results.

Accordingly,

inconsistency alone does not determine which proposition should be rejected.

### Assessment

Rejected.

---

## Counterexample Strategy

Attempt to identify:

- a proposition satisfying every applicable justification criterion that nevertheless should be rejected;
- a proposition failing applicable justification criteria that nevertheless should be accepted.

Either example requires revision.

---

## Hidden Assumptions

Current investigation assumes:

- justification failure can be objectively demonstrated;
- proposition types possess objective rejection criteria;
- rejection remains open to future revision.

Each assumption requires independent investigation.

---

## Dependency Analysis

Depends upon:

- Investigation M1 — Nature of Justification
- Investigation M3 — Nature of Acceptance

Supports:

- Canonicalization
- Revision of accepted results
- Future investigations

---

## Self-Audit

### Case 1

A theorem contains an invalid proof.

Result:

Rejection justified.

Reason:

The applicable justification criterion has failed.

---

### Case 2

An empirical proposition is contradicted by reliable evidence.

Result:

Rejection justified.

Reason:

Its applicable justification criterion has failed.

---

### Case 3

A proposition is unpopular but satisfies every applicable justification criterion.

Result:

Rejection not justified.

Reason:

Popularity is not a justification criterion.

---

## Provisional Theorem

### Statement

A proposition is rejected if and only if it demonstrably fails one or more objective justification criteria applicable to its proposition type.

### Current Status

Provisionally Accepted.

Further investigation should attempt to refute this theorem.

---

## Open Problems

The following questions remain unresolved.

1. Should rejection be permanent or provisional?

2. Under what conditions may rejected propositions be reopened?

3. Can two competing propositions both remain unresolved?

4. What distinguishes rejection from suspension of judgment?

Each question requires independent investigation.

---

## Interim Conclusion

Current evidence suggests that rejection is not determined by disagreement, lack of popularity, confidence, or absence of evidence.

Rather,

rejection appears to consist in demonstrable failure to satisfy the objective justification criteria applicable to the proposition under investigation.

# Investigation M5 — Nature of Objections

## Status

In Progress

---

## Research Question

What constitutes a valid objection?

---

## Motivation

Previous investigations repeatedly appealed to objections when evaluating propositions.

However, no investigation has established what qualifies as an objection.

Accordingly, the nature of objections requires independent investigation.

---

## Current Hypothesis

A valid objection identifies a demonstrable deficiency relative to the objective justification criteria applicable to a proposition.

This hypothesis remains provisional.

---

## Candidate Characterizations

Current candidates include:

- Disagreement
- Alternative explanation
- Counterexample
- Logical error
- Failure of justification

No characterization has yet been accepted.

---

## Investigation Strategy

Evaluate each candidate independently.

Reject every characterization permitting arbitrary or subjective objections.

---

## Current Assessment

Under Investigation.

---

## Candidate 1 — Disagreement

### Claim

An objection consists in disagreeing with a proposition.

### Analysis

Disagreement expresses a psychological state.

It need not identify any defect in the proposition.

Accordingly,

disagreement alone does not constitute an objection.

### Assessment

Rejected.

---

## Candidate 2 — Alternative Explanation

### Claim

An objection consists in presenting a different explanation.

### Analysis

An alternative explanation does not itself demonstrate a defect.

Both explanations may remain compatible with available evidence.

Accordingly,

alternative explanations alone are insufficient.

### Assessment

Rejected.

---

## Candidate 3 — Counterexample

### Claim

A valid counterexample constitutes an objection.

### Analysis

A genuine counterexample demonstrates failure of universality.

However,

counterexamples apply only where universal claims are made.

Accordingly,

counterexamples are sufficient in some cases but not universally applicable.

### Assessment

Provisionally Accepted.

---

## Candidate 4 — Logical Error

### Claim

Identification of a logical error constitutes an objection.

### Analysis

Logical errors invalidate arguments.

However,

not every proposition is justified by formal proof.

Accordingly,

logical error is proposition-dependent.

### Assessment

Provisionally Accepted.

---

## Candidate 5 — Failure of Justification

### Claim

A valid objection demonstrates that one or more applicable justification criteria have not been satisfied.

### Analysis

This characterization applies independently of proposition type.

The specific deficiency varies according to the applicable justification criteria.

Accordingly,

this characterization subsumes the previous successful candidates.

### Assessment

Provisionally Accepted.

---

## Counterexample Strategy

Attempt to identify:

- a valid objection that demonstrates no failure of justification;
- a demonstrable failure of justification that does not constitute a valid objection.

Either example requires revision.

---

## Hidden Assumptions

Current investigation assumes:

- justification criteria are objectively identifiable;
- deficiencies can be objectively demonstrated;
- proposition types possess applicable justification standards.

Each assumption requires independent investigation.

---

## Dependency Analysis

Depends upon:

- Investigation M1 — Nature of Justification
- Investigation M3 — Nature of Acceptance
- Investigation M4 — Nature of Rejection

Supports:

- Investigation methodology
- Critical review procedures
- Acceptance and rejection decisions

---

## Self-Audit

### Case 1

An argument contains an invalid inference.

Result:

Valid objection.

Reason:

A justification criterion has failed.

---

### Case 2

An investigator simply disagrees.

Result:

Not a valid objection.

Reason:

No deficiency has been demonstrated.

---

### Case 3

A counterexample falsifies a universal claim.

Result:

Valid objection.

Reason:

The applicable justification criterion has failed.

---

## Provisional Theorem

### Statement

A valid objection consists in demonstrating that a proposition fails one or more objective justification criteria applicable to that proposition.

### Current Status

Provisionally Accepted.

Further investigation should attempt to refute this theorem.

---

## Open Problems

The following questions remain unresolved.

1. Must every objection identify a specific justification criterion?

2. Can multiple objections identify the same deficiency?

3. How should conflicting objections be evaluated?

4. When does an objection become resolved?

Each question requires independent investigation.

---

## Interim Conclusion

Current evidence suggests that objections are not expressions of disagreement or preference.

Rather,

a valid objection consists in demonstrating an objective deficiency relative to the applicable justification criteria governing the proposition under investigation.

# Investigation M6 — Identification of Justification Criteria

## Status

In Progress

---

## Research Question

How are the objective justification criteria applicable to a proposition identified?

---

## Motivation

Previous investigations established that propositions are evaluated according to objective justification criteria.

However, no investigation has established how those criteria are identified.

Accordingly, the identification of justification criteria requires independent investigation.

---

## Current Hypothesis

The applicable justification criteria are determined by the proposition type rather than by investigator preference.

This hypothesis remains provisional.

---

## Candidate Characterizations

Current candidates include:

- Investigator judgment
- Community convention
- Proposition type
- Context
- Universal criteria

No characterization has yet been accepted.

---

## Investigation Strategy

Evaluate each candidate independently.

Reject every characterization permitting arbitrary assignment of justification criteria.

---

## Current Assessment

Under Investigation.

---

## Candidate 1 — Investigator Judgment

### Claim

The investigator determines the applicable justification criteria.

### Analysis

Different investigators may reach different conclusions.

Accordingly,

this characterization permits subjective evaluation.

### Assessment

Rejected.

---

## Candidate 2 — Community Convention

### Claim

The applicable justification criteria are determined by accepted practice.

### Analysis

Community practice may change.

Furthermore,

convention concerns agreement rather than objective justification.

### Assessment

Rejected.

---

## Candidate 3 — Context

### Claim

The applicable justification criteria depend upon context.

### Analysis

Context alone does not determine objective standards.

The same proposition evaluated under identical conditions should possess identical justification criteria.

### Assessment

Rejected.

---

## Candidate 4 — Universal Criteria

### Claim

Every proposition possesses identical justification criteria.

### Analysis

Formal theorems, axioms, primitive concepts, and empirical propositions require different methods of justification.

Accordingly,

a universal criterion appears insufficient.

### Assessment

Rejected.

---

## Candidate 5 — Proposition Type

### Claim

The applicable justification criteria are determined by the proposition type.

### Analysis

Different proposition types possess different epistemic roles.

Accordingly,

different justification standards are expected.

This characterization accounts for the diversity of accepted justification methods while preserving objectivity.

### Assessment

Provisionally Accepted.

---

## Counterexample Strategy

Attempt to identify:

- two propositions of the same type requiring different justification criteria;
- two propositions of different types requiring identical justification criteria despite differing epistemic roles.

Either example requires revision.

---

## Hidden Assumptions

Current investigation assumes:

- proposition types can be objectively identified;
- proposition types possess objective epistemic roles;
- justification criteria can be mapped objectively to proposition types.

Each assumption requires independent investigation.

---

## Dependency Analysis

Depends upon:

- Investigation M1 — Nature of Justification
- Investigation M3 — Nature of Acceptance
- Investigation M4 — Nature of Rejection

Supports:

- All future investigations
- Standardization of evaluation procedures
- Canonicalization of accepted results

---

### Case 3

An empirical proposition is evaluated by deductive proof alone.

# Investigation M7 — Nature of Proposition Types

## Status

In Progress

---

## Research Question

What constitutes a proposition type?

---

## Motivation

Previous investigations concluded that the objective justification criteria applicable to a proposition are determined by its proposition type.

However,

the notion of proposition type has not itself been established.

Accordingly,

the nature of proposition types requires independent investigation.

---

## Current Hypothesis

A proposition type is an objective classification determined by the epistemic role of a proposition within a formal investigation.

This hypothesis remains provisional.

---

## Candidate Characterizations

Current candidates include:

- Subject matter
- Linguistic form
- Intended purpose
- Method of justification
- Epistemic role

No characterization has yet been accepted.

---

## Investigation Strategy

Evaluate each candidate independently.

Reject every characterization that permits arbitrary or subjective classification.

---

## Current Assessment

Under Investigation.

---

## Candidate 1 — Subject Matter

### Claim

Proposition types are determined by what the proposition discusses.

### Analysis

Different proposition types may concern identical subject matter.

For example,

a definition,

an axiom,

and a theorem may all concern the same concept.

Accordingly,

subject matter does not determine proposition type.

### Assessment

Rejected.

---

## Candidate 2 — Linguistic Form

### Claim

Proposition types are determined by grammatical structure.

### Analysis

Equivalent propositions may be expressed in different forms.

Accordingly,

linguistic form does not determine proposition type.

### Assessment

Rejected.

---

## Candidate 3 — Intended Purpose

### Claim

Proposition types are determined by the author's intention.

### Analysis

Intentions are subjective.

Objective methodology cannot depend upon investigator psychology.

### Assessment

Rejected.

---

## Candidate 4 — Method of Justification

### Claim

Proposition types are determined by the justification method they require.

### Analysis
