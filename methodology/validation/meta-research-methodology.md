# Meta-Research Validation Methodology

## Purpose

This document defines the general methodology used to develop every formal theory within Project FAR.

Its purpose is to ensure that every component of Project FAR is constructed according to a consistent research process.

This methodology governs:

- FAR;
- FARA;
- FARO; and
- every future subsystem.

---

# Development Phases

Every formal theory within Project FAR shall be developed according to the following phases.

## Phase 1 — Scope

Precisely define the objectives and boundaries of the theory.

Questions to answer include:

- What does the theory study?
- What does the theory explicitly exclude?
- What problems is the theory intended to solve?

---

## Phase 2 — Definitions

Develop the canonical terminology.

Every technical term shall receive an explicit definition.

Definitions precede arguments.

---

## Phase 3 — Research

Investigate candidate concepts without assuming their correctness.

Research activities include:

- reduction attempts;
- counterexample search;
- model construction;
- hidden assumption analysis;
- competing hypotheses.

Research documents are exploratory.

Nothing within them is regarded as canonical.

---

## Phase 4 — Consolidation

Review research results.

Separate:

- accepted results;
- unresolved questions;
- rejected hypotheses.

Eliminate duplication.

Record dependencies.

---

## Phase 5 — Canonical Theory

Construct the formal theory.

Canonical documents include:

- definitions;
- axioms;
- inference rules;
- propositions;
- theorems;
- proofs.

Only established results belong within the canonical theory.

---

## Phase 6 — Validation

Attempt to refute the theory.

Validation activities include:

- counterexample search;
- independence investigations;
- consistency investigations;
- minimality investigations;
- sufficiency investigations.

The objective is to discover weaknesses rather than confirm expectations.

---

## Phase 7 — Demonstration

Apply the theory to representative examples.

Demonstrations illustrate:

- expressive power;
- generality;
- practical utility;
- limitations.

---

# General Principles

The following principles govern every phase.

- Definitions precede conclusions.
- Assumptions remain explicit.
- Every conclusion is justified.
- Counterexamples receive equal consideration.
- Canonical documents contain only established results.
- Research documents remain exploratory.

---

# Relationship to the Meta-Theory

This methodology governs the construction of every formal subsystem within Project FAR.

Individual theories may extend this methodology but shall not contradict it.
