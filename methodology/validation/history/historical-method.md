# Validation — Historical Method

## Purpose

This document evaluates the applicability of Project FAR to the historical method.

The objective is to determine whether the architectural components of FARA, the methodology of FAR, and the operations of FARO adequately represent historical reasoning.

---

# Framework

The historical method investigates past events through the critical analysis of historical sources and material evidence.

Historical reasoning constructs explanations from incomplete and often conflicting evidence.

---

# FARA Analysis

## Investigation

The investigation specifies the historical question under consideration.

---

## Representations

Representations include:

- Primary sources
- Secondary sources
- Archaeological evidence
- Historical records
- Chronological data
- Scholarly interpretations

---

## Representational Structure

Representations are organized according to evidential relationships, chronology, authorship, and source reliability.

---

## Interpretation

Interpretation assigns historical significance to the available evidence.

---

# FAR Analysis

## Reasoning Calculus

The reasoning calculus consists of source criticism, corroboration, chronological analysis, contextual interpretation, and evidential inference.

---

## Reasoning States

Each reasoning state consists of the current historical interpretation supported by the available evidence.

---

## Transition Signatures

Each transition signature records the analytical step connecting reasoning states.

---

## Candidates

Candidates consist of the competing historical explanations admitted for consideration.

---

## Admissibility Structure (Ω)

Ω classifies candidate explanations according to the available evidence and historical reasoning calculus.

---

## Resolution Rule

Select the admissible explanation best supported by the investigation.

---

## Resolution

The resulting resolution is the historical conclusion supported by the available evidence.

The resolution remains subject to revision if additional evidence becomes available.

---

# FARO Analysis

FARO compares competing historical interpretations by identifying:

- shared evidence,
- differing interpretations,
- differing evidential weight,
- differing admissibility structures,
- and the earliest point of divergence.

---

# Evaluation

Project FAR successfully represents the architecture of historical reasoning while remaining independent of any particular historical methodology.

---

# Conclusion

The historical method is naturally representable within Project FAR.

---

# Notes

Historical reasoning illustrates that a valid investigation may legitimately conclude that the available evidence is insufficient to justify a stronger conclusion.
