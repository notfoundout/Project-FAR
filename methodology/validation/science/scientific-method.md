# Validation — Scientific Method

## Purpose

This document evaluates the Scientific Method using the validation methodology defined by Project FAR.

The objective is to determine whether the Scientific Method can be represented using the Foundational Architecture of Reasoning Analysis (FARA), investigated using the Foundational Analysis of Reasoning (FAR), and analyzed using the Foundational Analysis of Reasoning Operations (FARO).

---

# 1. Framework

## Name

Scientific Method

---

## Purpose

To develop, test, and refine explanations of observable phenomena through systematic investigation.

---

## Domain

Natural sciences and any discipline employing empirical investigation.

---

# 2. Structural Analysis

## Primitive Concepts

Examples include:

- Observation
- Hypothesis
- Prediction
- Experiment
- Evidence

The precise primitive concepts depend upon the particular formulation of the Scientific Method.

---

## Derived Concepts

Examples include:

- Theory
- Model
- Confirmation
- Refutation
- Replication

---

## Architectural Structure

The Scientific Method organizes reasoning as an iterative process of observation, hypothesis formation, experimentation, evidence evaluation, and revision.

---

# 3. Reasoning Analysis

## Investigation

An investigation seeks to answer a scientific question.

---

## Representations

Observations, hypotheses, predictions, experimental results, measurements, models, and conclusions.

---

## Interpretation

Scientific meaning is assigned through established terminology, measurement procedures, and theoretical context.

---

## Reasoning Calculus

The Scientific Method employs empirical inference, logical reasoning, statistical analysis, and experimental evaluation.

The precise reasoning calculus varies between scientific disciplines.

---

## Reasoning States

Each stage of an investigation may be represented as a reasoning state.

---

## Transformations

Reasoning progresses through observation, hypothesis revision, experimentation, analysis, and interpretation.

---

## Candidates

Possible explanations or hypotheses admitted for consideration.

---

## Admissibility

Candidates are evaluated according to empirical evidence, experimental results, and the reasoning calculus employed.

---

## Resolution

The investigation concludes by selecting the hypothesis or explanation supported by the available evidence.

The resolution remains subject to future revision.

---

# 4. Mapping to FARA

Every major component of the Scientific Method appears representable within FARA.

Observations, hypotheses, experiments, and conclusions correspond naturally to representations and reasoning states.

No obvious architectural incompatibilities have yet been identified.

---

# 5. Application of FAR

The FAR methodology can represent the complete scientific investigation from question formulation through final resolution while maintaining explicit reasoning states and transition signatures.

---

# 6. Application of FARO

Scientific investigations can be:

- compared,
- audited,
- and subjected to disagreement analysis

using the methods defined by FARO.

---

# 7. Evaluation

## Strengths

- Highly structured.
- Explicit reasoning process.
- Naturally auditable.
- Well suited to iterative investigations.

---

## Limitations

Scientific practice varies between disciplines.

Some investigative decisions may remain implicit unless explicitly represented.

---

## Open Questions

- Can every scientific investigation be represented without loss?
- What is the minimal representation required?
- Can experimental uncertainty be represented uniformly?

---

# 8. Validation Summary

## Architecture Compatibility

**Full**

Every major architectural component appears representable.

---

## Methodology Compatibility

**Full**

The FAR methodology aligns naturally with scientific investigations.

---

## Operational Compatibility

**Full**

Comparison, auditing, and disagreement analysis appear directly applicable.

---

## Overall Assessment

The Scientific Method appears fully compatible with the current architecture, methodology, and operational framework of Project FAR.

---

## Recommended Research

- Formal representation of experimental uncertainty.
- Representation of probabilistic reasoning.
- Cross-disciplinary validation.

---

# 9. Conclusion

The Scientific Method appears fully representable within the current scope of Project FAR.

Further validation should determine whether specialized scientific methodologies require additional architectural concepts.
