# Proof Standards

## Purpose

This document defines the standards governing formal proofs within Project FAR.

Its purpose is to distinguish proposed results from established theory and to ensure that every accepted result satisfies consistent standards of rigor.

---

# Scope

These standards apply to:

- derived concepts;
- propositions;
- theorems; and
- any formal result incorporated into the established theory.

---

# General Principle

No formal result should be accepted solely because it appears plausible.

Every accepted result shall be supported by a proof satisfying the standards defined in this document.

---

# Proof Requirements

Every proof shall satisfy the following requirements.

## Logical Validity

Every inference shall follow from previously established results.

---

## Explicit Dependencies

Every proof shall identify all definitions, derived concepts, axioms, propositions, and theorems upon which it depends.

---

## Non-Circularity

No proof may depend directly or indirectly upon the result being proved.

---

## Completeness

Every logical step required by the argument shall be explicitly justified.

No essential step may be omitted.

---

## Consistency

A proof shall not contradict any established result unless the contradiction is explicitly identified and accompanied by a proposed revision to the theory.

---

## Verification

Every completed proof remains subject to independent verification and attempted falsification.

Acceptance of a proof does not terminate further analysis.

---

# Proof Structure

Every proof should contain the following sections.

## Statement

The formal result being proved.

---

## Dependencies

The established results required by the proof.

---

## Assumptions

Any assumptions introduced for indirect arguments.

---

## Argument

The complete logical derivation.

---

## Conclusion

The result established by the proof.

---

# Confidence Levels

Project FAR recognizes four levels of theoretical maturity.

## Proposed

A result has been formulated but has not yet undergone verification.

---

## Verified

A result has successfully completed the verification process.

---

## Established

A verified result has been formally proved and incorporated into the established theory.

---

## Revised

An established result has been modified in light of stronger theoretical evidence.

---

# Burden of Proof

The burden of proof increases with the strength of a claim.

Claims concerning:

- minimality;
- universality;
- completeness;
- independence; and
- equivalence

require stronger justification than local theoretical results.

---

# Research Principle

Every formal result remains open to future review.

Project FAR treats proofs as the strongest currently available justification for a result rather than as immunity from future revision.
