# Ω (Omega)

## The Admissibility Structure

---

# Purpose

Ω is the central derived object of FARA.

It represents the complete admissibility structure induced by applying a reasoning calculus to the possibility space associated with an investigation.

Unlike a conclusion, Ω does not identify a single answer.

Instead, Ω represents the complete organization of admissible candidate resolutions.

---

# Motivation

Many reasoning systems do not produce a unique conclusion.

Examples include:

- multiple mathematical models,
- competing scientific explanations,
- equally optimal solutions,
- stable models,
- legal interpretations,
- probabilistic hypotheses.

A foundational architecture should therefore represent admissibility itself rather than assuming uniqueness.

Ω provides this representation.

---

# Formal Definition

Given:

- a representational structure **S**,
- an interpretation **I**,
- an investigation **Q**,
- and a reasoning calculus **R**,

the possibility space **P** is generated by:

```
P = P(S, I, Q)
```

Applying the reasoning calculus produces the admissibility structure:

```
Ω = Ω(P, R)
```

Ω therefore depends upon:

- representational structure,
- interpretation,
- investigation,
- reasoning calculus.

---

# Properties

Ω does not necessarily contain:

- one conclusion,
- one proof,
- one explanation.

Instead, Ω may represent:

- multiple admissible models,
- probability distributions,
- partial orderings,
- equivalence classes,
- optimization frontiers,
- or any organization induced by the reasoning calculus.

---

# Resolution

Resolution is not Ω.

Resolution is the justified reduction of the possibility space using Ω.

Ω describes what remains admissible.

Resolution describes progress through that admissibility structure.

---

# Auditability

Every admissibility judgment should be traceable to:

- explicit representations,
- explicit interpretations,
- explicit investigations,
- explicit reasoning rules.

Accordingly, Ω supports complete reconstruction of admissibility decisions.

---

# Current Conjecture

Project FAR currently conjectures:

> Every structured, explicit, auditable reasoning framework induces an object equivalent in expressive power to Ω.

This conjecture remains unproven.

---

# Attempts at Elimination

Project FAR has repeatedly attempted to eliminate Ω.

The following alternatives have been considered:

- unique conclusions,
- proof trees,
- reasoning operators,
- admissible states,
- transition signatures.

Each reconstruction ultimately required an object equivalent in expressive power to Ω.

No successful reduction has yet been identified.

---

# Open Problems

Future work should determine:

- whether Ω is formally irreducible,
- whether Ω admits a simpler representation,
- whether every reasoning calculus necessarily induces Ω,
- whether a counterexample exists.

These questions remain central to the Project FAR research program.

---

# Current Status

Ω is currently regarded as a derived object.

Its precise mathematical characterization remains an active area of research.

No claim regarding the universality or irreducibility of Ω should presently be interpreted as formally established.
