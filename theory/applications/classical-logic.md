# Application — Classical Logic

## FAR Mapping

A classical proof can be represented as:

```text
<I, Rep, S, Int, C, T>
```

where:

- `I` is the objective of proving a target formula from premises;
- `Rep` is the set of formulas, premises, intermediate lines, and conclusion;
- `S` is the proof ordering plus dependency relation among proof lines;
- `Int` assigns truth-functional meanings to formula symbols;
- `C` is the classical proof calculus, such as natural deduction, sequent calculus, or Hilbert-style rules;
- `T` is the ordered trace of inference applications.

## Result

Classical logic fits Project FAR because its reasoning objects are explicit, structured, interpreted, investigation-relative, and rule-governed.

## Boundary

Project FAR does not claim classical logic is the correct calculus for all reasoning. It represents classical logic as one possible reasoning calculus.
