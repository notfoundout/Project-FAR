# Application — Bayesian Reasoning

## FAR Mapping

A Bayesian reasoning process can be represented as:

```text
<I, Rep, S, Int, C, T>
```

where:

- `I` is the objective of updating uncertainty about one or more hypotheses;
- `Rep` includes hypotheses, priors, likelihoods, evidence statements, posterior probabilities, and update equations;
- `S` records probabilistic dependency, conditionalization structure, and update order;
- `Int` assigns probabilistic meaning to hypotheses and evidence;
- `C` is the Bayesian calculus, including conditional probability and Bayes' theorem;
- `T` records updates from prior to posterior states.

## Result

Bayesian reasoning fits FAR because it is explicit, structured, semantically interpreted, investigation-relative, and governed by update rules.

## Boundary

FAR represents the Bayesian update structure. It does not by itself determine whether the priors or likelihoods are empirically justified.
