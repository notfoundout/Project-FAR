# Application — Everyday Argument

## FAR Mapping

An ordinary argument can be represented as:

```text
<I, Rep, S, Int, C, T>
```

where:

- `I` is the question being argued about;
- `Rep` includes claims, reasons, examples, objections, assumptions, and conclusions;
- `S` records support, dependency, contradiction, relevance, and response relations;
- `Int` assigns meaning to ordinary-language statements;
- `C` is the implicit or explicit standard of reasoning being used;
- `T` records how the argument moves from claim to objection to revision or conclusion.

## Result

Everyday argument fits FAR when the claims and inferential moves are made explicit.

## Boundary

If the argument relies on hidden emotional reactions, unstated assumptions, or ambiguous terms, FAR can represent only what has been made explicit unless reconstruction rules are supplied.
