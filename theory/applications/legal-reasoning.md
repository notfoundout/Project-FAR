# Application — Legal Reasoning

## FAR Mapping

A legal argument can be represented as:

```text
<I, Rep, S, Int, C, T>
```

where:

- `I` is the legal question or dispute to be resolved;
- `Rep` includes facts, statutes, precedent, rules, standards, claims, objections, and holdings;
- `S` records authority hierarchy, dependency, analogy, distinction, conflict, and procedural order;
- `Int` assigns legal meaning to texts, facts, and standards;
- `C` is the applicable legal reasoning calculus, including jurisdictional rules, evidentiary rules, precedent rules, and standards of review;
- `T` records the movement from facts and authorities to legal conclusion.

## Result

Legal reasoning fits FAR because it is explicitly structured, interpretation-heavy, objective-relative, and rule-governed.

## Boundary

FAR can represent competing legal interpretations without deciding which jurisdictional rule or interpretive philosophy should be adopted unless that rule is supplied by the investigation.
