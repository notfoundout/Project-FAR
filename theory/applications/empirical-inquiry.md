# Application — Empirical Inquiry

## FAR Mapping

An empirical inquiry can be represented as:

```text
<I, Rep, S, Int, C, T>
```

where:

- `I` is the question or explanatory target;
- `Rep` includes observations, measurements, hypotheses, models, predictions, and conclusions;
- `S` records evidential support, dependency, conflict, operational definitions, and sequence;
- `Int` assigns meaning to measurements, variables, models, and claims;
- `C` is the applicable method calculus, including rules for measurement, comparison, estimation, causal analysis, or model choice;
- `T` records the movement from inputs and method to assessment or conclusion.

## Result

Empirical inquiry fits Project FAR when its claims, evidence, methods, and inferential standards are explicit enough to audit.

## Boundary

FAR represents the reasoning architecture of empirical inquiry. It does not by itself determine whether a measurement is accurate, whether a model is true, or whether a conclusion is warranted without a supplied calculus and evidence standard.
