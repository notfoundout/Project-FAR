# CRE-003 Execution — Independent-Variation Vocabulary Competition

## Status

Completed bounded internal execution. Scientific conclusion: **inconclusive, with stronger discrimination evidence for FAR**.

## Method

The ten vocabularies frozen in AVC-001 were applied without semantic repair to the four CRE-003 matched cases. Three mappings were retained for every vocabulary. Each mapping was assessed on structural, semantic, operational, dependency, information, and historical preservation. `results.csv` reports the least-favorable resolved value across the four cases for each mapping; `Unknown` remains unresolved rather than being ordered between `Partial` and `Fail`.

The execution is internal to Project FAR. The three mappings are separate constructions, not independent external evaluators.

## Case-level adjudication

### CRE-003-I — interpretation variation

FAR preserved distinct denotation while holding syntax and inference fixed. The Sign vocabulary could not separate bearer identity from assigned content without reconstructing independent interpretation machinery. Graph, state-transition, categorical, event, and typed-graph vocabularies preserved execution structure but left semantic identity or information content Partial or Unknown.

### CRE-003-G — investigation variation

FAR preserved different objectives and success conditions over identical represented and interpreted material. The Goal-governed Process vocabulary could not separate objective conditions from governing procedure without recovering the Investigation/Calculus distinction. Several compact alternatives represented task labels but did not establish that investigation identity was primitive rather than an undeclared attribute convention.

### CRE-003-C — reasoning-calculus variation

FAR preserved the change from classical to defeasible admissibility and the resulting permitted outcome. The Goal-governed Process vocabulary partially collapsed objective and procedure. Operational alternatives reproduced the transition behavior, but semantic and information judgments remained unresolved where admissibility rules were encoded only as state or edge attributes.

### CRE-003-R — representational-organization variation

FAR preserved distinct propositional and graph organizations while holding interpreted content and objective fixed. State and information-channel alternatives lost or indirectly reconstructed part of the distinction between representational form and interpreted content. Graph-native alternatives represented this case efficiently but still did not resolve the other matched cases.

## Results

- AV-001 FAR control: 3/3 mappings passed all six dimensions across all four cases.
- AV-002 Sign system: merger failure; hidden Representation/Interpretation reconstruction present.
- AV-003 Goal-governed process: merger failure; hidden Investigation/Calculus reconstruction present.
- AV-004 Object-relation-transformation: unresolved semantic and information tradeoff.
- AV-005 State-transition system: unresolved structural, semantic, and information tradeoff.
- AV-006 Category-process vocabulary: unresolved semantic and information tradeoff with higher derived cost.
- AV-007 Information-channel vocabulary: not admissible because structural and dependency preservation remained Partial.
- AV-008 Semantic-commitment vocabulary: not admissible because structural, operational, and dependency preservation remained Partial.
- AV-009 Event-process vocabulary: unresolved semantic tradeoff.
- AV-010 Typed dependency graph: unresolved semantic and information tradeoff.

## Decision

CRE-003 strengthens the evidence that the current five-way FAR distinction is useful for independently varying representation, interpretation, investigation, and reasoning calculus. It also reproduces the two simple merger failures observed in AVC-001 on a benchmark specifically designed to expose those collapses.

It does **not** establish that FAR is globally minimal, uniquely optimal, necessary, or universal. The compact graph, state-transition, categorical, event, and typed-graph alternatives remain unresolved rather than defeated because some judgments are Partial or Unknown. No alternative dominated FAR under the preregistered Pareto rule, but the tested candidate space is not closed and the mappings were not externally independent.

## Exact bounded conclusion

> Across the four frozen CRE-003 independent-variation cases, all three FAR mappings preserved all six dimensions. No competing vocabulary produced an all-Pass mapping across all cases. Two merged vocabularies failed without hidden reconstruction, while five compact alternatives remained unresolved rather than defeated.

Therefore:

- existential FAR sufficiency on CRE-003: established internally;
- reproducible FAR sufficiency across the three internal mappings: established internally;
- competitor dominance: not established;
- FAR dominance over the unrestricted vocabulary space: not established;
- global minimality: not established;
- universality: not established.
