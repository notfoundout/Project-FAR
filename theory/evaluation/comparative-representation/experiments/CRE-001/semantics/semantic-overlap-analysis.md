# CRE-001 Semantic Overlap Analysis


## Status Vocabulary

- **Supported**: established for frozen CRE-001 vocabulary-semantics scope by explicit source definitions and this specification.
- **Unsupported**: not established and not licensed by this specification.
- **Unknown**: not determined by available repository evidence.
- **Assumed**: required CRE-001-scoped assumption recorded explicitly.
- **Implementation-specific**: property of the deterministic compiler/verifier artifacts, not primitive semantics.


## Overlaps

- A:Object, B:Label, and C:Representation can all carry identifiable represented items, but license different commitments: unit individuation, attachment markers, and represented content respectively.

- A:Transformation, B:Transition, and C:Calculus all support change/procedure semantics, but differ between operation-on-items, state movement, and rule-governed policy.

- A:Relation, B:State/Label combinations, and C:Representational Structure can all encode ordering/status/dependency, but through connection, snapshot/attachment, and explicit structure respectively.

## Genuine Differences

- Vocabulary A is object-relation-transformation oriented.

- Vocabulary B is state-transition-label oriented.

- Vocabulary C separates representation, structure, interpretation, investigation, and calculus.

## Do Not Collapse

These overlaps are documented for audit only. They do not establish equivalence, independence failure, superiority, or primitive redundancy.
