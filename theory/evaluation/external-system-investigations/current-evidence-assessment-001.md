# Current External-Validation Evidence Assessment 001

Status: provisional independent review

## Corpus Reviewed

This assessment reviews the completed external-validation evidence available in the repository as experimental results rather than as accepted proof:

- the external validation registry, containing EV-001 through EV-029;
- the v0.4.0 batch reports for EV-001 through EV-020 under `theory/evaluation/external-systems/`;
- the reports for EV-021 through EV-029 under `theory/evaluation/external-system-investigations/`, conducted under the expanded external-system investigation template;
- Cross-Investigation Synthesis 001;
- the external validation methodology and investigation template;
- the canonical proof standard, research doctrine, isolation doctrine, and research execution charter.

The review did not treat methodology compliance as correctness. It looked for overclaiming, hidden FAR/FARA assumptions, unsupported mappings, inconsistent preservation analysis, contradictions, bias, reproducibility weakness, and conclusions stronger than the methodology permits.

The reviewed EV-001 through EV-029 corpus remains legacy external validation evidence: preliminary compatibility evidence, scoped FAR/FARA mappings, preservation-pressure evidence, candidate-counterexample scanning, exploratory evidence, and external pressure identification. It is not a set of blinded comparative experiments and is not sufficient evidence for comparative superiority, reproducible sufficiency, primitive necessity, minimality, universality, or uniqueness.

Comparative Representation Protocol v1.0 remains the canonical methodology for future claims about comparative representational performance, reproducibility, relative representation economy, structural agreement, local necessity through ablation, and comparative constraint. This assessment does not retroactively convert EV-001 through EV-029 into CRP experiments. CRE-001 is treated as registered, frozen, prepared for execution, not executed, not yet evaluator-exposed, and without collected mappings or comparative results; it is therefore a future high-information test rather than evidence already obtained.

## Defects Found and Corrections Made

The audit found one synthesis defect: the first synthesis section said “All four investigations” even though the stated scope lists nine investigations. That wording understated the reviewed batch and could mislead later readers about recurrence counts. Cross-Investigation Synthesis 001 was corrected to refer to all nine scoped investigations and to distinguish the older registry background from the later external-validation batch.

No completed investigation required reclassification. No candidate primitive failure was discovered. No methodological defect prevented this evidence assessment, so the methodology itself was not modified.

## Recurring Findings

### Components Required by Every Investigated System

Across the completed corpus, every successful or partially successful representation used the five FAR roles: Investigation, Representation, Representational Structure, Interpretation, and Reasoning Calculus. The later external-validation batch also repeatedly used FARA state-transition vocabulary when a proof, update, inquiry, agent loop, expert protocol, legal sequence, or analogy process was in scope.

### Components Never Required

No FAR primitive was shown to be globally unused. No FARA component was shown to be globally necessary either, because some investigations can be scoped statically and because no ablation test has established primitive-level irreducibility.

### Recurring Conservative Extensions

Conservative extensions recur in these classes:

- domain semantics and interpretation policies, including higher-order, dependent, categorical, program-state, legal, empirical, probabilistic, and model-theoretic semantics;
- specialized structure, including resource structure, relevance structure, authority hierarchy, evidence graphs, source-target analogy mappings, agent-indexed context, and interaction graphs;
- specialized calculus or admissibility policies, including probabilistic update, proof rules, solver transitions, revision postulates, legal standards, scientific protocols, approximation diagnostics, and resolution rules;
- provenance and replay machinery, including source reliability, tool/environment state, model/run provenance, memory policies, data lineage, and dependency records.

The evidence currently classifies these as missing domain knowledge, missing implementation detail, missing reproducibility artifacts, or missing reusable methodology rather than as missing FAR primitives. There is insufficient evidence that any conservative extension is evidence against the current FAR/FARA architecture.

### Recurring Unresolved Preservation Dimensions

The recurring unresolved dimensions are operational preservation, dependency preservation, and information preservation. They recur most strongly in broad or opaque systems: scientific method as a general family, LLM reasoning, agentic AI reasoning, human expert reasoning, and analogical reasoning.

### Recurring Ambiguity Sources

Recurring ambiguity sources are broad-family scope, hidden or proprietary internals, mutable external dependencies, insufficient replay artifacts, tacit knowledge, generated explanations that may not match actual process, discretionary relevance criteria, and uncertain source-quality thresholds.

### Recurring Information-Loss Patterns

Information loss recurs when reports or logs preserve outputs but not the actual process, when source provenance is incomplete, when tacit expert cues are not captured, when model weights or runtime state are inaccessible, when approximation diagnostics are omitted, or when analogy feature-selection criteria are underspecified.

### Recurring Operational-Preservation Failures

Operational preservation remains unknown where a later investigator cannot replay rules, transitions, tool calls, updates, experimental procedures, or human cognitive steps from the available artifacts. These are evidence-access failures, not demonstrated primitive failures.

### Recurring Semantic-Preservation Failures

Semantic preservation pressure arises when the target meaning depends on domain-specific standards: truth conditions, model satisfaction, probability semantics, measurement definitions, legal authority, normative standards, or relevance criteria. Completed investigations generally handle these as interpretation policies, but opaque or underspecified systems leave semantic preservation unknown.

### Recurring Dependency-Preservation Failures

Dependency preservation pressure recurs when source reliability, background theory, model assumptions, legal authority, tool state, environment state, experimental protocol, or tacit experience is not explicit enough to reconstruct the reasoning chain.

### Methodological Strengths

The expanded external-system investigation template forced claim separation, preservation-dimension review, explicit limitations, deterministic classification precedence, and separation of representability from universality, necessity, and minimality.

### Methodological Weaknesses

The main weakness is not a rule defect but an evidence burden: broad or opaque systems need artifact bundles richer than ordinary reports, logs, papers, or generated explanations. Older EV-001 through EV-020 reports are also weaker under the current methodology because they do not record the full preservation matrix.

## Cross-Investigation Assessment

The evidence supports a recurring representational pattern: target objectives, objects, structures, interpretations, and admissible transitions can often be mapped to FAR/FARA roles when the reasoning process is explicit and documented. The evidence does not establish a single universal structure across all reasoning. It also does not show multiple incompatible structures, because no investigated system demonstrated an explicit in-scope reasoning capability that could not be represented through the existing roles or conservative extensions.

The most stable boundary condition is explicit accessibility. Systems with explicit objects, semantics, rules, and replayable transitions provide positive evidence. Systems whose process is hidden, tacit, mutable, underdocumented, or relevance-discretionary remain unresolved or only partially preserved.

The recurring classes of reasoning that resist faithful representation are not currently formal counterexamples. They are classes where available evidence does not expose enough process, dependency, semantic, or information content to assess faithful representation.

## Corrected Overclaims

The accumulated evidence does not justify any of the following claims:

- FAR/FARA has been proven universal;
- FAR/FARA has been proven necessary;
- FAR/FARA has been proven minimal;
- successful syntactic encoding establishes faithful representation;
- absence of a candidate primitive failure in 29 investigations establishes absence of such failures globally;
- unresolved opaque or tacit systems support universality.

The corrected strongest claim is narrower: no completed in-scope investigation has identified a candidate primitive failure, and multiple explicit reasoning systems have been represented directly or by conservative extension under stated assumptions.

## Strongest Support for the Central Hypothesis

The strongest support is the convergence across formal logic, formal verification, automated reasoning, probabilistic reasoning, legal reasoning, scientific reasoning, and AI-related investigations on the same five FAR roles, with no candidate sixth primitive discovered. Formal systems provide the cleanest support because their objects, semantics, and transitions are explicit. Bayesian and legal investigations add support that uncertain and institutional reasoning can be represented when domain policies are made explicit.

## Strongest Pressure Against the Central Hypothesis

The strongest pressure is the recurrence of unresolved preservation in systems where reasoning is opaque, tacit, mutable, or underdocumented. This pressure does not refute primitive sufficiency, but it blocks universal claims and exposes that faithful representation may require evidence or instrumentation unavailable in ordinary artifacts.

## Universality Assessment

Universality is not established. The evidence supports scoped representability for explicit and sufficiently documented reasoning systems, plus conservative-extension representability for several complex domains. Four EV-021 through EV-029 investigations remain unresolved, and older investigations do not meet the later preservation-record standard.

## Necessity Assessment

Necessity is not established. Recurrence of all five FAR roles across investigated systems is evidence against obvious unused primitives, and repeated rejection of syntax-only or output-only mappings supports practical necessity within the reviewed cases. However, no primitive-ablation or independence test has shown that each role is globally required.

## Minimality Assessment

Minimality is not established. The corpus contains no ablation proof, primitive-elimination experiment, minimal-model argument, or independence proof. Conservative extensions may be reusable derived concepts or implementation requirements, but current evidence does not decide their status.

## Scope Assessment

The supported scope is explicit reasoning with accessible representations, structures, semantics, and transition or evaluation rules. The weak scope includes opaque AI systems, tacit expert cognition, broad scientific practice without subtype-specific artifacts, analogy with underspecified relevance criteria, and any system where dependencies cannot be reconstructed.

## Best-Supported Allowable Conclusion

The best-supported allowable conclusion is **6. Available evidence remains insufficient**.

The alternatives are less justified for the following reasons:

1. **No universal structure exists** is not supported because no candidate primitive failure or incompatible in-scope structure has been found.
2. **A universal structure exists, but FAR is not it** is not supported because the evidence has not identified a replacement universal structure or a FAR-specific failure.
3. **FAR is universal only within a bounded domain** is plausible but not yet established because the current evidence identifies boundary pressures, not a proven maximal domain boundary.
4. **FAR is universal but not minimal** is too strong because universality is unproved, even though minimality is also unproved.
5. **FAR is universal and minimal under explicitly stated assumptions** is too strong because neither universality nor minimality has been proven under the current evidence and proof standards.

## Exact Barriers to a Stronger Conclusion

A stronger conclusion is blocked by:

- unresolved operational, dependency, and information preservation in opaque or tacit systems;
- lack of primitive-ablation evidence;
- lack of primitive-independence or minimality proof;
- lack of controlled cross-investigator replication of the same target systems;
- older report format for EV-001 through EV-020, which limits preservation-level comparability;
- insufficient criteria for classifying some conservative extensions as reusable derived concepts, implementation requirements, methodology requirements, or architecture pressure.

## Highest-Information Next Tests

The smallest high-information next tests are:

| Test need | Highest-information test | Why this is narrower than another broad domain investigation |
|---|---|---|
| Universality | Targeted counterexample test using one explicit, fully documented reasoning process selected because it stresses hidden state, context shift, or nonmonotonic relevance. | A deliberately adversarial counterexample attempt is more informative than adding another ordinary domain case. |
| Necessity | Primitive-ablation replay across one direct-fit formal system and one conservative-extension system, removing one FAR role at a time and recording preservation loss. | Directly tests necessity instead of inferring it from recurrence. |
| Minimality | Formal independence or elimination proof obligation for each FAR primitive under a fixed representation target. | Minimality requires proof or ablation, not more examples. |
| Scope boundaries | Controlled documentation-threshold test on one unresolved system, comparing output-only, log-level, and fully instrumented artifact bundles. | Identifies whether unresolved status is caused by the system or by evidence access. |
| Representation fidelity | Reconstruction check by an independent reviewer using only the recorded source evidence and FAR/FARA mapping for one EV-021 through EV-029 investigation. | Tests faithful reconstructibility without expanding the corpus. |
| Cross-investigator reliability | Blind duplicate classification of one existing investigation under the expanded external-system investigation template, with discrepancies recorded before reconciliation. | Tests reviewer variance directly. |

These tests should precede any broad new domain campaign unless a specific discovered issue requires an additional external-system investigation.
