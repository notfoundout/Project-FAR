# External System Evaluation — Multi-Agent Reasoning

## Overview

Multi-agent reasoning evaluates systems with multiple agents, beliefs, goals, communication, coordination, conflict, or strategic interaction.

## FAR Mapping

| FAR Primitive | Mapping |
|---|---|
| Investigation | Determine what agents believe, choose, coordinate, or infer in an interaction. |
| Representation | Agents, beliefs, goals, messages, actions, strategies, observations, and outcomes. |
| Representational Structure | Agent-indexed belief structures, interaction graphs, information partitions, and strategy dependencies. |
| Interpretation | Epistemic, game-theoretic, or protocol semantics. |
| Reasoning Calculus | Update rules, strategy selection, communication rules, equilibrium reasoning, or protocol rules. |

## Pressure Point

The pressure is agent-indexed context and interactive reasoning.

## Classification

`conservative extension`

## Justification

Multi-agent reasoning requires indexed interpretation and interaction structure, but those remain extensions of existing FAR primitives.

## Confidence

Provisional.
