# External System Evaluation — Bayesian Inference

## Overview

Bayesian inference reasons by assigning probabilities to hypotheses and updating those assignments in response to evidence. It is a strong external test because it uses quantitative interpretation and update rules rather than only deductive validity.

## Investigation

Determine whether Bayesian update and evidential reasoning can be represented using the five FAR primitives.

## FAR Mapping

| FAR Primitive | Mapping |
|---|---|
| Investigation | Determine how evidence changes the probability of a hypothesis. |
| Representation | Hypotheses, priors, likelihoods, evidence, posterior distributions, and models. |
| Representational Structure | Conditional-dependence relations, probability models, and evidence-hypothesis relations. |
| Interpretation | Probabilistic interpretation of propositions, variables, and observations. |
| Reasoning Calculus | Bayes rule, conditionalization, marginalization, and model-comparison procedures. |

## Pressure Point

The pressure is quantitative uncertainty. FAR must represent degrees of support rather than only categorical proof.

## Classification

`conservative extension`

## Justification

Bayesian reasoning requires quantitative semantics and update rules, but those are Interpretation and Reasoning Calculus. Hypotheses and evidence remain representations, and probability models provide representational structure.

## Confidence
