# External System Evaluation — Argumentation Frameworks

## Overview

Argumentation frameworks reason over arguments and attack, support, or defeat relations. They are an external test for FAR because admissibility often depends on graph structure rather than linear proof.

## FAR Mapping

| FAR Primitive | Mapping |
|---|---|
| Investigation | Determine which arguments or extensions are acceptable under a semantics. |
| Representation | Arguments, claims, attacks, supports, defeats, and extensions. |
| Representational Structure | Directed attack/support graphs and extension membership relations. |
| Interpretation | Argumentation semantics such as grounded, preferred, stable, or admissible semantics. |
| Reasoning Calculus | Rules for acceptability, defense, defeat, and extension construction. |

## Pressure Point

The pressure is graph-governed admissibility.

## Classification

`conservative extension`

## Justification

Graph-based argument acceptability fits FAR through representations, graph structure, interpretation semantics, and calculus-governed extension selection. No sixth primitive is indicated.

## Confidence

Provisional.
