# External System Evaluation — Category Theory

## Overview

Category theory reasons through objects, morphisms, composition, diagrams, functors, natural transformations, and universal properties. It pressures FAR because much of the reasoning is structural and diagrammatic rather than sentence-first.

## Investigation

Determine whether category-theoretic reasoning can be represented using the five FAR primitives.

## FAR Mapping

| FAR Primitive | Mapping |
|---|---|
| Investigation | Determine whether a categorical construction, morphism, equivalence, or universal property follows. |
| Representation | Objects, morphisms, diagrams, functors, natural transformations, equations, and universal properties. |
| Representational Structure | Composition, commutativity, diagram shape, domain/codomain relations, and naturality structure. |
| Interpretation | Categorical semantics assigning meaning to diagrams and morphism equations. |
| Reasoning Calculus | Diagram chase, composition rules, universal-property application, and equivalence rules. |

## Pressure Point

The pressure is diagrammatic representation. Category theory often reasons through structural diagrams rather than linear formulas.

## Classification

`conservative extension`

## Justification

Diagrammatic reasoning is still representational and structural. FAR can model diagrams as representations with explicit morphism and commutativity relations. Universal properties are calculus-governed inference resources. The required additions are domain-specific structures and rules, not a sixth primitive.

## Sixth Primitive Assessment

No sixth primitive is currently indicated.

## Confidence

Provisional.

## Remaining Questions

- Whether diagrammatic equivalence should be registered as a derived concept.
- Whether informal categorical practice requires separate treatment from formal category theory.
