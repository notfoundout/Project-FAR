# Axioms

## Purpose

This document records the foundational assumptions of Project FAR.

An axiom is accepted without proof within the framework and serves as the foundation for all subsequent formal results.

Axioms should be minimal, independent, and expressed solely in terms of primitive concepts.

---

# Primitive Architecture

The current primitive architecture of FARA consists of:

- Investigation
- Representation
- Representational Structure
- Interpretation
- Reasoning Calculus

Every axiom should reference only these primitive concepts.

---

# Axiom 1 — Explicit Representation

Every reasoning process within the stated scope of Project FAR admits one or more explicit representations.

---

# Axiom 2 — Representational Organization

Every collection of representations participating in a reasoning process possesses a representational structure.

---

# Axiom 3 — Interpretation

Every representation participating in a reasoning process is interpreted within an investigation.

---

# Axiom 4 — Investigation

Every reasoning process occurs within exactly one investigation.

---

# Axiom 5 — Reasoning Calculus

Every reasoning process proceeds according to a reasoning calculus governing its admissible reasoning transitions.

---

# Axiom Requirements

The axioms of Project FAR are intended to satisfy the following properties.

- Minimality
- Independence
- Internal consistency
- Generality
- Explicitness

Future research should seek to reduce the number of axioms whenever possible without reducing the expressive power of the framework.

---

# Research Status

The current axiomatic foundation is provisional.

Every axiom remains subject to verification, attempted reduction, and possible revision.

Additional axioms should be introduced only when they cannot be derived from the primitive architecture or existing axioms.
