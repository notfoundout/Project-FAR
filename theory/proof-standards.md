# Proof Standards

## Purpose

This document defines the standards required for accepting formal results within Project FAR.

Its purpose is to distinguish proposed results from established theory.

---

# General Principle

A formal result is considered established only after it has been justified according to the standards defined in this document.

No proposition or theorem should be accepted solely because it appears plausible.

---

# Requirements

Every proof should satisfy the following requirements.

## Logical Validity

Every inference shall follow from previously established results.

---

## Explicit Dependencies

Every proof shall identify every definition, derived concept, axiom, proposition, and theorem upon which it depends.

---

## Non-Circularity

No proof may depend directly or indirectly upon the result being proved.

---

## Completeness

Every logical step required by the argument shall be explicitly justified.

---

## Consistency

A proof shall not contradict any previously established result without explicitly identifying the contradiction and proposing a revision to the theory.

---

## Verification

Every proof remains subject to independent verification and attempted falsification.

Acceptance of a proof does not terminate further analysis.

---

# Classification of Results

Project FAR recognizes four levels of confidence.

## Proposed

A result has been formulated but not yet verified.

---

## Verified

A result has successfully passed the verification process.

---

## Established

A verified result has been incorporated into the formal theory.

---

## Revised

An established result has been modified in light of stronger theoretical evidence.

---

# Research Principle

The burden of proof increases with the strength of a claim.

Claims concerning universality, minimality, or completeness require stronger justification than local theoretical results.
