# Formal Definitions

## Purpose

This document defines the core terminology used throughout Project FAR.

Unless explicitly stated otherwise, every technical term appearing elsewhere in the project refers to the definitions given here.

---

# Definition 1 — Representation

A **representation** is an explicitly distinguishable object within a representational structure.

Representations are the fundamental objects manipulated during reasoning.

Examples include:

- statements,
- hypotheses,
- observations,
- models,
- definitions,
- mathematical objects,
- graphs,
- proofs.

FARA makes no ontological distinction among these beyond their explicit representation.

---

# Definition 2 — Representational Structure

A **representational structure** specifies the class of representations that may exist within a reasoning system.

It determines what kinds of representations can be expressed but does not assign meaning to them.

---

# Definition 3 — Interpretation

An **interpretation** assigns semantic meaning to representations.

Interpretation specifies what representations denote without altering their explicit structure.

---

# Definition 4 — Investigation

An **investigation** is an explicitly defined reasoning objective.

Every reasoning process exists relative to an investigation.

The investigation determines:

- relevance,
- explanatory target,
- termination conditions,
- evaluation criteria.

---

# Definition 5 — Reasoning Calculus

A **reasoning calculus** is the collection of rules governing permissible reasoning transitions.

Examples include:

- deductive logic,
- Bayesian inference,
- scientific reasoning,
- legal reasoning,
- optimization.

FARA remains neutral regarding which calculus is employed.

---

# Definition 6 — Reasoning State

A **reasoning state** is the complete explicit configuration of representations associated with an investigation at a particular stage of reasoning.

A reasoning state contains every explicit representation required to continue or audit the reasoning process.

---

# Definition 7 — Possibility Space

The **possibility space** is the set of all candidate resolutions generated by the representational structure, interpretation, and investigation.

The reasoning calculus does not create the possibility space.

It evaluates it.

---

# Definition 8 — Candidate Resolution

A **candidate resolution** is an element of the possibility space capable of resolving or refining the investigation question.

Candidate resolutions exist independently of admissibility.

Whether they are justified is determined later by the reasoning calculus.

---

# Definition 9 — Admissibility Structure (Ω)

The **admissibility structure**, denoted Ω, is the complete organization of candidate resolutions according to the reasoning calculus.

Ω may contain:

- one admissible resolution,
- many admissible resolutions,
- partially ordered resolutions,
- probabilistic structures,
- or any organization required by the reasoning calculus.

---

# Definition 10 — Resolution

A **resolution** is the justified reduction of the investigation's possibility space.

A resolution need not eliminate every possibility.

It consists of reducing the admissible possibilities according to the reasoning calculus.

---

# Definition 11 — Auditability

A reasoning process is **auditable** if every reasoning transition and every conclusion can be reconstructed from explicitly represented reasoning states.

---

# Definition 12 — Explicitness

A representation is **explicit** if it is available for inspection, comparison, and transformation within the reasoning process.

Implicit assumptions fall outside the scope of Project FAR until represented explicitly.

---

# Definition 13 — Structured Reasoning

**Structured reasoning** is reasoning that proceeds through explicitly representable reasoning states according to an identifiable reasoning calculus while remaining auditable.

---

# Current Status

These definitions represent the current working vocabulary of Project FAR.

They remain subject to revision as the framework develops.

Changes to these definitions should occur only through documented version updates.
