# Lemmas

## Purpose

This document records intermediate results used in proving larger results within Project FAR.

A lemma is a formally proven statement whose primary purpose is to support one or more theorems.

Although a lemma may be interesting independently, its principal role is to simplify larger proofs.

---

# Current Status

No lemmas have yet been formally established.

This reflects the current stage of Project FAR.

Future lemmas should be developed by proving propositions recorded elsewhere in the theory.

---

# Standard Form

Every lemma should contain the following sections.

## Statement

The formal statement of the lemma.

---

## Assumptions

The assumptions required.

---

## Dependencies

Definitions, propositions, and previous lemmas used.

---

## Proof

A complete proof.

---

## Scope

The domain over which the lemma is valid.

---

## Notes

Discussion, limitations, or future directions.

---

# Candidate Lemmas

The following results appear promising but have not yet been proven.

---

## Candidate Lemma 1

Every reasoning state possesses an explicit interpretation.

---

## Candidate Lemma 2

Every investigation induces a uniquely defined possibility space relative to a representational structure and interpretation.

---

## Candidate Lemma 3

Every reasoning calculus induces an admissibility structure over the possibility space.

---

## Candidate Lemma 4

Every admissible resolution belongs to the possibility space.

---

## Candidate Lemma 5

Every reasoning transition preserves auditability when all transformations are explicitly represented.

---

## Candidate Lemma 6

Equivalent reasoning states preserve explanatory content under admissible transformations.

---

# Promotion Policy

A candidate lemma is promoted to a formal lemma only after a complete proof has been verified.

A proven lemma may later become part of the proof of one or more theorems.

---

# Philosophy

Project FAR develops from small verified results toward larger conclusions.

Whenever possible, large proofs should be decomposed into independent lemmas whose correctness can be verified separately.
