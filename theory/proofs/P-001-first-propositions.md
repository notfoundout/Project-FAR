# P-001 through P-008 — First Derived Propositions

## Status

Established relative to the current definitions and axioms.

---

## P-001 — Representation Requirement

### Statement

Every scoped reasoning process satisfying Project FAR Axiom 1 admits, for Project FAR evaluation, at least one explicit representation.

### Proof

Project FAR Axiom 1 requires every reasoning process within the stated Project FAR scope to admit one or more explicit representations. Assume a scoped reasoning process satisfies Axiom 1. Then that process admits, for Project FAR evaluation, at least one explicit representation. Therefore every scoped reasoning process satisfying Project FAR Axiom 1 admits, for Project FAR evaluation, at least one explicit representation.

---

## P-002 — Structural Requirement

### Statement

Every scoped reasoning process involving more than one representation requires a representational structure.

### Proof

By Axiom 2, every collection of representations participating in a reasoning process possesses a representational structure. A collection with more than one representation is a collection of representations. Therefore it possesses a representational structure.

---

## P-003 — Semantic Relativity

### Statement

Semantic content is not determined by representation alone.

### Proof

By definition, semantic content is the meaning assigned to a representation under a specified interpretation. By definition, the same representation may receive different meanings under different interpretations. Therefore representation alone does not determine semantic content.

---

## P-004 — Investigation Relativity

### Statement

Admissibility, candidate status, and resolution are investigation-relative.

### Proof

By definition, an investigation establishes the context within which representations, interpretations, reasoning calculi, candidates, admissibility, and resolutions are defined. Therefore these statuses are relative to an investigation.

---

## P-005 — Calculus Relativity of Admissibility

### Statement

Admissibility is determined by the applicable reasoning calculus rather than by representation alone.

### Proof

By definition, admissibility is the property of satisfying the criteria established by the applicable reasoning calculus within an investigation. Therefore admissibility is calculus-relative and cannot be determined by representation alone.

---

## P-006 — Syntax/Semantics Separation

### Statement

Structural equivalence does not imply semantic equivalence.

### Proof

By definition, structural equivalence concerns representational organization rather than meaning. By definition, semantic equivalence depends on semantic content under an interpretation. Since the same structure may receive different interpretations, structural equivalence alone does not imply semantic equivalence.

---

## P-007 — Trace/Process Distinction

### Statement

A reasoning trace is not identical to the reasoning process it represents.

### Proof

By definition, a reasoning trace is an ordered collection of transition signatures representing the progression of a reasoning process. It represents reasoning but is not the reasoning process itself. Therefore the two are distinct.

---

## P-008 — Resolution Dependence

### Statement

A resolution depends on an admissibility structure and a resolution rule.

### Proof

By definition, a resolution is produced by a resolution execution. By definition, a resolution execution is the application of a resolution rule to an admissibility structure. Therefore a resolution depends on an admissibility structure and a resolution rule.
