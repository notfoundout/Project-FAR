## Proof

The accepted Root Theory establishes Distinction as the canonical working primitive.

A relation requires at least two distinguishable terms.

Distinction establishes the possibility of distinguishability.

However,

the existence of distinguishable terms does not by itself establish the existence of a relation between them.

Accordingly,

the current construction has not yet demonstrated that Relation necessarily follows.

The construction demonstrates only that Relation is compatible with the accepted foundations.

Compatibility is weaker than constructibility.

Therefore,

Construction C1.3 has not yet established the proposition.
