# Conjectures

## Introduction

Project FAR distinguishes between definitions, derived results, conjectures, and proven theorems.

This document records every major conjecture currently under investigation.

A conjecture remains part of the framework only until it is either:

- formally proven,
- disproven by counterexample,
- or replaced by a stronger formulation.

---

# Conjecture 1 — Representation Conjecture

Every structured, explicit, auditable reasoning process within the stated scope can be represented within the FARA architecture without loss of expressive power.

---

## Motivation

If true, FARA functions as a universal representational architecture for structured reasoning.

If false, at least one reasoning process exists that requires concepts absent from FARA.

---

## Falsification

Produce:

- a structured,
- explicit,
- auditable reasoning process

that cannot be represented using the current architecture.

---

# Conjecture 2 — Primitive Independence

The four candidate primitives are mutually independent.

Current candidate primitives are:

- Representational Structure
- Interpretation
- Investigation Question
- Reasoning Calculus

---

## Motivation

If one primitive can be derived from the remaining three, the architecture is not minimal.

---

## Falsification

Provide a formal derivation reducing one primitive to the remaining three.

---

# Conjecture 3 — Ω Irreducibility

Every framework capable of representing structured, explicit, auditable reasoning contains, either explicitly or implicitly, an object equivalent in expressive power to Ω.

---

## Motivation

Ω represents the admissibility structure induced by a reasoning calculus.

If Ω is reducible, the architecture may admit further compression.

---

## Falsification

Construct a reasoning framework satisfying the scope of Project FAR that contains no object equivalent to Ω.

---

# Conjecture 4 — Transition Completeness

Every valid reasoning transition can be represented as a transition between explicit reasoning states.

---

## Motivation

This conjecture underlies the auditability of reasoning.

---

## Falsification

Produce a reasoning process requiring information that cannot be represented within any explicit reasoning state.

---

# Conjecture 5 — Ontological Completeness

Every explicit component required for structured, explicit, auditable reasoning can be represented using the current ontology.

---

## Motivation

If true, no additional ontological primitive is required.

---

## Falsification

Present:

1. an explicit reasoning process,
2. a required component,
3. and demonstrate that the component cannot be represented using the current ontology.

---

# Conjecture 6 — Minimality

No architecture exists with fewer primitive assumptions while preserving equivalent expressive power over the stated scope.

---

## Motivation

This is the central minimality claim of Project FAR.

---

## Falsification

Produce a strictly simpler architecture satisfying:

- identical scope,
- equivalent expressive power,
- and fewer primitive assumptions.

---

# Conjecture 7 — External Equivalence

Every major foundational reasoning framework can either:

- be embedded within FARA,

or

- identify the precise point where FARA fails.

---

## Motivation

The objective is comparison rather than replacement.

---

## Current Comparison Targets

- Classical Logic
- Proof Theory
- Type Theory
- Category Theory
- Model-Based Reasoning
- Bayesian Reasoning
- Scientific Methodology

Additional frameworks may be added as research progresses.

---

# Research Policy

A conjecture should never be treated as an established theorem.

Progress occurs equally through:

- successful proofs,
- successful reductions,
- and successful counterexamples.

Each contributes to a more accurate understanding of the architecture of reasoning.

---

# Current Status

None of the conjectures contained in this document should presently be regarded as proven.

They represent the current frontier of Project FAR research.
