# Conjectures

## Purpose

This document records conjectures proposed within Project FAR.

A conjecture is a formal statement believed to be true but not yet established by proof.

Conjectures may eventually become propositions, lemmas, or theorems following formal proof.

---

# Conjecture 1 — Universal Architecture

There exists a universal architecture capable of representing every structured, explicit, and auditable reasoning process within the scope of Project FAR.

Status:

Open.

---

# Conjecture 2 — Minimal Architecture

The universal architecture, if it exists, admits a minimal collection of primitive concepts.

Status:

Open.

---

# Conjecture 3 — Primitive Independence

The current candidate primitive concepts are mutually independent.

Status:

Open.

---

# Conjecture 4 — Primitive Minimality

No current candidate primitive concept can be removed without reducing the expressive power of the architecture.

Status:

Open.

---

# Conjecture 5 — Architectural Completeness

The current architecture is sufficient to represent every reasoning process within the stated scope of Project FAR.

Status:

Open.
