# Comparison

## Purpose

This document defines comparison within the Foundational Analysis of Reasoning Operations (FARO).

Comparison identifies similarities and differences between reasoning processes represented within FARA.

It does not evaluate correctness or admissibility.

---

## Objective

The objective of comparison is to identify structural relationships between reasoning processes.

Comparison may be performed between complete investigations, reasoning states, transitions, or other architectural components.

---

## Comparison Criteria

Reasoning processes may be compared with respect to:

- Structure
- Interpretation
- Reasoning Calculus
- Reasoning States
- Transition Signatures
- Admissibility Structures
- Resolution

Different investigations may require different comparison criteria.

---

## Result

Comparison produces an explicit description of the similarities and differences between the reasoning processes under examination.

It does not determine which process is preferable.

That determination belongs to subsequent analysis.
