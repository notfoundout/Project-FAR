# Validation — Mathematical Proof

## Purpose

This document evaluates mathematical proof using the validation methodology defined by Project FAR.

The objective is to determine whether mathematical proofs can be represented using the Foundational Architecture of Reasoning Analysis (FARA), investigated using the Foundational Analysis of Reasoning (FAR), and analyzed using the Foundational Analysis of Reasoning Operations (FARO).

---

# 1. Framework

## Name

Mathematical Proof

---

## Purpose

To establish the truth of mathematical statements through deductive reasoning from explicitly accepted premises.

---

## Domain

Mathematics and formal logic.

---

# 2. Structural Analysis

## Primitive Concepts

Examples include:

- Axiom
- Definition
- Inference Rule
- Statement

The precise primitive concepts depend upon the formal system being employed.

---

## Derived Concepts

Examples include:

- Proposition
- Lemma
- Theorem
- Corollary
- Proof

---

## Architectural Structure

Mathematical proofs organize reasoning as a sequence of explicitly justified deductive steps leading from premises to conclusions.

---

# 3. Reasoning Analysis

## Investigation

An investigation seeks to determine whether a mathematical statement can be formally established.

---

## Representations

Definitions, axioms, propositions, lemmas, theorems, proof steps, and symbolic expressions.

---

## Interpretation

Meaning is assigned through the formal semantics of the mathematical system.

---

## Reasoning Calculus

The reasoning calculus consists of the inference rules accepted by the formal system.

---

## Reasoning States

Each stage of a proof may be represented as a reasoning state.

---

## Transformations

Reasoning progresses through the application of valid inference rules.

---

## Candidates

Possible proof steps, intermediate results, or complete proofs admitted for consideration.

---

## Admissibility

Candidates are admissible when they satisfy the inference rules and assumptions of the formal system.

---

## Resolution

The investigation concludes when the target statement has been established or shown not to follow from the accepted premises.

---

# 4. Mapping to FARA

Every major component of mathematical proof appears directly representable within FARA.

Proof steps naturally correspond to reasoning states connected by transition signatures.

No significant architectural incompatibilities have been identified.

---

# 5. Application of FAR

The FAR methodology can explicitly represent the complete development of a mathematical proof from its initial assumptions through its final result.

---

# 6. Application of FARO

Mathematical proofs can be:

- compared,
- audited,
- and subjected to disagreement analysis

using the methods defined by FARO.

---

# 7. Evaluation

## Strengths

- Completely explicit reasoning.
- Well-defined inference rules.
- Highly reproducible.
- Naturally auditable.

---

## Limitations

Different formal systems may employ different inference rules.

Large proofs may require hierarchical representation to remain manageable.

---

## Open Questions

- What constitutes the minimal reasoning state for a proof?
- Can proof strategies be represented independently of proof steps?
- How should automated theorem proving be represented?

---

# 8. Validation Summary

## Architecture Compatibility

**Full**

All major architectural components appear directly representable.

---

## Methodology Compatibility

**Full**

The FAR methodology closely mirrors the development of mathematical proofs.

---

## Operational Compatibility

**Full**

Comparison, auditing, and disagreement analysis are naturally applicable.

---

## Overall Assessment

Mathematical proof appears fully compatible with the current architecture, methodology, and operational framework of Project FAR.

---

## Recommended Research

- Representation of automated theorem proving.
- Representation of proof strategies.
- Comparison of alternative proofs of the same theorem.

---

# 9. Conclusion

Mathematical proof appears fully representable within the current scope of Project FAR.

Its formal structure makes it one of the strongest validation domains for evaluating the completeness and rigor of the framework.
