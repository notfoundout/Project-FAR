# Validation — Software Engineering

## Purpose

This document evaluates software engineering using the validation methodology defined by Project FAR.

The objective is to determine whether software engineering reasoning can be represented using the Foundational Architecture of Reasoning Analysis (FARA), investigated using the Foundational Analysis of Reasoning (FAR), and analyzed using the Foundational Analysis of Reasoning Operations (FARO).

---

# 1. Framework

## Name

Software Engineering

---

## Purpose

To design, develop, verify, deploy, and maintain software systems through structured engineering practices.

---

## Domain

Software development, systems engineering, and computer science.

---

# 2. Structural Analysis

## Primitive Concepts

Examples include:

- Requirement
- Specification
- Design
- Implementation
- Test

The precise primitive concepts depend upon the development methodology employed.

---

## Derived Concepts

Examples include:

- Architecture
- Module
- Interface
- Bug
- Refactoring
- Release

---

## Architectural Structure

Software engineering organizes reasoning through iterative development, implementation, verification, testing, deployment, and maintenance.

---

# 3. Reasoning Analysis

## Investigation

An investigation seeks to solve a software engineering problem or satisfy one or more requirements.

---

## Representations

Requirements, specifications, diagrams, source code, tests, documentation, bug reports, and design artifacts.

---

## Interpretation

Meaning is assigned through programming languages, specifications, engineering standards, and project context.

---

## Reasoning Calculus

The reasoning calculus consists of logical reasoning, engineering principles, programming language semantics, verification techniques, testing methodologies, and design constraints.

---

## Reasoning States

Each stage of software development may be represented as a reasoning state.

---

## Transformations

Reasoning progresses through design, implementation, testing, debugging, refactoring, review, and deployment.

---

## Candidates

Possible designs, implementations, fixes, optimizations, or architectural decisions admitted for consideration.

---

## Admissibility

Candidates are evaluated according to requirements, correctness, testing results, engineering constraints, and the applicable reasoning calculus.

---

## Resolution

The investigation concludes by selecting the implementation or design satisfying the established requirements according to the applicable resolution rule.

The resulting resolution remains subject to revision as requirements or constraints change.

---

# 4. Mapping to FARA

Software engineering appears representable within FARA.

Requirements, designs, implementations, tests, and engineering decisions correspond naturally to representations and reasoning states.

No significant architectural incompatibilities have been identified.

---

# 5. Application of FAR

The FAR methodology can explicitly represent software engineering investigations from requirements analysis through implementation, testing, deployment, and maintenance.

---

# 6. Application of FARO

Software engineering reasoning processes can be:

- compared,
- audited,
- and subjected to disagreement analysis

using the methods defined by FARO.

These operations naturally support activities such as design reviews, code reviews, architectural comparisons, and postmortem analyses.

---

# 7. Evaluation

## Strengths

- Explicit engineering artifacts.
- Highly iterative reasoning.
- Well-documented development process.
- Naturally auditable.

---

## Limitations

Development methodologies vary significantly.

Practical engineering decisions may incorporate organizational or economic considerations beyond technical reasoning.

---

## Open Questions

- How should collaborative reasoning be represented?
- How should concurrent development be modeled?
- How should evolving requirements affect reasoning states?

---

# 8. Validation Summary

## Architecture Compatibility

**Full**

The architectural components of software engineering appear representable within FARA.

---

## Methodology Compatibility

**Full**

The FAR methodology naturally represents iterative engineering investigations.

---

## Operational Compatibility

**Full**

Comparison, auditing, and disagreement analysis are directly applicable.

---

## Overall Assessment

Software engineering appears fully compatible with the current architecture, methodology, and operational framework of Project FAR.

---

- Representation of collaborative reasoning.
- Representation of version control and branching.
- Validation across Agile, Waterfall, and other development methodologies.

---

# 9. Conclusion

Software engineering appears fully representable within the current scope of Project FAR.

Its iterative and artifact-driven nature provides another strong validation of the generality of the framework across applied engineering domains.
