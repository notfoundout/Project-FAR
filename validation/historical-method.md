# Validation — Historical Method

## Purpose

This document evaluates the Historical Method using the validation methodology defined by Project FAR.

The objective is to determine whether the Historical Method can be represented using the Foundational Architecture of Reasoning Analysis (FARA), investigated using the Foundational Analysis of Reasoning (FAR), and analyzed using the Foundational Analysis of Reasoning Operations (FARO).

---

# 1. Framework

## Name

Historical Method

---

## Purpose

To investigate and explain past events through the critical analysis of historical evidence.

---

## Domain

History, archaeology, historiography, and related disciplines.

---

# 2. Structural Analysis

## Primitive Concepts

Examples include:

- Source
- Evidence
- Event
- Interpretation

The precise primitive concepts depend upon the historical methodology employed.

---

## Derived Concepts

Examples include:

- Historical Narrative
- Corroboration
- Source Criticism
- Historical Explanation

---

## Architectural Structure

The Historical Method organizes reasoning through the collection, evaluation, interpretation, and synthesis of historical evidence.

---

# 3. Reasoning Analysis

## Investigation

An investigation seeks to answer a question concerning past events.

---

## Representations

Historical documents, archaeological findings, testimonies, artifacts, chronologies, and historical claims.

---

## Interpretation

Meaning is assigned through linguistic, cultural, chronological, and contextual interpretation.

---

## Reasoning Calculus

The reasoning calculus consists of historical criticism, source evaluation, logical inference, and evidential reasoning.

The precise calculus varies among historical methodologies.

---

## Reasoning States

Each stage of evidence collection, evaluation, and interpretation may be represented as a reasoning state.

---

## Transformations

Reasoning progresses through evidence acquisition, source evaluation, reinterpretation, synthesis, and revision.

---

## Candidates

Possible historical explanations admitted for consideration.

---

## Admissibility

Candidates are evaluated according to the available evidence, source reliability, internal consistency, and the applicable historical reasoning calculus.

---

## Resolution

The investigation concludes by selecting the historical explanation supported by the available evidence according to the applicable resolution rule.

The resulting resolution remains open to revision as new evidence becomes available.

---

# 4. Mapping to FARA

Historical reasoning appears representable within FARA.

Historical evidence, interpretations, competing explanations, and reasoning processes correspond naturally to architectural components.

Some historical judgments remain probabilistic rather than deductive, but this does not require additional architectural concepts.

---

# 5. Application of FAR

The FAR methodology can explicitly represent historical investigations, including evidence collection, source evaluation, interpretation, and final resolution.

---

# 6. Application of FARO

Historical reasoning processes can be:

- compared,
- audited,
- and subjected to disagreement analysis

using the methods defined by FARO.

---

# 7. Evaluation

## Strengths

- Naturally structured investigations.
- Explicit evidential reasoning.
- Supports competing interpretations.
- Well suited for disagreement analysis.

---

## Limitations

Interpretation often depends upon incomplete evidence.

Different historical methodologies may employ different standards for evaluating evidence.

---

## Open Questions

- How should evidential uncertainty be represented?
- How should conflicting primary sources be modeled?
- Can historiographical methodologies be represented uniformly?

---

# 8. Validation Summary

## Architecture Compatibility

**Full**

The major components of historical reasoning appear representable within FARA.

---

## Methodology Compatibility

**Full**

The FAR methodology naturally accommodates historical investigations.

---

## Operational Compatibility

**Full**

Comparison, auditing, and disagreement analysis are directly applicable.

---

## Overall Assessment

The Historical Method appears fully compatible with the current architecture, methodology, and operational framework of Project FAR.

---

## Recommended Research

- Representation of evidential confidence.
- Validation across different schools of historiography.
- Representation of competing historical narratives.

---

# 9. Conclusion

The Historical Method appears fully representable within the current scope of Project FAR.

Its combination of structured investigation, interpretation, and evidential reasoning provides an important validation of Project FAR beyond deductive and probabilistic reasoning.
