# Validation — Debate

## Purpose

This document evaluates the applicability of Project FAR to structured debate.

The objective is to determine whether the architectural components of FARA, the methodology of FAR, and the operations of FARO adequately represent and compare competing reasoning processes within a debate.

---

# Framework

A debate is a structured exchange in which two or more participants present competing arguments concerning a specified question or claim.

Each participant constructs a reasoning process intended to support a preferred conclusion while criticizing competing conclusions.

---

# FARA Analysis

## Investigation

The investigation specifies the question, proposition, or resolution under debate.

---

## Representations

Representations include:

- Claims
- Premises
- Evidence
- Definitions
- Objections
- Rebuttals
- Conclusions

---

## Representational Structure

Representations are organized according to their inferential and argumentative relationships.

Each participant constructs an independent representational structure.

---

## Interpretation

Interpretation assigns meaning to the representations employed by each participant.

Differences in interpretation frequently produce divergent reasoning.

---

# FAR Analysis

## Reasoning Calculus

Each participant applies a reasoning calculus governing how conclusions are derived and evaluated.

Different participants may employ different reasoning calculi.

---

## Reasoning States

Each reasoning state represents the current state of a participant's argument.

---

## Transition Signatures

Transition signatures record argumentative moves such as:

- Presenting evidence
- Challenging a premise
- Introducing an objection
- Responding to criticism
- Revising a conclusion

---

## Candidates

Candidates consist of the competing conclusions advanced during the debate.

---

## Admissibility Structure (Ω)

Ω classifies candidate conclusions according to each participant's reasoning process.

Different participants may construct different admissibility structures.

---

## Resolution Rule

Each participant applies a resolution rule to determine which conclusion should ultimately be accepted.

---

## Resolution

The resulting resolution is the conclusion supported by the participant's reasoning process.

---

# FARO Analysis

FARO compares competing debates by identifying:

- Shared representations
- Differing definitions
- Differing interpretations
- Differing reasoning calculi
- Differing admissibility structures
- Differing resolution rules
- The earliest point of divergence

This permits explicit analysis of why participants disagree.

---

# Evaluation

Project FAR successfully represents both individual reasoning processes and the relationships between competing reasoning processes.

FARO provides an explicit framework for locating, comparing, and explaining disagreement.

---

# Conclusion

Structured debate is naturally representable within Project FAR.

The validation demonstrates one of FARO's primary capabilities: systematic disagreement analysis.
