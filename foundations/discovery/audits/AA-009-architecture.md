# AA-009 — Architecture Artifact Audit

## Status

Active Artifact Audit

## Target

`frameworks/FARA/architecture.md`

## Overall Assessment

The architecture document is one of the strongest FARA artifacts audited to date.

## Principal Findings

1. It clearly distinguishes the architecture from the research program by stating that the existence of a minimal architecture remains an open research question.

2. It distinguishes design principles from architectural components, reducing category collapse.

3. It correctly identifies the primitive concepts as candidate primitives rather than established primitives.

4. It separates representation from prescription by stating that FARA represents reasoning but does not prescribe how reasoning should be performed.

5. It explicitly states that completeness, minimality, and universality have not yet been established.

## Remaining Issues

- The scope of "structured reasoning" should eventually be defined more precisely.
- Architectural claims should eventually link to supporting Knowledge Layer objects.
- Candidate primitives should be traced to their grounding investigations.

## Outcome

Status: Strong.

No substantial revision is recommended before auditing `primitives.md` and the canonical definitions.
