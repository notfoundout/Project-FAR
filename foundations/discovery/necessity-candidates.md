# Necessity Candidate Register

## Status

Research

## Purpose

This register records candidate necessities discovered during first-principles investigation.

No candidate listed here is accepted as canonical merely by inclusion.

Each candidate must survive the Foundational Discovery Protocol before promotion.

## Promotion Standard

A candidate may be promoted only after completing applicable tests:

- definition test;
- category test;
- scope test;
- removal test;
- reduction test;
- independence test;
- replacement test;
- minimal-model test;
- alternative-model test;
- counterexample test;
- proof audit;
- failure characterization.

## Candidate N1 — Detectable Difference

### Status

Candidate / Under Investigation

### Informal Statement

Explicit reasoning may require at least one detectable difference.

### Current Rationale

If no difference is detectable, then no content can be distinguished from any other content. Without distinguishable content, there appears to be no operand for comparison, selection, contradiction, revision, or inference.

### Current Limit

This has not been proven.

It remains possible that detectable difference is derivable from a weaker condition or that the candidate is improperly formulated.

### Required Work

- define detectable;
- define difference;
- define explicit reasoning without using the candidate circularly;
- test whether reasoning about a single apparent object reintroduces difference;
- test whether internal reasoning requires distinguishable internal states;
- search for weaker formulations.

## Candidate Cluster N2 — Persistence / Availability / Recoverability

### Status

Unresolved Candidate Cluster

### Informal Problem

Explicit reasoning appears to require that some content remain usable beyond an isolated instant.

However, it is not yet clear whether the relevant condition is persistence, availability, accessibility, memory, recoverability, reconstructibility, or another weaker condition.

### Current Rationale

Reasoning chains appear to require more than isolated, disconnected moments.

Comparison, revision, dependency, and audit appear to require access to content across operations.

### Current Limit

No member of this cluster is accepted as primitive or necessary.

Persistence may be only one mechanism by which the deeper condition is satisfied.

Availability may be too broad or may itself be derivable.

Recoverability may be a better candidate, but it has not been tested.

### Required Work

- distinguish persistence from availability;
- distinguish availability from recoverability;
- test deterministic reconstruction as a possible substitute for storage;
- test memoryless systems;
- test systems with external records;
- identify the weakest formulation that survives reduction.

## Candidate N3 — Comparison

### Status

Candidate / Not Tested

### Informal Statement

Explicit reasoning may require comparison between distinguishable contents, states, or relations.

### Current Limit

Not tested.

It may be reducible to detectable difference plus an operation over difference.

## Candidate N4 — Selection / Discrimination

### Status

Candidate / Not Tested

### Informal Statement

Explicit reasoning may require the ability to select, privilege, or discriminate some content for further processing.

### Current Limit

Not tested.

It may be an operation, not a primitive condition.

It may be derivable from directedness or objective structure.

## Candidate N5 — Directedness

### Status

Candidate / Not Tested

### Informal Statement

Reasoning investigation may require directed activity toward resolving, evaluating, transforming, or determining something.

### Current Limit

This may apply to investigations rather than reasoning in general.

It must not be smuggled into the definition of reasoning without proof.

## Current Result

No necessity has been accepted.

No primitive has been accepted.

The current research state is candidate discovery and reduction.
