# H-001 — Category Collapse Is an Independent Methodological Failure Mode

## Status

Active Hypothesis

## Origin

- MI-001 — Category Collapse as a Methodological Failure Mode
- MI-001A — Category Collapse Validation

## Hypothesis

Category collapse is an independent methodological failure mode that cannot be completely derived from existing principles such as Explicitness or Orthogonality.

## Motivation

Grounding investigations repeatedly exposed distinct conceptual roles being conflated. The hypothesis is that explicitly testing for these conflations improves repository quality beyond existing methodology.

## Current Supporting Evidence

- GP-001 through GP-012
- MI-001
- MI-001A
- AA-003

## Falsification Criteria

The hypothesis fails if:

- all observed category collapses are adequately explained by existing methodological principles;
- explicit category-collapse analysis does not improve audit outcomes;
- the concept cannot be operationalized consistently.

## Success Criteria

The hypothesis is supported if:

- independent artifact families repeatedly expose category collapse;
- explicit testing improves audits and revisions;
- the principle cannot be reduced to existing methodology.

## Dependencies

- MI-001
- MI-001A
- Additional Artifact Audits

## Next Actions

- Complete additional AA audits.
- Compare category collapse directly against Explicitness and Orthogonality.
- Decide whether to promote, revise, or reject the hypothesis.
