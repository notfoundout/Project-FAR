# C-002 — Rule Is Distinct from Execution

## Status

Pilot Claim

## Claim

A rule governing a transformation, transition, or reasoning process is distinct from the execution or occurrence of that transformation, transition, or process.

## Current Support Level

Supported by GP-006 and downstream testing.

Not yet canonical.

## Origin

- GP-006 — Transformation Grounding Investigation

## Supporting Investigations

- GP-006 separated transformation rule, transformation instance, transformation execution, and transformation representation.
- GP-006A tested this split against transition signature, reasoning state, reasoning calculus, and FARA transition architecture.

## Supporting Audits

- Pending additional framework audits.

## Counterexamples Considered

- a rule that specifies permitted transitions without any transition occurring;
- a process that occurs without an explicit rule;
- a mapping that exists without execution.

## Open Challenges

- Determine whether rule-governed transformations are a subclass of transformations.
- Determine whether transformation is reducible to relation plus ordered configurations.
