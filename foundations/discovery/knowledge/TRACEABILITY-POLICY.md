# Knowledge Traceability Policy

## Status

Pilot Repository Policy

## Purpose

Define the minimum traceability requirements for knowledge objects within Project FAR.

## Policy

Every knowledge object should be traceable to its evidential basis.

A knowledge object should identify, where applicable:

- origin investigation;
- supporting evidence;
- related claims;
- related hypotheses;
- related open questions;
- dependencies;
- revision history;
- current status.

## Promotion Path

Knowledge should normally progress through the following lifecycle:

Question → Hypothesis → Investigation → Evidence → Claim → Validation → Theorem

Progression is not mandatory. Objects may remain unresolved or be rejected.

## Rejection

Rejected hypotheses and unsuccessful investigations should remain traceable unless there is a compelling repository-maintenance reason to remove them.

## Objective

The goal is not to maximize the number of claims, but to maximize the transparency of why any claim exists and what evidence supports it.

## Pilot Scope

This policy applies only to the pilot Knowledge Layer until validated by future audits and methodology investigations.
