# Root Theory Methodology

## Principle of Concept Emergence

Derived concepts shall not be introduced merely because they appear useful.

A derived concept may be introduced only if:

1. Multiple previously established theorems exhibit a common structural pattern.
2. The proposed concept compresses those theorems into a simpler formulation.
3. The concept introduces no additional assumptions.

Accordingly, concepts emerge from theory rather than preceding it.

---

## Principle of Theorem Priority

Whenever a proposed derived concept can instead be expressed as a theorem of existing concepts, the theorem shall be preferred.

Derived concepts should be introduced only when they reduce structural complexity without increasing assumptions.

---

## Primitive Candidate Evaluation

Every candidate primitive shall be evaluated according to:

1. Irreducibility
2. Independence
3. Necessity
4. Non-Circularity
5. Generative Adequacy

No candidate may be accepted until it has been evaluated against every criterion.
