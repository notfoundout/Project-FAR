# Root Investigation — Primitive Foundation of Project FAR

## Status

In Progress

---

## Research Question

What is the most fundamental concept upon which every component of Project FAR depends?

---

## Motivation

Project FAR consists of multiple foundational theories organized into a logical hierarchy.

The hierarchy cannot be justified until its root has been identified.

Accordingly, the primitive foundation of Project FAR requires independent investigation.

---

## Current Hypothesis

Project FAR possesses a unique primitive foundation.

Every other foundational concept is directly or indirectly derivable from this foundation.

The identity of the primitive foundation remains unknown.

---

## Candidate Primitive Foundations

Current candidates include:

- Identity
- Distinction
- Structure
- Relation
- Representation
- Information
- Object
- Existence

No candidate has yet been established.

---

## Investigation Method

For each candidate:

1. Assume the candidate is primitive.
2. Attempt to derive every remaining candidate.
3. Attempt to derive the candidate from another.
4. Search for circular dependencies.
5. Search for counterexamples.

---

## Evaluation Criteria

The primitive foundation should satisfy all of the following.

- Irreducibility
- Universality
- Independence
- Foundationality
- Non-circularity

---

## Counterexample Strategy

Attempt to identify:

- two independent primitive foundations;
- a concept more fundamental than the current candidate;
- circular derivations;
- concepts that cannot be derived.

Any successful counterexample requires revision of the hierarchy.

---

## Hidden Assumptions

Current investigations assume:

- a unique primitive foundation exists;
- the dependency hierarchy terminates;
- foundational concepts admit objective comparison.

Each assumption requires independent investigation.

---

## Current Assessment

The primitive foundation of Project FAR has not yet been identified.

---

## Dependency Analysis

Depends upon:

None.

Supports:

Every subsequent component of Project FAR.

# Investigation 1.1 — Existence

## Candidate Claim

Existence is the primitive foundation of Project FAR.

---

## Motivation

Existence appears to underlie every object and concept.

If nothing exists, no further concepts can be constructed.

---

## Current Hypothesis

Existence is necessary but may not be sufficient as the primitive foundation.

---

## Reduction Attempt

Can every other candidate be derived from existence alone?

Current observations:

- Existence does not distinguish one object from another.
- Existence does not establish identity.
- Existence does not establish relations.
- Existence does not establish structure.
- Existence does not establish representation.

Accordingly, existence alone does not presently appear sufficient.

---

## Counterexample Strategy

Attempt to construct a universe consisting solely of existence.

Question:

Does anything beyond "something exists" necessarily follow?

---

## Current Assessment

Existence appears universal.

Its sufficiency as the primitive foundation remains doubtful.

# Investigation 1.2.1 — Derivation of Identity

## Research Question

Can identity be derived from distinction?

---

## Motivation

Identity appears throughout reasoning.

If distinction is the primitive foundation of Project FAR, then identity should arise from distinction rather than being independently primitive.

Accordingly, the derivability of identity requires investigation.

---

## Current Hypothesis

Identity is derivable from distinction.

This hypothesis remains provisional.

---

## Candidate Derivation

Suppose no distinctions exist.

Then no object can be differentiated from anything else.

Without differentiation,

there is no criterion for referring to one object rather than another.

Accordingly,

identity appears impossible.

Suppose distinctions exist.

Objects become distinguishable.

A distinguishable object can be consistently referred to.

Such referability is a candidate basis for identity.

---

## Counterexample Strategy

Attempt to construct:

- distinction without identity;
- identity without distinction.

If either construction succeeds,

the proposed derivation fails.

---

## Hidden Assumptions

Current investigations assume:

- identity requires distinguishability;
- distinguishability permits stable reference.

Each assumption requires independent investigation.

---

## Current Assessment

The derivation remains unresolved.

## Phase 8 — Consequences of Distinction

### Research Question

What necessarily follows from the existence of distinction?

---

### Theorem D3 — Distinction Is Informative

#### Statement

A distinction necessarily excludes at least one possible state.

---

#### Motivation

A distinction appears to reduce uncertainty by ruling out the possibility that no distinction exists.

This investigation determines whether such exclusion is a necessary consequence of distinction.

---

#### Candidate Proof

Suppose distinction exists.

Suppose further that every possible state remains possible.

Then the state of complete indistinguishability remains possible.

However,

complete indistinguishability is incompatible with the existence of distinction.

Therefore,

at least one possible state has been excluded.

Accordingly,

every distinction necessarily excludes at least one possibility.

---

#### Current Assessment

Provisionally Accepted.

Further investigation should determine whether stronger consequences follow.
