# Investigation I1-INVESTIGATION — Discovery of Investigation

Previous Identifier: I1

## Status

In Progress

---

# Purpose

Discover the minimum conditions under which an investigation can legitimately begin.

This investigation assumes nothing about:

- dependency;
- admissibility;
- discoverability;
- architecture;
- methodology beyond the currently accepted results.

---

# Research Question

What is the minimum condition required for an investigation to begin?

---

# Objective

Discover the weakest condition that distinguishes a legitimate investigation from an illegitimate one.

---

# Discovery Procedure

1. Attempt to begin an investigation without introducing any prerequisite conditions.

2. If the investigation can proceed objectively, reject the necessity of additional conditions.

3. If the investigation cannot proceed, identify the precise point of failure.

4. Attempt to resolve the failure while introducing the weakest possible additional condition.

5. Repeat until no weaker condition resolves the failure.

---

# Acceptance Criterion

This investigation succeeds when the weakest condition necessary for initiating an investigation has been discovered.

---

# Rejection Criterion

Reject every stronger condition shown to be unnecessary.

---

# Failure Criterion

This investigation remains incomplete if:

- multiple equally weak conditions survive;
- no condition distinguishes legitimate investigations from illegitimate investigations;
- additional undefined concepts are introduced.

---

# Current Status

In Progress.

---

# Notes

The objective is not to define investigation.

The objective is to discover the minimum condition that permits one to begin.
