# Project Scope

## Purpose

This document defines the intended scope of Project FAR.

It specifies the classes of reasoning processes the framework is intended to represent and identifies the boundaries beyond which no claims are currently made.

---

## Primary Scope

Project FAR investigates the representation, analysis, comparison, and formalization of structured, explicit, and auditable reasoning.

The framework seeks to determine whether such reasoning admits a common minimal architecture independent of any particular reasoning calculus or application domain.

---

## Intended Capabilities

Within its stated scope, Project FAR seeks to:

- represent structured reasoning,
- analyze reasoning processes,
- compare reasoning processes,
- audit reasoning processes,
- identify sources of disagreement,
- develop a formal mathematical theory of reasoning representation.

---

## Independence

Project FAR is intended to remain independent of:

- any particular reasoning calculus,
- any application domain,
- any implementation,
- any programming language,
- any software platform.

---

## Current Limitations

Project FAR does not currently claim to:

- represent every possible form of reasoning,
- determine objective truth,
- determine which reasoning calculus is universally correct,
- prescribe conclusions,
- replace domain-specific expertise.

---

## Future Expansion

The scope of Project FAR may expand as additional theoretical results are established.

Any expansion should preserve the principles of explicitness, minimality, generality, auditability, and formal rigor.

---

## Research Status

The precise scope of Project FAR remains an active subject of investigation.

The current scope reflects the present state of the framework rather than a final or complete specification.
