# Philosophy

## Purpose

This document defines the philosophical principles that guide the development of Project FAR.

These principles govern the design of the framework but are not themselves components of the architecture or formal theory.

---

## Foundational Principle

Project FAR is developed by seeking the simplest architecture capable of representing structured, explicit, and auditable reasoning within its stated scope.

Simplicity is pursued without sacrificing expressive power.

---

## Definitions Before Conclusions

Project FAR gives priority to precise definitions before analysis, classification, or proof.

Every formal conclusion depends upon explicitly stated definitions.

---

## Explicit Representation

Reasoning should be represented explicitly whenever possible.

Representations, interpretations, transformations, and reasoning processes should be sufficiently explicit to permit independent examination.

---

## Minimality

The framework seeks the smallest collection of primitive concepts capable of supporting its intended expressive power.

Concepts that can be derived from simpler concepts should not remain primitive.

---

## Generality

The framework is intended to remain independent of any particular reasoning calculus or application domain.

Architectural components should be reusable across diverse forms of structured reasoning.

---

## Auditability

Reasoning should be represented in a manner that permits independent reconstruction and examination.

The reasoning process should remain accessible rather than relying upon implicit assumptions.

---

## Reproducibility

Equivalent investigators applying the same methodology to equivalent reasoning processes should be capable of reproducing equivalent results.

---

## Formal Development

Project FAR progresses through successive stages of formalization.

Definitions precede axioms.

Axioms precede propositions.

Propositions support lemmas.

Lemmas support theorems.

Unproven statements remain conjectures until formally established.

---

## Continuous Refinement

Every component of Project FAR remains subject to refinement.

Definitions, primitive concepts, architectural components, methodologies, and theoretical results may be revised whenever doing so improves clarity, consistency, simplicity, or expressive power.
