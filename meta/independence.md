# Proof System

## Purpose

This document defines the standards by which theoretical claims within Project FAR are established.

It specifies what constitutes a valid proof, the admissible methods of proof, and the evidentiary standards required for acceptance.

All propositions and theorems appearing elsewhere in Project FAR are evaluated relative to this proof system.

---

# Objective

The objective of the Project FAR proof system is to establish theoretical claims through explicit, reproducible, and logically valid reasoning.

No claim is accepted solely because it appears plausible, intuitive, or widely accepted.

Every accepted result must be justified according to the standards defined herein.

---

# Components of a Proof

Every formal proof should identify:

- the statement to be established;
- the assumptions upon which the proof depends;
- the definitions employed;
- the logical steps of the argument;
- the conclusion.

Hidden assumptions should be identified whenever possible.

---

# Standards of Proof

Project FAR recognizes the following categories of proof.

## Constructive Proof

A statement is established by explicitly constructing the required object or demonstrating the required procedure.

---

## Deductive Proof

A statement is established through a finite sequence of logically valid inferences from accepted assumptions.

---

## Proof by Contradiction

A statement is established by demonstrating that its negation produces a contradiction.

---

## Proof by Counterexample

A universal statement is refuted by exhibiting a single counterexample.

---

## Independence Proof

A statement asserting non-derivability is established by demonstrating that the target concept cannot be uniquely determined from the remaining assumptions.

The precise methodology for establishing independence remains under active investigation.

---

# Research Status

Not every proof technique has presently been formalized.

Additional proof methods may be introduced as the theoretical development of Project FAR progresses.

Accordingly, the proof system should be regarded as extensible rather than complete.

---

# Burden of Proof

The burden of proof rests upon the party asserting a theoretical claim.

Unsupported assertions do not become accepted results.

Claims remain provisional until justified according to the standards of this proof system.

---

# Relationship to Research

Research investigations may employ exploratory reasoning while developing candidate proofs.

However, only proofs satisfying the standards defined in this document may be incorporated into the canonical theory.

---

# Current Open Questions

The following questions remain under investigation.

- What formally establishes non-derivability?
- Under what conditions is model separation sufficient?
- Are additional proof techniques required for foundational primitives?
- Can every accepted proof technique be formally justified?
