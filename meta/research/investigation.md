# Investigation MT1 — Entry Conditions of the Meta-Theory

## Status

In Progress

---

## Research Question

What information must exist before the Meta-Theory can begin?

---

## Motivation

The Root Theory establishes the primitive basis of Project FAR.

However,

the precise point at which the Meta-Theory begins has not been established.

Accordingly,

the entry conditions of the Meta-Theory require independent investigation.

---

## Current Hypothesis

The Meta-Theory begins when sufficient foundational information exists to justify the construction of formal systems.

This hypothesis remains provisional.

---

## Candidate Entry Conditions

Current candidates include:

- Accepted primitive basis only.
- Primitive basis plus derived Root propositions.
- Primitive basis plus methodology.
- Primitive basis plus justification criteria.
- Other candidate entry conditions.

No candidate has yet been accepted.

---

## Investigation Strategy

Evaluate each candidate independently.

Reject every candidate insufficient to begin objective formal construction.

Accept only the minimal sufficient entry condition.

---

## Current Assessment

Under Investigation.

---

## Candidate 1 — Primitive Basis Only

### Claim

The Meta-Theory begins immediately after acceptance of the primitive basis.

### Analysis

If formal construction can proceed directly from accepted primitives,

no additional Root propositions are required.

Current evidence neither establishes nor refutes this possibility.

### Assessment

Under Investigation.

---

## Candidate 2 — Primitive Basis Plus Derived Root Propositions

### Claim

The Meta-Theory requires derived Root propositions.

### Analysis

This candidate survives only if the Root Theory demonstrates indispensable derived propositions.

No such propositions have yet been established.

### Assessment

Under Investigation.

---

## Candidate 3 — Primitive Basis Plus Methodology

### Claim

The Meta-Theory requires both the accepted primitive basis and the canonical methodology.

### Analysis

Every formal construction depends upon objective investigative procedures.

Accordingly,

the methodology appears necessary regardless of the Root Theory's contents.

Current evidence supports this characterization.

### Assessment

Provisionally Accepted.

---

## Candidate 4 — Primitive Basis Plus Justification Criteria

### Claim

The Meta-Theory additionally requires explicit justification criteria.

### Analysis

The canonical methodology already supplies justification procedures.

This candidate therefore appears reducible.

### Assessment

Provisionally Rejected.

---

## Counterexample Strategy

Attempt to construct the Meta-Theory under each candidate entry condition.

Failure of construction rejects that candidate.

---

## Dependency Analysis

Depends upon:

- Canonical Methodology
- Root Theory

Supports:

- Entire Meta-Theory
- Resolution of the Root Theory boundary.

---

## Failure Modes

The investigation fails if:

- multiple incompatible entry conditions survive;
- no candidate permits formal construction;
- objective entry conditions cannot be established.

---

## Interim Conclusion

Current evidence does not yet establish the minimum information required to begin the Meta-Theory.

Accordingly,

the boundary between the Root Theory and the Meta-Theory remains under investigation.

# Investigation MT — Foundations of the Meta-Theory

## Status

In Progress

---

## Research Question

What are the minimal formal objects and principles required for the existence of any formal system?

---

## Purpose

This investigation establishes the complete primitive architecture of the Meta-Theory.

Every formal object used anywhere in Project FAR shall either:

- be accepted as primitive;
- be derived;
- or be rejected.

No formal object shall remain unjustified.

---

# Candidate Primitive Formal Objects

Current candidates:

- Representation
- Proposition
- Symbol
- Structure
- Relation
- Rule
- Constraint
- Identity
- Equivalence
- Set
- Collection
- Function
- Mapping
- Object
- State
- Property
- Operation
- Transformation
- Context

Additional candidates may be introduced only through objective justification.

---

# Candidate Primitive Principles

Current candidates:

- Composition
- Ordering
- Containment
- Dependency
- Consistency
- Closure
- Determinacy
- Distinguishability
- Representation
- Identity Preservation

---

# Evaluation Criteria

Every candidate shall satisfy:

1. Independence
2. Necessity
3. Non-Circularity
4. Minimality
5. Reconstructability
6. Sufficiency

Failure of any criterion rejects the candidate.

---

# Comparative Evaluation

Every candidate shall answer:

1. Can it be characterized independently?

2. Does it presuppose another formal object?

3. Can every formal system exist without it?

4. Can another accepted object derive it?

5. Does removing it prevent formal construction?

6. Does every reconstructed Meta-Theory require it?

---

# Reduction Procedure

After evaluation:

- Merge equivalent objects.
- Eliminate reducible objects.
- Eliminate unnecessary objects.
- Repeat until no further reduction succeeds.

---

# Completion Criterion

The investigation terminates only when:

- every surviving primitive is independent;
- every rejected primitive has objective justification for rejection;
- no successful reduction exists;
- no missing primitive can be demonstrated;
- every higher FAR layer can be reconstructed.

---

# Deliverables

The investigation shall produce:

- Canonical primitive formal objects.
- Canonical primitive formal principles.
- Canonical dependency graph.
- Canonical derivation graph.
- Canonical Meta-Theory.
