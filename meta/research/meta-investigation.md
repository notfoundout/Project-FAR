# Meta-Theory Investigation

## Purpose

This document records the investigation of the foundational concepts required to construct formal theories.

Unlike the canonical theory documents, this document serves as a research notebook.

Its purpose is to investigate, challenge, and refine the concepts underlying the Meta-Theory before their incorporation into the canonical theory.

No result recorded herein should be regarded as established until formally incorporated into the Meta-Theory.

---

# Primary Research Program

The Meta-Theory research program proceeds according to the following stages.

## Stage 0 — Nature of Formal Theories

Determine what distinguishes a formal theory from other intellectual systems.

---

## Stage 1 — Universal Properties

Determine the properties possessed by every formal theory.

---

## Stage 2 — Primitive Architecture

Determine the minimal primitive architecture required for a formal theory.

---

## Stage 3 — Minimality

Determine whether any primitive can be eliminated.

---

## Stage 4 — Sufficiency

Determine whether the primitive architecture generates every required component of a formal theory.

---

## Stage 5 — Axiomatization

Construct the canonical Meta-Theory.

---

# Investigation 4 — Criteria for Primitive Status

## Status

In Progress

---

## Research Question

What properties must a formal object satisfy in order to qualify as primitive?

---

## Motivation

Previous investigations identified candidate primitive concepts.

However, no formal criterion presently exists for determining whether a concept is genuinely primitive.

Without such criteria, primitive status becomes a matter of judgment rather than formal analysis.

Accordingly, the concept of primitive status itself requires investigation.

---

## Current Hypothesis

Primitive status is determined by objective structural criteria rather than by arbitrary declaration.

---

## Candidate Criteria

The following properties are proposed as candidate requirements.

### Criterion 1 — Irreducibility

A primitive cannot be completely defined in terms of more fundamental objects within the same theory.

---

### Criterion 2 — Necessity

Removing the primitive prevents construction of the intended theory.

---

### Criterion 3 — Independence

The primitive is not derivable from the remaining primitives.

---

### Criterion 4 — Foundationality

The primitive serves as a dependency for additional concepts.

No primitive should depend upon concepts derived from itself.

---

### Criterion 5 — Explicitness

Primitive status must be explicitly declared within the theory.

Hidden primitives are prohibited.

---

## Counterexample Strategy

Attempt to identify a concept satisfying some criteria but not others.

Determine whether every criterion is necessary.

Determine whether the proposed collection is sufficient.

---

## Hidden Assumptions

Current investigations assume that primitive status admits an objective characterization.

This assumption itself requires justification.

---

## Current Assessment

No definitive criteria have yet been established.

Current candidates provide a working framework for further investigation.

# Investigation 5 — Relativity of Primitive Status

## Status

In Progress

---

## Research Question

Is primitive status absolute or relative?

---

## Motivation

Current investigations suggest that primitive concepts serve as the foundations of formal theories.

However, it remains unclear whether primitiveness is an intrinsic property of a concept or a property relative to a particular formal theory.

Resolving this question is essential before establishing criteria for primitive architecture.

---

## Current Hypothesis

Primitive status is relative to a formal theory.

A concept may be primitive within one theory while remaining derivable within another.

---

## Candidate Example

Suppose Theory A accepts:

- Representation

as primitive.

Suppose Theory B instead derives Representation from more fundamental concepts.

Representation is therefore primitive in Theory A but not in Theory B.

Accordingly, primitive status appears theory-relative rather than absolute.

---

## Necessary Conditions

If primitive status is relative,

then every claim of primitiveness must specify the formal theory within which it is asserted.

---

## Counterexample Strategy

Attempt to identify a concept that is primitive in every conceivable formal theory.

Such a concept would support the existence of absolute primitives.

---

## Hidden Assumptions

Current investigations assume that different formal theories may adopt different primitive architectures.

This assumption requires independent investigation.

---

## Current Assessment

Current evidence favors the relativity of primitive status.

No convincing evidence presently supports absolute primitive status.

This conclusion remains provisional.

---

## Consequences

If primitive status is relative,

then primitive investigations must always be theory-specific.

Accordingly,

the primitive architectures of FAR, FARA, FARO, and the Meta-Theory should be investigated independently.

---

## Dependency Analysis

Depends upon:

- Investigation 3 — Primitive Architecture.
- Investigation 4 — Criteria for Primitive Status.

Supports:

- Primitive investigations throughout Project FAR.
- Meta-Theory axiomatization.

---

## Next Investigation
